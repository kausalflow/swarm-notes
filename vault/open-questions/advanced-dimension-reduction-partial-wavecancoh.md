title: Advanced dimension-reduction for Partial WaveCanCoh
---

**Background:** High-dimensional confounding processes often cause instability in statistical estimation, making direct adjustment computationally challenging and prone to errors.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigating richer dimension-reduction strategies such as dynamic factor models or specialized wavelet-domain reduction approaches is necessary to improve the stability and performance of partial association measures in high-dimensional nonstationary settings.

**Why It Matters:** Robust handling of high-dimensional confounding is critical for applying this framework to complex multivariate datasets where confounding effects are pervasive.

**Evidence:** An important next step is to integrate Partial WaveCanCoh with richer dimension-reduction strategies for dependent and nonstationary data, including dynamic factor models, dynamic principal components, and wavelet-domain reduction approaches.
