title: Topology Transfer of Graph Surrogates
---

**Background:** Leveraging pre-trained models across different physical configurations requires transferring learned dynamics and structural priors to new layouts without extensive data collection. Graph-structured models offer a unique potential for this transfer if their latent representations are geometry-invariant.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate the capability of graph-based surrogate models to perform topology transfer, where pre-trained spatial priors are adapted to new system layouts or facility configurations to reduce the need for extensive experimental training data.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing topology transfer capability is essential for scaling digital twins across different industrial designs and reducing the dependency on expensive, facility-specific experimental campaigns.

**Evidence:** In the medium term, we plan to evaluate topology transfer by instantiating the architecture on facilities with related but distinct thermal-hydraulic layouts
