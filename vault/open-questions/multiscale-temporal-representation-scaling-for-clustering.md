title: Multiscale temporal representation scaling
---

**Background:** Clustering performance and computational efficiency involve an inherent trade-off in time-series analysis, and existing methods often struggle to balance expressive temporal representations with the scalability required for large-scale datasets.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is needed to determine how to effectively integrate diverse temporal dynamics across multiple scales without incurring substantial computational overhead or relying on complex, iterative training procedures for time-series clustering.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing this bottleneck is essential for enabling efficient, large-scale time-series clustering in resource-constrained environments while maintaining high model performance.

**Evidence:** Although anchor-based methods improve scalability, their extension to time-series data remains limited due to the difficulty of capturing multiscale temporal representations.
