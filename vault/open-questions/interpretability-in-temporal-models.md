title: Interpretability in Temporal Models
---

**Background:** In high-stakes applications like healthcare and finance, the interpretability of model decisions is as important as predictive accuracy. Although large models show promise in these areas, their internal mechanisms often remain opaque, making it challenging to establish trust or perform root cause analysis.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work must address the gap in interpretability by developing frameworks that explain why large models produce specific temporal forecasts or predictions. This involves moving beyond black-box performance metrics to provide human-understandable rationales, counterfactual analysis, and causal insights.

**Why It Matters:** Without interpretability, large models face adoption barriers in sectors where accountability, regulatory compliance, and the ability to explain decisions are mandatory.
