title: Generalizability of LUT-based Precomputation
---

**Background:** Lookup table (LUT)-based precomputation techniques, which map neural network components directly to FPGA hardware, currently struggle with scalability for complex architectures despite their benefits in latency and resource efficiency.

**Question / Future Work:** Expanding the approach to broader classes of time-series datasets, such as the CHB-MIT seizure prediction database, is required to evaluate the generalizability of grouped convolutional block designs and automated hardware generation.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical to determine if the proposed grouped convolution method is a universal architectural pattern for tiny sensor systems or specific to ECG-based atrial fibrillation tasks.
