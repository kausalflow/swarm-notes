title: Harmonizing Global Pollutant Units
---

**Background:** Monitoring networks globally use heterogeneous air quality measurement units, which complicate the aggregation of model performance metrics and hinder direct cross-region comparisons.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to perform a comprehensive station-level audit of pollutant concentration units and develop standardized, explicit conversion rules to harmonize disparate datasets. Achieving this is necessary to move beyond current benchmark practices that rely on either fragmented provider-native units or potentially obfuscating normalization methods, ultimately allowing for physically homogeneous error interpretation.

**Why It Matters:** This is crucial for moving the field toward physically interpretable evaluation, ensuring that forecasting metrics are meaningful and comparable across different global monitoring networks and pollutant types.
