title: Modeling complex interaction structures
---

**Background:** Complex systems in diverse domains often exhibit behaviors governed by delayed interactions, high-order dependencies, or non-local influences.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing extensions to symbolic discovery frameworks to explicitly model non-local, time-delayed, or hypergraph-based interaction structures is necessary for capturing complex dynamics in fields such as biology and climate science.

**Why It Matters:** Current frameworks are primarily limited to pairwise interactions; generalizing to higher-order dependencies is crucial for modeling many real-world complex systems accurately.

**Evidence:** Second, higher-order, delayed, or nonlocal interactions common in biology and climate may require hypergraph or delay-embedded extensions.
