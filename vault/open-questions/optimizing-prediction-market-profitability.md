title: Optimizing Prediction Market Profitability
---

**Background:** Prediction markets face challenges in effectively converting a forecaster's predictive accuracy into consistent trading profits due to market frictions and the non-trivial nature of mapping probabilities to bet sizes.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to explore data-adaptive scoring-rule selection to maximize profitability, investigate market design to better elicit large-scale accurate forecasting, and extend expected profit guarantees to risk-adjusted criteria for real-world deployment.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing these gaps is essential for bridging the discrepancy between theoretical accuracy and empirical financial performance in prediction markets.

**Evidence:** Several future directions remain open. First, our persona analysis in Section 4.2 hints at the potential of data-adaptive scoring-rule selection: identifying the most suitable rule for each forecaster. Second, the framework opens market-design questions on which scoring rules and market structures best elicit accurate forecasting from participants at scale. Finally, our guarantees concern expected profit; extending them to risk-adjusted criteria would narrow the gap between theory and live deployment.
