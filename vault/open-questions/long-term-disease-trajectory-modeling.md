title: Optimizing long-term trajectory modeling
---

**Background:** Predicting disease onset from electronic health records often involves modeling patient histories as sequences of events. While transformer-based architectures have shown promise, the optimal methods for representing temporal information and diagnostic histories for long-range forecasting remain unsettled.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is needed to develop more explicit interval-aware temporal encodings and evaluate if alternative training objectives can better support longer-horizon clinical forecasting beyond standard next-event prediction.

**Why It Matters:** Optimizing architectural approaches for long-term health trajectory modeling is a prerequisite for moving beyond simple diagnostic classification to robust clinical decision support.
