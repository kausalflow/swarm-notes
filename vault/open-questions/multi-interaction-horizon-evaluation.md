title: Multi-interaction Horizon Evaluation
---

**Background:** Possession value (PV) models and world models in sports analytics often rely on point-estimate metrics rather than distributions of counterfactual futures, limiting the depth of tactical evaluation. Extending these simulations beyond immediate interactions remains a significant challenge due to compounding bias.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research should investigate extending decision-focused forecasting frameworks beyond the immediate next ball interaction to account for multi-interaction horizons, such as second balls and subsequent combination play, while addressing the compounding bias inherent in long-horizon rollouts.

**Why It Matters:** Evaluating beyond the immediate interaction is essential for a holistic understanding of play effectiveness and long-term tactical value.

**Evidence:** Extending MCPS to multi-interaction horizons (e.g., second balls and subsequent combinations) is a natural next step, but requires careful value-model calibration to avoid compounding rollout bias.
