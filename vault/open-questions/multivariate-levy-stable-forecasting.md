title: Multivariate Lévy Stable Forecasting
---

**Background:** Lévy stable distributions provide a robust framework for modeling heavy-tailed phenomena, yet the intractability of their density functions often complicates their integration into standard neural forecasting architectures.

**Question / Future Work:** The extension of characteristic function matching to multivariate settings is needed to capture complex cross-variable dependencies in tail parameters for high-dimensional risk management, beyond the currently limited univariate implementations.

**Why It Matters:** Many real-world forecasting tasks involve multi-variate dependencies where extreme events are correlated, making multivariate extensions critical for robust risk assessment.
