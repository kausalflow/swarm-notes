title: Fundamental limits of decay-rate axion searches
---

**Background:** Radioactive decay rate modulations are a potential signature of axion dark matter due to the time-varying QCD topological angle, but such measurements are sensitive to environmental systematics such as temperature-induced fluctuations in detector dead time. The primary sensitivity limitation in current laboratory experiments searching for these modulations is the total counting statistics and the signal dependence on the square of the axion decay constant.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the fundamental limits of nuclear decay-rate monitoring as a probe for axion dark matter remains an open challenge. Specifically, it is necessary to identify how to further mitigate systematic effects (such as temperature-dependent amplifier dead time) and whether reaching the shot-noise limit through significantly higher event rates can make this technique competitive with current linear-coupling searches like electric dipole moment experiments.

**Why It Matters:** This is a foundational question for the field of nuclear-based axion detection because it defines whether these experiments can transcend their status as 'proof of principle' to become precision tools capable of probing viable regions of the axion parameter space currently inaccessible to other laboratory methods.

**Evidence:** The ultimate reach is expected to be limited mainly by the achievable counting statistics and by the fact that decay-rate modulations arise from θ^2 effects, unlike EDM searches which are linear in θ.
