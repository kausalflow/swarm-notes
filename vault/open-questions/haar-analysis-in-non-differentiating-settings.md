title: Haar Analysis in Non-Differentiating Settings
---

**Background:** The study of Lipschitz regularity in non-atomic probability spaces often utilizes dyadic families that do not possess the structure of a differentiation basis, where standard density results for Lebesgue spaces fail.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains to determine which generalized dyadic systems permit the construction of a complete orthonormal basis for L^2 in the absence of the classical differentiation property, or to establish alternative analytical tools for function representation when density arguments are unavailable.

**Why It Matters:** This is technically important because the lack of density impacts the ability to represent arbitrary functions as limits of projections onto these Haar-type subspaces, a fundamental requirement for generalizing wavelet analysis.

**Evidence:** In this note we work on probability non-atomic spaces, without metric, but equipped with a family of dyadic sets that does not need to be a differentiation basis for the Lebesgue spaces of the setting... Under our general assumptions, the density of U_{j>=0} V_j fails to be true.
