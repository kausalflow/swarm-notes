title: Finite-Sample Size Control in Non-Stationary Tests
---

**Background:** Non-stationary time series analysis relies heavily on limit theorems for kernel-weighted partial sums under physical dependence conditions.

**Question / Future Work:** The simulation study reveals that the proposed localized testing procedures display noticeable finite-sample conservatism or size distortion depending on the specific form of non-stationarity and serial dependence. Developing refined bandwidth selection rules or higher-order bias corrections to mitigate these finite-sample size distortions remains an important area for future research.

**Why It Matters:** Finite-sample size control is critical for practical deployment of change detection tests in non-stationary time series, making the reduction of these distortions a key methodological priority.
