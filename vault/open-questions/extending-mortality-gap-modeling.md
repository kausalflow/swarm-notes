title: Extending Mortality Gap Modeling
---

**Background:** Mortality forecasting frequently involves modeling disparities between groups (e.g., gender or regional gaps) to improve predictive accuracy and understanding of trends.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate whether these mortality gap forecasting frameworks can be improved by integrating exogenous explanatory variables, such as socio-economic indicators or cause-of-death data, rather than relying solely on temporal dependencies of the gaps themselves.

**Why It Matters:** Moving from univariate gap forecasting to incorporating structural covariates is essential for improving the precision of public health policy and understanding the drivers of demographic divergence.

**Evidence:** The mortality gap could be divided by other factors, such as differences in socio-economics or household wealth... or causes-of-death.
