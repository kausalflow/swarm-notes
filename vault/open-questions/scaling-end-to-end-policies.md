title: Scaling End-to-End Portfolio Policies
---

**Background:** End-to-end portfolio optimization models can theoretically scale to a vast number of instruments to increase diversification and signal breadth. Developing methods to incorporate large-scale contract and asset-class embeddings allows models to learn shared relationships across diverse instruments.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research should address the integration of larger, more diverse futures universes and the development of contract-specific embeddings to allow models to learn shared relationships while maintaining unique asset-specific behavior.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses a fundamental scaling bottleneck in quantitative finance where capturing meaningful patterns across an expansive, heterogeneous asset universe is essential for robust, diversified strategies.
