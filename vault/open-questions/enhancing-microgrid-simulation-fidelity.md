title: Enhancing Microgrid Simulation Fidelity
---

**Background:** Microgrid energy management has traditionally relied on quantitative time-series data, often ignoring rich, unstructured contextual information such as human-generated event logs and schedules that significantly influence energy consumption. Integrating this qualitative context with quantitative physical models remains a central challenge for developing truly intelligent, context-aware energy systems.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop more sophisticated, high-fidelity surrogate models for lithium-ion batteries and PV generation that can capture complex nonlinear dynamics, such as battery degradation effects, to improve upon simple linear models. Furthermore, integrating these models with established reinforcement learning (RL) frameworks would provide a standardized benchmark environment for evaluating optimization and in-context reasoning algorithms in microgrid management.

**Why It Matters:** Advancing these surrogate models and RL integration is critical for transitioning from basic proof-of-concept simulators to robust, industrial-grade testbeds capable of supporting advanced, reliable control strategy development.

**Evidence:** Third, more sophisticated surrogate models for lithium-ion batteries and PV generation will be added, enabling higher-fidelity simulations that capture nonlinearities and degradation effects. Finally, we will provide integration with RL frameworks to provide a benchmark environment for optimization, reinforcement learning, and in-context reasoning.
