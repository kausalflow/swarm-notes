title: Theoretical Bounds for Heterogeneous MI Estimators
---

**Background:** Theoretical consistency analysis, particularly regarding error bounds and stability, remains underdeveloped for nonparametric mutual information estimators applied to finite-sample temporal data. Current theoretical guarantees often rely on large-sample assumptions that do not hold in real-world time series settings.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to establish more rigorous, finite-sample error bounds and consistency proofs for mutual information estimators applied to heterogeneous temporal data, particularly when employing latent clustering or mixture models.

**Why It Matters:** Reliable dependency estimation for causal inference and feature selection requires robust theoretical error bounds beyond empirical validation.
