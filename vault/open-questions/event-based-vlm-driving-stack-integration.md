title: Event-Based VLM Driving Stack Integration
---

**Background:** Autonomous driving systems require robust perception, prediction, and planning under challenging conditions like low light and high-speed motion, where conventional sensors often fail. Integrating asynchronous, high-temporal-resolution event camera data remains limited by the lack of large-scale benchmarks and unified frameworks for high-level driving tasks.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a fundamental need for unified benchmarks and frameworks that bridge asynchronous event sensing with vision-language models for full-stack autonomous driving tasks (perception, understanding, prediction, and planning). Future research must establish how to effectively align high-frequency event representations with semantic vision-language embeddings to enable robust reasoning and decision-making.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing this integration is necessary for moving beyond isolated perception tasks to holistic, safety-critical decision-making architectures.
