title: Internal Model Principle Integration
---

**Background:** The internal model principle is a fundamental concept in control theory where a controller incorporates a model of the reference signal or disturbance to achieve asymptotic tracking or rejection. While currently applied to various control architectures, its integration into data-driven state-space model frameworks remains an area for development.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed to integrate the internal model principle into the established structured state-space model (SSM) framework to enhance robustness and performance in tracking tasks and disturbance rejection. This involves extending the existing LMI-based controller synthesis to accommodate the structural requirements of the internal model principle while maintaining the contraction-based stability guarantees.

**Why It Matters:** This integration is critical for transitioning the proposed SSM control framework from stabilization to high-performance tracking and disturbance rejection in practical, real-world control applications.

**Evidence:** Future work will focus on integrating the internal model principle to enhance robustness and performance.
