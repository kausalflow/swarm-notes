title: Robust High-Frequency WD Detection
---

**Background:** Pulsating white dwarfs exhibit complex light curve features and high-frequency oscillations that often challenge standard detection and classification algorithms, particularly when signal-to-noise ratios are limited.

**Question / Future Work:** The current methodology for detecting periodic white dwarfs, specifically using multi-band Lomb-Scargle periodograms with a fixed Lomb-Scargle power threshold, may systematically exclude high-frequency variables like ZZ Ceti stars due to their low amplitude-to-noise ratio. Research is needed to develop more sensitive, robust techniques for detecting and classifying high-frequency periodic white dwarfs that can operate reliably despite noisy light curves or sparse sampling.

**Why It Matters:** The current inability to consistently detect these variables limits the completeness of white dwarf variability surveys, which are essential for mapping the crystallization sequence and understanding stellar evolution.

**Evidence:** We suspect this to be caused by the high frequency of ZZ Ceti stars (up to ∼900 cycles a day) - increasing the Lomb-Scargle periodogram’s sensitivity to noise in the measurements, which decreases the peak power value of the periodogram.
