title: Surface-to-Environment Mapping Uncertainty
---

**Background:** Translating stellar surface magnetic field maps into predictions for the stellar wind, EUV emission, and the astrospheric environment of exoplanets currently relies on extrapolations such as the Potential Field Source Surface (PFSS) method. The validity of these methods outside the solar regime, especially for rapidly rotating or highly magnetized stars, remains unverified.

**Question / Future Work:** Establish reliable methods for mapping surface magnetic fields to stellar wind, EUV fluxes, and the wider astrospheric environment for non-solar stars. This includes cross-validating extrapolation methods on the Sun, extending stellar wind models to encompass fast rotators and strongly magnetized M dwarfs, and quantifying the impact of cycle phase, active nests, and transients on local exoplanetary environments.

**Why It Matters:** This is the critical bottleneck in linking stellar activity to exoplanetary habitability and atmospheric evolution, as EUV/X-ray inputs are primary drivers of planetary atmospheric escape.

**Evidence:** Translating ZDI maps into reliable wind, EUV, and astrospheric current-sheet predictions for exoplanet hosts requires assumptions – a PFSS source-surface radius, a wind-driving prescription, an EUV reconstruction method – whose validity outside the solar regime is uncertain.
