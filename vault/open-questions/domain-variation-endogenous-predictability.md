title: Domain-Specific Endogenous Predictability
---

**Background:** Scientific fields exhibit varying degrees of predictability regarding endogenous concept reinforcement, which often contrasts with the more stable predictability of exogenous concept diffusion.

**Question / Future Work:** While exogenous diffusion and entropy-related metrics demonstrate consistent cross-domain predictability driven by upstream conceptual heterogeneity, endogenous self-reinforcement predictability varies significantly across different research fields. Future work is needed to systematically categorize and explain these domain-specific differences to better understand the varying roles of persistence and innovation across the scientific landscape.

**Why It Matters:** Determining why certain fields exhibit high endogenous predictability while others do not is critical for building a unified theory of scientific evolution and for refining predictive models of technological growth.

**Evidence:** the quantum-computing asymmetry—endogenous reinforcement negligible relative to exogenous diffusion—therefore does not extend uniformly: mature biomedical corpora can exhibit structured self-reinforcement that the quantum-computing testbed obscures.
