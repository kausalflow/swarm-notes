title: Generative forecasts for operational planning
---

**Background:** The cold-start forecasting problem involves predicting product life-cycle trajectories with limited or no historical data, typically requiring a model to leverage cross-product analogies and static descriptors. While existing frameworks facilitate forecasting under these conditions, there remains a need to improve the integration of long-term planning objectives into the generative process.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to connect the predictive distributions generated by the Conditional Diffusion Life-cycle Forecaster (CDLF) to downstream operational planning problems, such as launch-stage capacity planning, compute resource provisioning, and managing staged rollouts under uncertainty. Expanding the framework to handle multivariate trajectories and incorporating more diverse contextual signals (e.g., specific marketing interventions and platform-level exposure dynamics) also remain as open challenges.

**Why It Matters:** Linking generative forecasts to actionable operational decisions is critical for the practical utility of cold-start forecasting models in industrial settings.

**Evidence:** A third is to connect the predictive distributions generated by CDLF to downstream planning problems, such as launch-stage capacity planning, compute deployment, support provisioning, and staged rollout decisions under uncertainty.
