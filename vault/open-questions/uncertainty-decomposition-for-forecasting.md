title: Decomposing Aleatoric and Epistemic Uncertainty
---

**Background:** Aleatoric uncertainty arises from inherent stochasticity in the data generation process, while epistemic uncertainty stems from limitations in model knowledge or insufficient training data.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work aims to decompose predictive uncertainty into aleatoric and epistemic components to develop more interpretable and physically consistent ensemble representations in data assimilation systems. This involves evaluating whether distinguishing between these two sources of uncertainty can improve the quality of conformal prediction intervals and lead to more robust atmospheric forecasts.

**Why It Matters:** Separating these uncertainty sources is crucial for identifying whether forecasting errors originate from model deficiencies or intrinsic system randomness, which directly informs whether model architecture improvements or better observational coverage is required.
