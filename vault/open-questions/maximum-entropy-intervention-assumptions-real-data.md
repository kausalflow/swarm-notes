title: Assumptions for Maximum-Entropy Interventions
---

**Background:** Maximum-entropy interventions identify causal relationships by removing correlations among source variables in dynamical systems, yet the feasibility of this method is constrained when variables are governed by complex physical dependencies or latent confounders.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to determine how to formulate or derive valid maximum-entropy interventions for systems where observational data is inherently constrained by physical laws or latent confounding variables, rather than assuming simple independent interventions.

**Why It Matters:** This is essential for applying causal decomposition frameworks to real-world datasets where the independence assumption of maximum-entropy interventions is violated.
