title: Robustness of Tail Dependence Measures
---

**Background:** In the context of quantifying extremal dependence in time series with heavy-tailed innovations, there is a recognized need to assess the reliability of dependence measures when datasets contain outliers, heavy-tailed noise, or structural breaks.

**Question / Future Work:** The proposed tail cross-correlation measures require further investigation to determine their robustness against outliers, noise, or structural shifts, and to establish if alternative loss quantifications are necessary in these conditions.

**Why It Matters:** Financial time series are frequently characterized by non-stationarity and structural instabilities; establishing the robustness of dependence metrics is critical for valid risk management and forecasting under such conditions.

**Evidence:** An important question to ask is: “When the data gets contaminated by outliers, heavy-tailed noise, or structural breaks, can still this dependence measure work or some loss quantification is required”.
