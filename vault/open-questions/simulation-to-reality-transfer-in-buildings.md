title: Bridging Simulation-to-Reality Gap
---

**Background:** Transfer learning methods are widely used to estimate thermal dynamics in buildings where historical data are unavailable, but they have historically been evaluated primarily on simplified, single-zone, or synthetic datasets. Evaluating these methods under realistic operating conditions remains a challenge due to the lack of datasets that integrate real-world multi-zone measurements with diverse, high-fidelity synthetic data.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to systematically investigate the performance of transfer learning methods when transitioning from simulated training environments to real-world deployment (simulation-to-reality transfer). Future research should explore the gap between these two data domains and determine how to improve the robustness and generalizability of thermal models across diverse building types, HVAC configurations, and climatic conditions.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental challenge for the practical deployment of data-driven control and fault detection algorithms in buildings, as training models purely on simulated data often fails to capture the complexity of real-world operational environments.
