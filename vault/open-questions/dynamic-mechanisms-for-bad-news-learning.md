title: Mechanisms for Bad News Learning
---

**Background:** Bad news and mixed news learning processes introduce complexities in mechanism design because the buyer's potential to falsify bad signals can bind, requiring the designer to account for recursive option values.

**Question / Future Work:** Generalize the characterization of optimal dynamic informed principal mechanisms to bad news or mixed news models, where signal arrivals do not perfectly disclose match quality, requiring recursive tracking of posterior beliefs and option values.

**Why It Matters:** Many experience goods reveal information through negative experiences, making understanding bad news mechanisms essential for the broader applicability of the theory.

**Evidence:** Dealing with bad news, or a mixed news model with greater generality creates more challenges in tractability... We leave it for future research.
