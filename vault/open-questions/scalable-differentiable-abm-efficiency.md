title: Scalable Differentiable ABM Efficiency
---

**Background:** Agent-based models (ABMs) represent population-level phenomena through individual agent interactions, but they are often computationally expensive to simulate at high population densities.

**Question / Future Work:** Optimizing the computational efficiency of differentiable agent-based models for large-population simulation remains a significant bottleneck for scaling policy-intervention benchmarking. Increasing the capacity for agent-based modeling while preserving fidelity is crucial for realistic modeling of metropolitan-scale dynamics.

**Why It Matters:** Computational constraints in ABMs limit the population size and resolution of epidemic simulations, which is a major hurdle for scaling counterfactual benchmarks.
