title: Learning from Informative Missingness
---

**Background:** Clinical observations in electronic health records, such as laboratory tests and clinical notes, are often sparse and subject to non-random missingness patterns influenced by patient health states and clinician behavior.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains an open challenge to effectively integrate multimodal clinical time-series data while fully leveraging the informative, time-varying nature of missingness across both structured measurements and unstructured clinical text, particularly under multi-task scenarios that risk negative transfer.

**Why It Matters:** This is crucial for developing reliable, data-efficient, and generalizable AI-driven decision support systems in clinical settings, where data quality and completeness are significant bottlenecks.
