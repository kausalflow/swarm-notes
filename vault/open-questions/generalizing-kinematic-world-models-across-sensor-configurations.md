title: Generalizing Kinematic World Models
---

**Background:** Clinical-grade inertial measurement unit (IMU) datasets are typically limited in size due to constraints on human subject recruitment and longitudinal monitoring. Current models often fail to capture specific biomechanical and kinematic structures essential for neurological disorders when training data is restricted.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop kinematic world models capable of maintaining robust performance across varied clinical settings and sensor configurations. Research must address how to effectively leverage heterogeneous sensor setups while preserving essential translational-rotational kinematic coupling for clinical biomarker extraction.

**Why It Matters:** Handling heterogeneous, multi-site, and multi-sensor data is essential for enabling the clinical adoption of digital biomarkers in neurology, where data scarcity is a fundamental barrier to scalability.
