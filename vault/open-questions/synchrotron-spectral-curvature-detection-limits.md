title: Detecting Synchrotron Spectral Curvature
---

**Background:** Synchrotron emission spectral models are typically approximations of complex physical processes, and detecting deviations such as spectral curvature remains a significant observational challenge for accurate foreground separation in Cosmic Microwave Background studies.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining whether synchrotron spectral curvature can be robustly detected and distinguished from a power-law spectrum remains a central unresolved problem, as current sensitivity and frequency coverage are insufficient for high-significance, per-pixel detections. Future work must investigate the impact of even more complex spectral features, including those arising from Faraday rotation and depolarization, and determine the necessary instrumental sensitivity and broader frequency lever arms required to break the inherent degeneracies between spectral index and curvature.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental bottleneck for foreground cleaning in B-mode cosmology; failing to distinguish between spectral models leads to biased CMB parameter recovery. Understanding the limits of spectral characterization is critical for designing future low-frequency CMB instruments.

**Evidence:** In practice, only for |Cs| ≳ 0.14 would the data permit a 2σ detection of curvature in an individual high SNR pixel such as the Fan. ... Detecting synchrotron curvature on a pixel-by-pixel basis is limited not only by sensitivity but also by the frequency leverage of the current data.
