title: GARCH critical boundary behavior
---

**Background:** The behavior of GARCH-type models when the top Lyapunov exponent of the process is zero represents a critical threshold between stationarity and nonstationarity.

**Question / Future Work:** The asymptotic behavior of parameters and the statistical validity of inference procedures for GARCH models remain largely unresolved when the top Lyapunov exponent equals zero. Current methods for establishing consistency and asymptotic normality for stationary or explosive processes do not readily extend to this critical case.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding this case is essential for a complete inferential theory of GARCH models, as real-world financial data often exist near the boundary of stationarity.

**Evidence:** Theorems 1-2 exclude the critical case γ_α0 = 0. To the best of our knowledge, the behavior of y_t in this critical case remains largely unresolved in the literature.
