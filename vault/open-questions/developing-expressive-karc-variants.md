title: Developing expressive KARC variants
---

**Background:** Kolmogorov-Arnold networks (KANs) represent multivariate functions through compositions of learnable univariate functions placed on network edges. Kolmogorov-Arnold Reservoir Computing (KARC) utilizes a fixed basis-function expansion to represent these univariate functions, facilitating efficient closed-form training through ridge regression.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing more expressive KARC variants that retain the computational efficiency of closed-form training while capturing hierarchical compositions remains an important direction for scaling these models.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the fundamental trade-off between model expressivity and computational efficiency, which is critical for scaling these methods to more complex dynamical systems.

**Evidence:** Developing more expressive KARC variants while retaining closed-form or efficient training remains an important direction for future work.
