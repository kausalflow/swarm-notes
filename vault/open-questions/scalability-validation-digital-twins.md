title: Scalability of Digital Twin Frameworks
---

**Background:** Digital twin frameworks for data center energy management are often tested in controlled, small-scale environments, complicating the assessment of their scalability and performance in heterogeneous, large-scale deployments.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to validate existing digital twin energy optimization frameworks within large-scale, heterogeneous, and real-world data center environments to confirm their scalability and generalization beyond controlled, small-scale experimental setups. Future research should prioritize field trials in diverse infrastructure contexts and incorporate more complex operational variables.

**Why It Matters:** Ensuring that energy optimization frameworks are robust and scalable is essential for their industry adoption, as performance in constrained, small-scale models does not guarantee effectiveness in complex, high-traffic production environments.
