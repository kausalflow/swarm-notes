title: Characterizing Wireless ECG Error Sources
---

**Background:** Wireless electrocardiogram (ECG) devices offer scalable, cost-effective alternatives to traditional clinical ECG systems for long-term monitoring. However, data quality inconsistencies arising from device imperfections and non-clinical recording conditions remain significant hurdles to establishing their clinical reliability.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to isolate and characterize the distinct contributions of various error sources to overall signal degradation in wireless ECGs. Specifically, separating the influences of intrinsic device inaccuracies, calibration errors, R-peak detection failures, and sensor placement inconsistencies is necessary to improve the reliability of data collected outside controlled clinical environments.

**Why It Matters:** Distinguishing between these error sources is essential for developing standardized calibration protocols, improving algorithmic preprocessing, and ultimately meeting the strict data quality requirements for medically binding decision-making.

**Evidence:** Furthermore, we aim to differentiate the various potential sources of error (e.g., intrinsic device inaccuracies, calibration error, peak detection errors, placement errors).
