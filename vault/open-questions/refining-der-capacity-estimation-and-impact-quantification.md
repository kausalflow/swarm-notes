title: Refining DER Capacity Estimation
---

**Background:** Estimating Distributed Energy Resource (DER) installed capacity at low-voltage (LV) aggregation points remains a technical challenge due to limited grid observability and the degradation of metadata quality over time. Improved methods are required to accurately infer this metadata from readily available, aggregated substation and feeder-level measurements.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to refine the methodologies for estimating aggregated DER installed capacities, specifically addressing the accuracy of these estimations. Additionally, there is a need to quantify the actual operational and planning impact of integrating this metadata-aware knowledge into downstream decision-making processes.

**Why It Matters:** Quantifying the impact of metadata-aware methods is critical for DSOs to justify investments in better monitoring infrastructure and to validate the theoretical benefits of DER metadata on grid stability and planning efficiency.

**Evidence:** Future work should focus on further improving methods for estimating DER installed capacity, as well as testing the integration of metadata-aware methods into operational and planning use cases, quantifying the impact of DER metadata knowledge.
