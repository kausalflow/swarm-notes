title: Root Cause Diagnosis Integration
---

**Background:** Anomaly detection in complex multivariate systems is often limited by a reliance on point-wise numerical deviations rather than the violation of underlying causal relationships.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to develop robust mechanisms for automated, real-time root cause diagnosis that can integrate with existing causality-aware anomaly detection frameworks to provide localized, interpretable root-cause attributions.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the gap between anomaly detection and root cause analysis is critical for minimizing system downtime in industrial practice.

**Evidence:** Unlike traditional models with undirected and static graphs that are unable to attribute and localize the root cause, our model supports directed and real-time gradient-based causal matrix, which can be aggregated into interpretable root‑cause scores.
