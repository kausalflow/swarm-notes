title: SDDP optimality and convergence certification
---

**Background:** Stochastic Dual Dynamic Programming (SDDP) is a standard methodology for multi-stage stochastic hydrothermal scheduling, where the approximation of the cost-to-go function is central to determining dispatch policies. However, large-scale applications currently lack universally accepted finite-sample convergence tests to certify policy optimality.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of rigorous, finite-sample convergence metrics or error bounds is needed to quantify the optimality of policies generated by SDDP in complex, large-scale power system applications. This is critical for distinguishing whether observed operational inefficiencies stem from input biases or the algorithmic approximation process.

**Why It Matters:** This bottleneck is essential for distinguishing between systemic forecast bias effects and numerical optimization errors in critical energy infrastructure planning.
