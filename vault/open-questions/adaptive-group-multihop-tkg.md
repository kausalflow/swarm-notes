title: Adaptive Grouping and Multi-hop Reasoning
---

**Background:** Temporal knowledge graph reasoning often involves modeling the conditional intensity of future events, but currently lacks mechanisms for adaptive group structure and comprehensive multi-hop relational dependencies.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research should explore the development of adaptive group structures within group-based neural Hawkes processes to better represent the evolving semantics of knowledge graphs. Furthermore, extending the current point-process formulation to explicitly model multi-hop reasoning patterns across temporal knowledge graphs remains an important open challenge for improving extrapolation reasoning.

**Why It Matters:** These directions address the limitations of static group assignments and the challenges of complex, multi-hop relationship evolution in dynamic graphs, which are critical for advancing TKG extrapolation capabilities.

**Evidence:** Future work will explore adaptive group structures and extend the point-process formulation to multi-hop reasoning.
