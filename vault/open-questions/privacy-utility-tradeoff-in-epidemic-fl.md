title: Privacy-Utility Tradeoff in FL
---

**Background:** Federated learning allows collaborative training of machine learning models across decentralized nodes without centralizing raw data, but it is prone to privacy leakage from gradient updates, leading to the integration of differential privacy mechanisms that often cause significant model utility loss.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigating the tradeoff between predictive utility and formal privacy guarantees in differentially private federated learning for infectious disease dynamics requires systematic evaluation of architectural choices and privacy mechanisms to identify optimal strategies for sensitive, fragmented public health data.

**Why It Matters:** As public health authorities increasingly rely on data-driven interventions, establishing robust, privacy-preserving collaborative forecasting frameworks is essential for reliable decision support.
