title: Standardizing Transfer Robustness Metrics
---

**Background:** Transfer learning for building energy forecasting is often hampered by significant domain gaps caused by variations in building typology, climate, and occupancy patterns. A major challenge in this domain is the lack of standardized, architecture-agnostic metrics to evaluate the effectiveness and robustness of model transfer across different buildings.

**Question / Future Work:** The development and adoption of a standardized, architecture-agnostic robustness metric, such as the Transfer Robustness Index (TRI), are required to systematically compare transfer learning performance across diverse real-world building energy studies. Future research should investigate how such metrics behave across a broader range of building portfolios, climate zones, and structural typologies to ensure consistency and comparability in the field.

**Why It Matters:** Without a standardized metric, it is difficult to benchmark progress in transfer learning for energy management, making it hard to determine which methods truly improve model scalability and robustness in real-world deployments.
