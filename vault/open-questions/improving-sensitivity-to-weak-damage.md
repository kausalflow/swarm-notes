title: Improving Sensitivity to Weak Damage
---

**Background:** Structural damage identification often relies on separating damage-related information from non-damage-related operational and environmental variability, yet existing self-supervised frameworks struggle with subtle structural changes and complex nuisance factors.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop more sophisticated representation learning models to enhance the sensitivity and robustness of damage identification, particularly for subtle structural damage signatures.

**Why It Matters:** Improving sensitivity to subtle structural changes is critical for the practical deployment of structural health monitoring, as early-stage damage often presents with weak signal signatures that can be easily obscured.
