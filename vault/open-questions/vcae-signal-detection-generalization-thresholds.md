title: VCAE signal detection generalization thresholds
---

**Background:** Deep neural network performance in signal pulse identification is often evaluated using specific synthetic datasets that simulate expected experimental noise and signal topologies. Systematic quantification of these models' sensitivity and generalization capabilities remains necessary to establish robust detection thresholds across varying experimental conditions.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need for a comprehensive, rigorous quantification of the signal identification efficiency and the corresponding detection threshold for VCAE-based architectures, specifically addressing the observed discrepancies across cross-validation folds. Further investigation is also required to assess how this identification methodology generalizes when subjected to varying noise levels or different experimental backgrounds, particularly when transitioning from synthetic data to raw experimental measurements.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical for establishing the reliability of data-driven signal discovery methods in rare-event physics, where distinguishing low-energy physical signals from background fluctuations is a key bottleneck.
