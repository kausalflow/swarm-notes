title: Adaptive Prediction Horizon Design
---

**Background:** Proactive service scheduling in dynamic networks relies on predictive models to estimate the risk of mission disruption. Existing approaches typically use fixed-horizon predictions, which may not capture environmental variability or varying mission criticality.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate mechanisms for dynamically adjusting prediction horizons for risk estimation to improve resource reservation efficiency and robustness under varying mobility and environmental conditions. Current approaches typically rely on fixed windows that may not optimally adapt to heterogeneous task requirements or volatile network dynamics.

**Why It Matters:** The current limitation of fixed-horizon prediction in proactive handover schemes presents a bottleneck for robustness in highly variable mobile edge environments.
