title: Physical origin of inner rim substructures
---

**Background:** Post-AGB circumbinary discs are known to be highly dynamic, but systematic studies of the inner rim morphology and dynamics have been limited by the availability of high-fidelity, multi-epoch interferometric imaging. The origins of detected azimuthal brightness enhancements in these inner discs, potentially linked to binary interaction or hydrodynamical instabilities, remain poorly constrained.

**Question / Future Work:** Future multi-wavelength and time-series interferometric imaging is required to constrain the spatial extent, motion, and wavelength-dependence of the detected inner rim substructures to distinguish between competing physical mechanisms for these features, such as radiative responses, dynamical interactions with the central binary, or hydrodynamical instabilities.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing the physical origin of these substructures is critical to understanding the dynamical interaction between central binaries and their discs, as well as testing hypotheses related to refractory depletion and second-generation planet formation.

**Evidence:** Multi-wavelength and time-series imaging will be essential in constraining the extent, motion and wavelength-dependence of these features, and uncover their physical origins.
