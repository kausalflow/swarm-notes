title: Causal Identification of Convergence Capacity
---

**Background:** Current production function frameworks in the AI era struggle to explain why high AI investment often fails to yield commensurate productivity gains, typically treating AI as an independent factor of production while failing to account for the cognitive human mediation required for its effective use. The proposed framework posits that convergence capacity (C) is a critical, yet previously implicit, cognitive mediator comprising embodied understanding, metacognition, temporal integration, and integrative thinking.

**Question / Future Work:** A key unresolved question is the causal identification of the convergence capacity (C) variable's role in mediating AI-driven productivity. Future research must determine the specific antecedents of C formation, including the institutional, social, and motivational conditions under which C develops concurrently with human capital, to move beyond descriptive cross-national patterns toward validated, firm-level causal identification.

**Why It Matters:** Identifying the causal mechanisms for C development is essential to move from descriptive cross-national frameworks to an actionable, policy-driven model for sustainable productivity growth.
