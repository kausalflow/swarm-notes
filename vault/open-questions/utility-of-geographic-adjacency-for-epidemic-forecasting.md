title: Utility of Geographic Adjacency
---

**Background:** Geographic adjacency is frequently utilized in spatiotemporal epidemic models under the assumption that it captures meaningful epidemiological transmission patterns.

**Question / Future Work:** Current evidence suggests that geographic adjacency may serve as a poor proxy for the complex cross-region dependencies observed in epidemic spreading, and it remains unclear how to define more effective spatial information structures for predictive modeling. Future work is needed to determine whether more sophisticated spatial features—such as mobility or contact networks—can reliably outperform simple univariate benchmarks.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the limitations of geographic adjacency is critical for moving beyond current performance plateaus in spatiotemporal epidemic forecasting.

**Evidence:** geographic adjacency may be a poor proxy for cross-region dependencies and can hinder performance.
