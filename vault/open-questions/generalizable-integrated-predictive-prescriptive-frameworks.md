title: Scaling Integrated AI Frameworks
---

**Background:** Supply chain management requires balancing predictive demand forecasting with prescriptive operational optimization, often under conditions of high volatility and uncertainty. Conventional systems typically approach these tasks sequentially, which can lead to suboptimal decision-making when predictive errors propagate into operational constraints.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of scalable, adaptive architectures capable of jointly training predictive and prescriptive modules across diverse industrial domains (e.g., beyond textiles and PPE) remains an open challenge. Future research is needed to validate the cross-domain transferability, computational scalability, and real-time adaptability of integrated end-to-end learning frameworks, specifically investigating the use of multi-agent reinforcement learning or graph neural networks for managing complex, dynamic supplier networks.

**Why It Matters:** This is central to the field because it addresses the core bottleneck of creating generalizable, industrially scalable, and truly integrated AI systems that move beyond domain-specific pilot studies.

**Evidence:** Future work will extend the framework to multiple industrial sectors to empirically validate scalability and demonstrate cross-domain transferability... Future extensions could incorporate multi-agent reinforcement learning or graph neural networks for dynamic supplier coordination.
