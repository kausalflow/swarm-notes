title: Complex Outbreak Segmentation Methods
---

**Background:** Infectious disease forecasting often relies on segmenting continuous surveillance time series into discrete outbreak episodes, which typically ignores multi-wave dynamics caused by external interventions and viral evolution.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop more robust methods for segmenting complex, multi-wave epidemic time series that move beyond existing single-outbreak, derivative-based constraints to better capture the realities of long-term public health surveillance.

**Why It Matters:** This is a core methodological bottleneck in transitioning from single-wave forecasting benchmarks to real-world, multi-pathogen epidemiological environments.
