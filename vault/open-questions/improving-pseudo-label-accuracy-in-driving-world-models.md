title: Improving Pseudo-label Accuracy
---

**Background:** Autonomous driving models often rely on pretrained foundational models to generate pseudo-labels for training when human annotations are unavailable. These pseudo-labels frequently suffer from inaccuracies that can negatively impact the final model performance.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed to improve the quality, reliability, and precision of pseudo-labels (such as depth and semantic maps) generated by pretrained foundational models. Establishing more accurate self-supervision signals is crucial for perception-free driving world models to reach or exceed the performance of perception-based models that utilize manual annotations.

**Why It Matters:** This is the primary bottleneck identified by the authors for why their perception-free model does not yet outperform SOTA perception-based models, and it represents a core challenge for the broader field of self-supervised autonomous driving.

**Evidence:** However, our model does not yet surpass SOTA perception-based models, primarily due to challenges arising from the inherent imprecision of the pseudo depth and semantic labels generated by pretrained foundational models.
