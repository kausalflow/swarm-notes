title: Viscous-epicyclic overstability in GRMHD
---

**Background:** Viscous-epicyclic overstability is a mechanism for quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) in thin accretion disks, where stress-pressure phase lags facilitate energy extraction from orbital shear. While confirmed in local models, its robustness in fully nonlinear, global GRMHD flows with varying magnetic topologies remains limited.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to determine whether viscous-epicyclic overstability persists in global, three-dimensional, radiation-GRMHD simulations of radiatively efficient accretion systems. It is currently unclear how complex magnetic topologies and radiative cooling interact to modulate these oscillations and whether this mechanism consistently explains observed X-ray binary timing properties.

**Why It Matters:** This question is central to determining the physical validity of a leading theoretical mechanism for accretion disk variability and connecting simulations to astrophysical observations.

**Evidence:** Extending this analysis to fully three-dimensional, radiation-GRMHD simulations will be essential to establish whether the viscous-epicyclic overstability can operate in luminous, radiatively efficient systems and to determine its potential role in producing the observed QPOs in BHXRBs.
