title: Theoretical Estimation for Mixed-Frequency Networks
---

**Background:** Frequency alignment remains a central bottleneck in mixed-frequency time series analysis, where traditional linear or fixed-structure parametric models often struggle to capture complex, non-linear cross-frequency dependencies.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to establish rigorous statistical estimation theory for neural-network-based mixed-frequency models that moves beyond simple least-squares frameworks to provide reliable non-asymptotic error bounds and formal convergence guarantees.

**Why It Matters:** Theoretical reliability is required to transition deep learning architectures from empirical experiments to robust tools for complex economic and financial forecasting.

**Evidence:** neural-network variants have yet to offer reliable estimation theory.
