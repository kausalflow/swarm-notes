title: Efficient Covariate-Informed TSFMs
---

**Background:** Time series foundation models (TSFMs) are increasingly designed for zero-shot forecasting, yet most current architectures are limited by computational complexity or a lack of support for heterogeneous variable types, particularly exogenous covariates.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an ongoing need to develop model architectures that can effectively ingest and utilize a wide range of variable types—univariate, multivariate, and covariate-informed—within a unified, computationally efficient zero-shot framework. Future research is required to explore more scalable and flexible transformer-based modules that can handle heterogeneous data inputs without relying on massive parameter counts or expensive inference procedures.

**Why It Matters:** Improving the covariate-informed forecasting capability of tiny TSFMs is essential for enabling real-time, low-latency intelligence in resource-constrained environments.

**Evidence:** TSFM architectures that can accommodate such diverse variables remain largely underexplored.
