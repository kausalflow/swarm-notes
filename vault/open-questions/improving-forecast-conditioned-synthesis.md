title: Improving Forecast-Conditioned Data Synthesis
---

**Background:** Software engineering benchmark construction faces a tradeoff between using realistic historical data, which risks pre-training contamination, and synthetic generation, which may lack project-specific project priorities. Forecast-conditioned synthesis aims to bridge this by using predictive models to guide task creation.

**Question / Future Work:** Substantial research is needed to refine the forecasting of repository evolution and to enhance the fidelity of synthesized test patches generated through these forecasts. Key challenges include improving the retrieval mechanisms over repository evidence, filtering metadata noise, and ensuring synthesized tests accurately mirror human maintainer patterns.

**Why It Matters:** The reliability of coding-agent benchmarks depends on their ability to simulate authentic future project needs without replaying known historical pull requests.
