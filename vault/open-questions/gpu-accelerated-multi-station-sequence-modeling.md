title: GPU-accelerated multi-station sequence modeling
---

**Background:** Temporal sequence modeling for air-quality estimation often faces significant computational challenges when implemented across multiple geographical monitoring stations.

**Question / Future Work:** Addressing computational and training bottlenecks in applying deep sequence-based models to large, multi-station environmental datasets remains critical for surpassing the performance of simpler regression baselines.

**Why It Matters:** The study highlights that computational constraints prevented deeper sequence models (LSTM) from being effectively trained and evaluated against simpler architectures in air quality nowcasting. Overcoming this is essential for realizing the potential of more sophisticated temporal dynamics.
