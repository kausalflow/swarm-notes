title: Distributionally Robust Portfolio Layers
---

**Background:** Portfolio construction often relies on quadratic programming techniques that may struggle with asset return uncertainty and real-world constraints.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed to develop distributionally robust portfolio layers for benchmarking time-series forecasts that can better account for ambiguity in expected returns and risk beyond standard quadratic programming.

**Why It Matters:** Standard constrained-QP layers often fail to account for the full complexity of market regimes, making them a bottleneck in assessing the practical viability of deep time-series models.
