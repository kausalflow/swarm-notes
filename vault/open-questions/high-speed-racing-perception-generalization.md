title: High-Speed Racing Perception Generalization
---

**Background:** High-speed autonomous racing involves extreme dynamics and significant domain shifts that are poorly captured by conventional urban-driving datasets. Current benchmarks struggle to mitigate performance drops when transferring models across simulated and real-world high-speed scenarios.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining effective techniques for domain adaptation specifically for high-speed racing environments, joint detection-prediction modeling, and multi-agent interaction reasoning remains an open challenge. Research is required to bridge the persistent sim-to-real gap while improving the robustness of perception systems under extreme racing dynamics.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding these bottlenecks is critical for advancing full-stack autonomy in extreme high-speed scenarios where conventional perception systems currently fail.

**Evidence:** Future work will investigate domain adaptation techniques tailored to high-speed racing, joint detection–prediction modeling, and multi-agent interaction reasoning. Expanding real-world racing datasets and incorporating additional sensing modalities such as radar may further reduce the sim-to-real gap and improve robustness under extreme dynamics.
