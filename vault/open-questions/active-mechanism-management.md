title: Active Mechanism Management
---

**Background:** Mechanistic World Models are designed to support active inquiry, where the model itself proposes hypotheses and designs experiments to refine its internal explanatory structures. Current research in AI for Science primarily focuses on passive learning from observational data.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a significant lack of machinery for active mechanism management, which includes determining when to create, merge, or refine mechanisms, selecting optimal information-seeking interventions to verify hypotheses, and dynamically reorganizing the mechanism library. Developing these 'active inquiry' capabilities is necessary for a model to function as an autonomous scientific agent rather than a passive observer.

**Why It Matters:** Active inquiry is what distinguishes true scientific discovery from mere curve fitting or pattern matching. Implementing these capabilities is essential for models to move from descriptive modeling to the iterative, hypothesis-driven cycle of the scientific method.
