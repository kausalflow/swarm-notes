title: Optimal Horizon Set Selection
---

**Background:** Long-horizon forecasting in geophysical systems often suffers from error accumulation and distribution drift when autoregressive models are applied over extended time periods. While multi-horizon training and greedy inference strategies can mitigate these issues, the optimal selection of horizon sets to balance training complexity and forecast accuracy remains an area for further investigation.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work should systematically explore the theoretical and empirical guidelines for selecting optimal discrete horizon sets for multi-horizon emulators. Investigating automated strategies or adaptive horizon selection methods that account for the varying dynamics of different physical systems could improve model robustness and efficiency.

**Why It Matters:** The selection of horizon sets directly impacts both computational cost and prediction quality, and understanding how to systematically choose these sets is essential for scaling to more complex systems.
