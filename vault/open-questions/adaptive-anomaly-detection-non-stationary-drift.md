title: Adaptive Detection Under Drift
---

**Background:** Real-world industrial environments often exhibit dynamic environmental changes, leading to slow distributional drift in time-series data.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing mechanisms that maintain model accuracy without frequent full retraining is a critical area for anomaly detection in non-stationary industrial systems. Future work aims to refine adaptive, lightweight network architectures and universal contrastive learning frameworks capable of adjusting to evolving data distributions in real-time.

**Why It Matters:** This is a significant bottleneck for deploying deep learning-based anomaly detectors in real-world industrial settings, where retraining is costly and data distributions change over time.

**Evidence:** In practical industrial scenarios, time-series data features often exhibit slow distributional drift due to dynamic environmental changes. ... Future research directions will focus on lightweight network optimization, adaptive receptive field tuning, and the construction of a universal contrastive learning framework.
