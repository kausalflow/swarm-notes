title: Multivariate spatiotemporal downscaling consistency
---

**Background:** Statistical downscaling methods for climate variables often lack the ability to simultaneously model multiple climate variables or multiple spatial locations while maintaining physical and spatial consistency.

**Question / Future Work:** Current downscaling frameworks typically operate on a location-by-location basis, failing to capture the multivariate dependency between variables like temperature and precipitation, or the spatial dependence between nearby locations. Future research is required to develop multivariate downscaling models that can jointly simulate weather variables across multiple spatial points while preserving their inter-variable and spatial correlations.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental bottleneck for hydrological applications that rely on coherent spatiotemporal weather series, and addressing it is necessary to move beyond independent, univariate downscaling.
