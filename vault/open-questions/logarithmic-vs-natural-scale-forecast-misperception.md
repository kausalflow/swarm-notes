title: Logarithmic Scale Forecast Misperception
---

**Background:** Forecasts in human judgment experiments are often solicited using logarithmic scales, though the impact of this scale on user interpretation and probabilistic intent remains unclear.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need for randomized studies comparing logarithmic versus natural scale solicitation to determine if observed skew and uncertainty intervals are intentional or artifacts of the user interface. Understanding whether logarithmic scales distort user intent is vital for the design of accurate public health forecasting tools.

**Why It Matters:** This targets a fundamental limitation in forecast elicitation design that may lead to systematically biased human judgment in pandemic scenarios.
