title: TSFM Scaling Performance Relationship
---

**Background:** Time Series Foundation Models (TSFMs) have shown promise for various forecasting tasks, yet their performance varies significantly depending on model architecture, parameter scale, and the heterogeneity of the data.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need for a systematic analysis of the relationship between TSFM model size, architecture, and performance across diverse domains. Current findings indicate that performance scaling is non-uniform, necessitating research to identify optimal model configurations for specific temporal data patterns.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding TSFM scaling behavior is critical for deploying efficient, reliable models in resource-constrained infrastructure applications like decarbonization.

**Evidence:** These results underscore the need for more in-depth analysis of model size, architecture, and performance. ... However, in some grids, smaller models perform better — especially for Time-MoE.
