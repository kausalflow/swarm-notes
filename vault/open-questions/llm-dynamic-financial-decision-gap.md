title: LLM Dynamic Financial Decision-making Gap
---

**Background:** Large language models (LLMs) often exhibit high performance on static financial benchmarks, but this success frequently fails to translate into effective decision-making in dynamic, multi-agent financial environments such as board games.

**Question / Future Work:** Current research identifies a performance gap where advanced LLMs struggle to execute multi-step strategic interactions, manage liquidity under uncertainty, and convert financial knowledge into consistent, long-term success in dynamic environments. Further investigation is required to determine whether this failure stems from inherent limitations in planning, deficiencies in internal world models, or insufficient integration of real-time feedback loops in complex competitive scenarios.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the misalignment between static knowledge and dynamic decision-making is critical for deploying LLMs in real-world financial systems where adaptation to stochastic events and multi-agent competition is required.
