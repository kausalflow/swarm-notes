title: Phys-JEPA under explicit constraints
---

**Background:** Multivariate forecasting in physical systems often relies on latent state representations that may lack explicit physical structure, potentially leading to instability or interpretability issues over long horizons.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigating the performance and validity of latent-level physics constraints in environments where governing physical equations are explicit and known, rather than relying on weak-domain descriptors.

**Why It Matters:** Determining whether latent-level physical grounding scales to rigorous law-based systems is critical for verifying the generality of this approach.

**Evidence:** Phys-JEPA should be studied on problems with stronger and more explicit physical constraints, where descriptor quality and transition laws can be evaluated more directly.
