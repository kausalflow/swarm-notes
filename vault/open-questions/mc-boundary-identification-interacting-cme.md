title: Boundary Identification in Interacting ICMEs
---

**Background:** Interplanetary coronal mass ejections (ICMEs) frequently interact with one another, leading to merged, complex magnetic structures that complicate boundary identification. Conventional identification methods designed for isolated, single events may struggle to resolve the boundaries of such interacting systems.

**Question / Future Work:** The performance of automated boundary identification methods in the context of interacting ICMEs remains untested, and it is unclear if these techniques can effectively distinguish between internal and external boundaries in complex, non-isolated events.

**Why It Matters:** Most geoeffective events involve complex ICME interactions; therefore, developing tools that accurately identify boundaries in these environments is critical for space weather forecasting.

**Evidence:** The present method has not been tested on such interacting events, and its performance under those conditions remains an open question.
