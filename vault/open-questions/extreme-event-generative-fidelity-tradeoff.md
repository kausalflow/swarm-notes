title: Extreme Event Generative Fidelity Tradeoff
---

**Background:** Generating synthetic time-series data that faithfully represents extreme events, which are inherently rare and often poorly captured by standard generative models, remains a significant challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to understand how generative frameworks can balance the accurate synthesis of rare, high-impact extreme events with the preservation of overall distributional fidelity, especially when explicit extreme-event labels are unavailable.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental limitation in generative modeling for time-series, as current metrics often favor average-case performance at the expense of tail-risk and anomaly realism.
