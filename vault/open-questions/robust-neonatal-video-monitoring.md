title: Robustness of Neonatal Video Monitoring
---

**Background:** The deployment of video-based respiratory monitoring in neonatal intensive care units is challenged by environmental variability such as changing lighting, occlusion, and caregiver intervention. Current frameworks often rely on limited, single-center datasets that may not capture the full spectrum of clinical operational conditions.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed to improve the robustness of video-based respiratory monitoring under real-world clinical stressors and to standardize evaluation using large-scale, multi-center datasets that capture diverse patient populations and incubator configurations. Addressing the performance gap in tracking region-of-interest (ROI) reliability in the presence of occlusion and caregiver interaction is a critical bottleneck.

**Why It Matters:** Identifying solutions for these environmental bottlenecks is essential for transitioning non-contact medical monitoring from controlled research settings to reliable bedside clinical care.
