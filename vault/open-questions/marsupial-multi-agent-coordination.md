title: Marsupial Multi-Agent Coordination
---

**Background:** Marsupial robotic systems consist of an aerial unit carried by a ground platform to combine endurance with reachability in challenging environments.

**Question / Future Work:** While marsupial systems are effective for accessing difficult terrain, the coordination of collaborative mission planning and dynamic map sharing between a legged-aerial pair remains an area requiring further investigation, specifically regarding real-time efficiency and scalability in complex, unstructured forest environments.

**Why It Matters:** Multi-agent coordination in unstructured, GPS-denied environments is a critical bottleneck for scaling autonomous precision forestry; resolving this is essential for fully leveraging heterogeneous robotic teams.
