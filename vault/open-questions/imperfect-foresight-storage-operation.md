title: Imperfect foresight in storage
---

**Background:** Energy systems modeling typically relies on the assumption of perfect foresight to determine optimal storage capacity, which deviates significantly from the operational realities faced by system operators.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research should evaluate the impacts of relaxing the perfect foresight assumption in energy models, particularly for long-duration storage operation during renewable droughts, to account for real-world risk-hedging and operational constraints.

**Why It Matters:** Necessary to bridge the gap between idealized planning benchmarks and practical market-based energy system operation.

**Evidence:** Accordingly, the least-cost operational and capacity decisions of the long-duration storage found in this analysis should be interpreted as benchmark solutions. Under imperfect foresight, which applies to operators in the real world, long-duration storage needs may exceed those determined here.
