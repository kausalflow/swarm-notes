title: Compositional OOD Dynamics Generalization
---

**Background:** Generative models of complex dynamical systems are often limited by categorical conditioning, which prevents flexible, compositional specification of tasks for out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization.

**Question / Future Work:** The capability to synthesize realistic neural dynamics for arbitrary, complex experimental designs through compositional language alone requires further refinement and better understanding of the limits of OOD extrapolation in high-dimensional state spaces. Bridging the gap between linguistic prompts and realistic dynamic outputs while maintaining compositionality is a key bottleneck for in-silico experimental design.

**Why It Matters:** This is a central bottleneck for flexible in-silico scientific design, where researchers need to test novel, non-existent experimental scenarios without new data.

**Evidence:** far-extrapolative text conditions remain difficult and performance degrades with distance from the training support.
