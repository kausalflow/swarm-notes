title: Non-Euclidean Residual Diagnostic Theory
---

**Background:** Residual-based model diagnostic frameworks often require asymptotic theory to account for parameter estimation uncertainty, which is historically established primarily for Euclidean-valued observations.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing a rigorous asymptotic theory for AutoHSIC-based residual diagnostics in functional, matrix-valued, or other non-Euclidean time series domains remains an open challenge for valid statistical inference.

**Why It Matters:** This is a critical gap for applying robust model diagnostic tools to complex time series data where Euclidean assumptions are invalid.

**Evidence:** Extending it to functional, matrix-valued or other non-Euclidean time series would require additional theory for estimated objects in structured spaces.
