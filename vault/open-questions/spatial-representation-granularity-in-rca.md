title: Spatial Representation Granularity
---

**Background:** Spatial abstractions used in network root cause analysis often facilitate system modeling at the cost of losing critical topological and node-level specificity.

**Question / Future Work:** How to develop spatial representation methods for network root cause analysis that balance complexity reduction with the preservation of signal granularity necessary to differentiate between proximate but distinct fault scenarios.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses a fundamental limitation where excessive compression leads to the misclassification of faults in geographically or topologically proximate network locations.

**Evidence:** While the spatial simplifications introduced in Section IV-A successfully removed unnecessary location-specific complexity, they also resulted in excessive information compression.
