title: Forecasting Turbulent Flows with Incomplete Data
---

**Background:** Data-driven surrogate models in fluid mechanics are often restricted by sparse or noisy experimental measurements, which hampers their ability to capture transient features and high-frequency turbulent dynamics.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing surrogate models that maintain physical fidelity and capture high-frequency energy when observations are spatially incomplete or temporally noisy remains a critical research challenge for real-world fluid control.

**Why It Matters:** This issue is a central hurdle for applying machine learning to real-world engineering fluid mechanics.

**Evidence:** the data-driven models struggle to preserve transient flow features and high-frequency energy content when faced with noisy measurements and incomplete state observations.
