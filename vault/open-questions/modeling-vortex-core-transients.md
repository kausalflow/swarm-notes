title: Modeling Vortex Core Transients
---

**Background:** Magnetic vortex systems can exhibit self-sustained core gyration driven by nonlinear interactions with azimuthal spin wave modes. Extending these models to capture the temporal evolution of the vortex core during the onset of steady-state motion remains an open challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** The existing collective-coordinate (Thiele) model successfully describes steady-state gyration, but it relies on an equilibrium state assumption. Future work is required to incorporate bilinear terms into the Lagrangian to explicitly model the transient dynamics of the vortex core as it deviates from its initial equilibrium position.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the transient phase is essential for accurately describing the onset of auto-oscillations and the transition into specific Floquet states, which is critical for potential applications in non-linear magnon-based computing.

**Evidence:** It may be worthwhile to extend this formalism in future work in order to account for the transient dynamics of the vortex core, e.g., how it is initially driven away from its equilibrium position, by including bilinear terms in the Lagrangian.
