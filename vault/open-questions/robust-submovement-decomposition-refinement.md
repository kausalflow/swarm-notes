title: Robust Submovement Decomposition Refinement
---

**Background:** Submovement decomposition is an iterative process used to resolve overlapping ballistic components of motion, yet current techniques struggle with robustness in high-overlap scenarios. Reliance on heuristic-based termination criteria remains a bottleneck for accuracy and consistency across different human motion patterns.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed to develop more robust confidence metrics and adaptive termination criteria for submovement decomposition, specifically to differentiate between fit quality issues and genuine motion components. Further investigation should explore how varying termination conditions impact the identification of secondary corrective movements compared to primary ballistic phases.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing standardized, robust decomposition frameworks is vital for motor assessment and adaptive human-computer interaction applications.

**Evidence:** Additional decomposition loop termination criteria can, and should, be investigated depending on the use of the decomposition strategy.
