title: Quantifying Causal Drivers of Bias
---

**Background:** Gender bias in media coverage is intrinsically linked to broader societal structures, creating a reciprocal relationship where news reporting both reflects and influences social inequality. While advanced statistical models can identify these patterns, quantifying the precise causal mechanisms driving this bias remains a complex challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an explicit need to develop methodologies that can move beyond identifying and describing patterns of gender bias to quantifying the driving factors behind these representations. Current research struggles to isolate the causal relationships between media content and external social structures, necessitating future investigation into the underlying drivers of bias.

**Why It Matters:** Distinguishing between descriptive patterns and causal drivers is essential for moving from mere identification of bias to effective policy interventions. Understanding the 'why' behind media representation is the primary bottleneck in gender bias research.

**Evidence:** our project does not quantify the reasons for gender bias.
