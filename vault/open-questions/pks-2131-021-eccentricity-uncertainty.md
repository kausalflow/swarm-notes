title: Constraining PKS 2131-021 Eccentricity
---

**Background:** Blazar PKS 2131-021 exhibits periodic sinusoidal radio flux variations consistent with a supermassive black hole binary (SMBHB) model, but current models struggle to definitively constrain residual orbital eccentricity when stochastic red noise is present in the data.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains unresolved whether the SMBHB candidate PKS 2131-021 possesses a small, non-zero orbital eccentricity or is purely circular. Future work is required to distinguish eccentricity from potential stochastic red noise (e.g., Damped Random Walk processes) by extending the observational time baseline and incorporating additional multi-wavelength data to improve the precision of binary parameter estimation.

**Why It Matters:** Quantifying orbital eccentricity is critical for understanding the dynamical evolution, orbital decay, and gravitational wave emission characteristics of supermassive black hole binaries.

**Evidence:** When the analysis is refined to account for the presence of red noise in our data through a Damped Random Walk (DRW) process ... the circular orbital model emerges as statistically preferred, constraining the orbital eccentricity to an upper limit of e < 0.15.
