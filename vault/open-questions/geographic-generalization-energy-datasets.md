title: Globalizing Multimodal Energy Datasets
---

**Background:** Large language models (LLMs) and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) applications for the energy sector require datasets that are both multimodal and geographically diverse. While energy systems share common characteristics, national regulatory frameworks, data reporting standards, and grid operations vary significantly across global regions.

**Question / Future Work:** Current datasets are often geographically restricted. There is a need to extend existing multimodal energy datasets to include data from diverse global regulatory and operational contexts to ensure models are robust, representative, and accurate for international applications. Developing automated or scalable pipelines that can adapt to the different data structures and reporting formats found in non-European countries remains an unresolved challenge.

**Why It Matters:** Geographic bias in training data limits the generalizability and utility of AI decision-support tools in the energy sector, which is fundamentally a global industry with localized operational dependencies. Identifying methods to expand coverage while maintaining the integrity of multimodal relationships is essential for creating truly global energy knowledge bases.
