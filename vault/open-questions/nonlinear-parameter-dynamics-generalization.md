title: Generalizing to Nonlinear Dynamics
---

**Background:** Time-varying optimization often assumes the objective function's governing parameters evolve according to known dynamics, limiting applicability in uncertain real-time environments where parameters, dynamics, and measurements are all subject to unknown stochastic processes.

**Question / Future Work:** The current framework is restricted to linear stochastic dynamics of the underlying parameters. Generalizing these methods to nonlinear parameter dynamics—which frequently appear in complex real-world control systems—remains a significant open research challenge.

**Why It Matters:** Generalizing to nonlinear dynamics is crucial for expanding the applicability of these optimization frameworks to more complex, non-quadratic control tasks where linear assumptions fail.

**Evidence:** Future work includes extensions to constrained optimization, and generalizations to nonlinear parameter dynamics.
