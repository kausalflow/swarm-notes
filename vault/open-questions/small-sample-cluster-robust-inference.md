title: Robust inference for small clusters
---

**Background:** Small-sample studies with dependent data often lack reliable, model-free exact methods for constructing confidence intervals for complex performance metrics. Existing asymptotic methods for these metrics are generally valid only in large-sample regimes and may require finite-sample corrections when the number of independent clusters is limited.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an open need to develop robust inferential techniques for performance evaluation in studies with a very small number of independent clusters, where current large-sample asymptotic approximations fail. Investigating finite-sample corrections or resampling-based approaches such as cluster bootstrap is essential for improving reliability in data-constrained settings.

**Why It Matters:** In critical domains like clinical trials, small sample sizes are common, and incorrect uncertainty quantification leads to invalid statistical conclusions.
