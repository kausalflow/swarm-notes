title: Evaluation in Realistic Underwater Conditions
---

**Background:** Underwater acoustic channels are characterized by extreme temporal variability, Doppler shifts, and long propagation delays that complicate real-time channel state information acquisition.

**Question / Future Work:** Current deep learning-based channel prediction frameworks for underwater acoustic communication require rigorous validation in more challenging real-world scenarios, including at-sea conditions with severe Doppler effects and long multipath propagation delays, as well as implementation in real-time closed-loop experimental systems.

**Why It Matters:** The performance of predictive models is sensitive to the specific statistical properties of the acoustic channel, making validation in open-ocean conditions essential to confirm practical feasibility.

**Evidence:** Future work will extend validation to at-sea data with stronger Doppler and longer delays, and to real-time closed-loop experiments.
