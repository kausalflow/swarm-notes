title: GFM performance on regression tasks
---

**Background:** Geospatial Foundation Models (GFMs) often show inferior performance on fine-grained pixel-level regression tasks compared to task-specific models or standard supervised baselines.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains an open challenge to effectively adapt large-scale self-supervised Geospatial Foundation Models to complex, pixel-level regression tasks—such as continuous canopy height change—where fine-grained spatial and temporal sensitivity is critical. Future research is required to bridge the performance gap between these general-purpose foundation models and task-specific architectures currently utilized for high-resolution environmental monitoring.

**Why It Matters:** This is a foundational problem in the field of remote sensing, as the utility of large-scale foundation models is currently constrained by their inability to reliably handle high-precision regression tasks essential for ecological monitoring.
