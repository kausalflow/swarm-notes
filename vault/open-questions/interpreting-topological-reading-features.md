title: Interpreting Topological Reading Features
---

**Background:** Topological data analysis, specifically persistent homology, captures geometric and structural properties of time series that are distinct from traditional statistical features.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to bridge the gap between topological features extracted from fixation sequences and cognitive theories of reading, such as linguistic processing, oculomotor control, or phonological decoding, to improve the clinical interpretability of these models.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the gap between high-dimensional topological representations and cognitive mechanisms is critical for the clinical adoption and interpretability of these models.

**Evidence:** Future work should focus on... interpreting the topological features to better connect them to cognitive theories of reading and clinical practice.
