title: Empirical Pipeline with Constraints
---

**Background:** Portfolio selection procedures are often evaluated based on their performance in stylized backtests. Practical implementation requires addressing realistic market constraints that affect the viability of these procedures.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research should formally incorporate realistic financial constraints such as portfolio turnover, transaction costs, and liquidity limits into the MDS procedure to bridge the gap between theoretical asset selection and practical implementation.

**Why It Matters:** These considerations determine whether theoretical performance gains remain viable in actual trading environments.

**Evidence:** It is also important to extend the empirical pipeline to account for turnover, transaction costs, and liquidity constraints.
