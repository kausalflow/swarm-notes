title: Adaptive Oscillatory Alignment Scalability
---

**Background:** Time series forecasting often assumes recurring temporal patterns, which are typically modeled using static periodic templates or global spectral components. Real-world signals frequently exhibit non-stationary behaviors, including amplitude modulation, phase drift, and local frequency variation, that are not adequately captured by these rigid assumptions.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research should explore the scalability of Hilbert-guided oscillatory-state alignment in larger Transformer architectures and its applicability to probabilistic, multiresolution, and irregularly sampled time-series data. Additionally, investigating the integration of conditional priors (e.g., regime-dependent or covariate-aware priors) remains a significant open challenge.

**Why It Matters:** These questions define the operational limits of current Hilbert-guided forecasting and highlight necessary evolutions for handling more complex, non-stationary temporal dynamics in real-world applications.
