title: Mitigating VLM Forensic Hallucinations
---

**Background:** Vision-language models exhibit tendencies to generate plausible but factually incorrect explanations for visual content, a behavior known as hallucination. In high-precision tasks, these rationalizations can lead to performance degradation or incorrect forensic conclusions.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is required to develop techniques for quantifying and mitigating forensic hallucinations in multimodal reasoning models. Investigating whether these models can provide reliable, calibration-aware forensic evidence is a critical open challenge for deploying them in high-stakes verification scenarios.

**Why It Matters:** The Rationalization Trap represents a significant barrier to using VLMs for reliable decision-making where internal reasoning transparency and factual accuracy are mandatory.
