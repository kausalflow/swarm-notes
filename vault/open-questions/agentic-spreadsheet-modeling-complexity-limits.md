title: Agentic spreadsheet modeling complexity limits
---

**Background:** Financial modeling requires spreadsheets that are readable, modifiable, and maintainable for multi-stakeholder review. Existing benchmarks for LLM-based spreadsheet generation primarily focus on atomic operations or simple QA rather than complex, end-to-end multi-step modeling workflows.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved need for high-complexity, multi-part financial modeling tasks to stress-test the limits of agentic spreadsheet engineering. Future work should focus on expanding benchmarks to include tasks that require extensive multi-step planning, long-term reasoning, and adherence to professional financial structural standards.

**Why It Matters:** As agentic systems attempt to move from atomic formula generation to full financial modeling, identifying the ceiling of their architectural capabilities requires increasingly challenging, non-atomic benchmarks. Tracking progress on such tasks is essential for measuring reliable performance in high-stakes enterprise settings.
