title: Mechanistic interpretation of burst-merging kernels
---

**Background:** The burst-merging kernel is a mathematical construct used in burst-tree decomposition to quantify how different event clusters coalesce across various timescales, reflecting higher-order temporal correlations in event sequences.

**Question / Future Work:** While current cross-section analyses of the burst-merging kernel provide insight into temporal correlations, a more comprehensive understanding of the physical mechanisms behind these kernels remains unexplored. Future work is required to investigate the deeper physiological or mechanical processes that these kernel structures represent in heartbeat time series, particularly to determine why different heart conditions manifest as distinct kernel patterns.

**Why It Matters:** The burst-merging kernel is the fundamental object characterizing higher-order temporal correlations. Moving beyond descriptive statistics to understanding the generative physical processes captured by these kernels is critical for moving from classification toward mechanistic diagnosis of cardiovascular diseases.
