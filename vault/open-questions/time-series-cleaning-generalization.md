title: Generalization to Unseen Domains
---

**Background:** Automated data cleaning in multivariate time series often requires optimizing sequences of cleaning operators in the absence of ground truth. While hierarchical reinforcement learning systems have shown success in learning these strategies, their performance across diverse and unseen domains remains a significant bottleneck.

**Question / Future Work:** It is necessary to determine how hierarchical RL-based cleaning pipelines can generalize across fundamentally different underlying physical processes or data distributions without explicit domain adaptation or extensive retraining. Evaluating the robustness of these systems under significant distributional shift is critical for practical, domain-agnostic deployment.

**Why It Matters:** Generalization across domains is essential for real-world utility where labeling is expensive or impossible.
