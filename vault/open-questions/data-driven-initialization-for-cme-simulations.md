title: Data-Driven CME Simulation Initialization
---

**Background:** Current global CME modeling frameworks often initialize the magnetic field of active regions using idealized or synthetic magnetic flux distributions.

**Question / Future Work:** Future development of Sun-to-Earth CME modeling requires integrating high-resolution observational vector magnetograms directly into the initial boundary conditions to enable data-driven simulations. This transition is essential for enhancing the physical representativeness of the simulated eruption initiation, pre-eruption energy buildup, and subsequent propagation.

**Why It Matters:** The use of observed data is fundamental for moving from idealized physical experiments to operational forecasting tools that can accurately predict the behavior of specific, observed active regions.

**Evidence:** Furthermore, we aim to drive the AR evolution using observed vector magnetograms, enabling a data-driven MHD simulation of the initiation, evolution, and propagation of CMEs (Jiang et al., 2022). The ultimate goal is to develop a data-driven MHD modeling framework capable of predicting interplanetary space weather parameters and assessing the geoeffectiveness of Earth-directed CMEs.
