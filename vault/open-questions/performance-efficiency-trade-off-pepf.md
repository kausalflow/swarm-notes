title: TSFM vs Task-Specific Model Efficiency
---

**Background:** Probabilistic electricity price forecasting requires balancing accuracy in uncertainty quantification with computational efficiency and deployment constraints. There is currently no consensus on whether specialized machine learning models or general-purpose time series foundation models are superior for this task across varying market conditions.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed to establish formal criteria for choosing between task-specific deep learning models and foundation models for probabilistic electricity price forecasting. This involves investigating the optimal balance of computational overhead, model performance, and the capacity for cross-market few-shot adaptation in renewable-dominated power systems.

**Why It Matters:** As foundation models proliferate in time series, understanding when to favor specialized architectures versus general ones is a critical decision-making bottleneck for practitioners.
