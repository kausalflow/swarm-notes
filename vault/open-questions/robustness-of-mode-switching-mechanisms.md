title: Robustness of Mode Switching Mechanisms
---

**Background:** Human mobility often exhibits polycentric modularity, where individuals transition between local and long-range movement modes. Existing models often rely on simplistic or uniform assumptions that do not fully capture the influence of varying urban topographies or sudden societal changes on these transitions.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigating the adaptability of mode-switching mechanisms in mobility models when faced with significant non-stationary dynamics, such as urban development shifts, demographic heterogeneity, or external shocks like pandemics.

**Why It Matters:** Determining if structural mobility priors are robust to environmental and societal shifts is critical for epidemic forecasting and reliable urban planning.

**Evidence:** Future efforts could focus on refining the model to address specific scenarios or integrating it with complementary approaches to enhance its applicability.
