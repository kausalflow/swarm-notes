title: Extending TTV observational baselines
---

**Background:** The characterization of Transit Timing Variations (TTVs) in multi-planet systems requires extensive temporal coverage to adequately sample the long-term modulation induced by planetary interactions.

**Question / Future Work:** The current observational baseline for the TOI-5624 system, while sufficient to detect significant TTVs in planet e and suggest the presence of an outer perturbing planet f, is limited. Future photometric observations are necessary to accurately map the phase of the TTV super-period, reduce degeneracies between planetary mass and eccentricity, and confirm the dynamical connection between the observed transit-timing variations and the proposed outer companion.

**Why It Matters:** The accurate measurement of planetary masses in resonant or near-resonant systems relies heavily on the full sampling of TTV cycles; incomplete data leads to poorly constrained dynamical models.
