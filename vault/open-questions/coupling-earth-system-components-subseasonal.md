title: Coupling Earth-system components for subseasonal forecasting
---

**Background:** Data-driven weather models currently use atmospheric-only frameworks, missing essential interactions with the broader Earth system at sub-seasonal timescales.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to couple existing atmosphere-only data-driven forecasting models with land, surface ocean, and sea-ice components to enhance predictive skill for surface variables, particularly in tropical regions.

**Why It Matters:** Atmospheric coupling is a critical bottleneck for sub-seasonal skill, as surface variables depend heavily on long-memory components of the climate system which are currently treated only as boundary conditions.

**Evidence:** AIFS-SUBS forecasts the atmosphere alone. At sub-seasonal lead times, however, much of the predictability arises from the slowly varying boundary conditions such as the land, surface ocean and sea ice. Coupling AIFS-SUBS to land and ocean components is therefore a natural next step.
