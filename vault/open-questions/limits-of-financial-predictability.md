title: Limits of Financial Predictability
---

**Background:** Financial time series reasoning tasks involve both deterministic assessment of current market states and stochastic prediction of future market behavior, which are influenced by unobservable factors.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the theoretical ceiling of model performance is critical for distinguishing between improvements in reasoning capabilities versus the fundamental limits of signal availability in noisy financial datasets.

**Why It Matters:** Fundamental for understanding whether models are optimizing reasoning or merely chasing irreducible noise in financial time series.

**Evidence:** Even with a perfect model, prediction accuracy cannot reach 100% because the necessary information is not contained in the price history alone.
