title: Demonstrating End-to-End QIML Advantage
---

**Background:** The practical quantum advantage of quantum-informed machine learning (QIML) relies on efficiently representing invariant measures of chaotic systems and reading out statistical functionals. Future progress in this architecture requires evaluating performance against state-of-the-art classical pipelines at scale and implementing high-fidelity hardware extraction using error mitigation.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to demonstrate an end-to-end wall-clock advantage of the QIML architecture against the strongest classical machine-learning pipelines for forecasting chaotic systems. This involves not only scaling the quantum hardware to support higher-order Q-Priors but also integrating advanced error-mitigation techniques on near-term or fault-tolerant hardware to ensure the fidelity of the extracted statistical functionals in complex, real-world scientific workflows.

**Why It Matters:** This is the primary bottleneck to transitioning from a theoretical/simulation-based advantage to a verified practical utility in real-world scientific applications.
