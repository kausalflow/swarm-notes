title: Causal mechanisms of plasticity
---

**Background:** Understanding the drivers of neural network plasticity is essential for improving performance across long-term sequential learning tasks. Current empirical plasticity measures often lack causal grounding, leaving the mechanism behind observed correlations with downstream adaptability unclear.

**Question / Future Work:** A controlled investigation is needed to determine whether local redundancy has a causal influence on downstream model adaptability, potentially through interventions like parameter perturbation or regularization. This would distinguish whether local redundancy is a fundamental driver of plasticity or simply an emergent correlate of the parameter state.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing causality is necessary to move beyond descriptive heuristics in the study of neural plasticity.

**Evidence:** Our results establish that local redundancy predicts downstream performance, but the lower bound does not establish that it causes it.
