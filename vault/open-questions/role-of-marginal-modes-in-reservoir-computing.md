title: Role of Marginal Modes in Reservoir Computing
---

**Background:** Reservoir computing architectures often rely on the edge-of-chaos heuristic for hyperparameter tuning, yet standard Lyapunov exponents fail to fully explain forecasting performance optimization.

**Question / Future Work:** The precise functional role of marginal Lyapunov modes located near the top of the spectrum remains unclear, particularly regarding how they contribute to generating or sustaining the strong finite-time fluctuations observed in deeper stable modes of teacher-forced recurrent neural networks.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding marginal modes is critical for connecting microscopic network stability criteria to macroscopic reservoir forecasting capabilities and refining stability-expressivity measures.

**Evidence:** The role of the marginal modes near the top of the Lyapunov spectrum is not yet understood. One possibility is that these modes help generate or sustain the strong finite-time fluctuations observed in the deeper stable modes, but this conjecture requires a careful examination.
