title: Capturing abrupt climate policy shifts
---

**Background:** Integrated Assessment Model (IAM) climate scenarios are characterized by high-dimensional time series data, which often necessitates the use of simplified metrics to facilitate comparison and analysis of their underlying mitigation pathways.

**Question / Future Work:** The existing decarbonization speed metric, which relies on a smooth parametric function to capture long-term trends, is inherently limited in its ability to represent abrupt policy shifts or sudden adjustments in transition efforts, such as those found in delayed-transition scenarios. Future research is required to develop more refined metrics or extensions to the current framework that can explicitly capture and quantify these transient or discontinuous dynamics within climate scenarios.

**Why It Matters:** As transition policy analysis increasingly focuses on the economic and financial implications of delayed or abrupt policy changes, the inability to capture these features limits the utility of current summary metrics in risk assessment and transition modeling.
