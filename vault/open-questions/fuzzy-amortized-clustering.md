title: Fuzzy Amortized Neural Clustering
---

**Background:** Amortized neural inference approaches for clustering often face limitations when modeling partial membership or overlapping cluster structures.

**Question / Future Work:** The current framework primarily focuses on hard, disjoint clustering partitions through pairwise affinity learning. Future work is needed to extend this methodology to fuzzy clustering, where time series can possess fractional membership degrees across multiple clusters, better capturing the complex, overlapping temporal patterns frequently encountered in real-world applications like regime-switching series.

**Why It Matters:** Extending the framework to fuzzy clustering would significantly increase its applicability to complex real-world datasets where series boundaries are not clearly defined, providing a more nuanced and accurate representation of temporal dynamics than crisp assignment.
