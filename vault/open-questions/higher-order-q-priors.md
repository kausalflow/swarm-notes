title: Exploring Higher-Order Q-Priors
---

**Background:** Higher-order quantum statistical priors (Q-Priors) are capable of capturing multi-point spatial correlations of invariant measures beyond the pairwise covariances used in existing case studies. Exploring these higher-order structures allows for more sophisticated constraints in downstream scientific models.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work should investigate the use of higher-order (k >= 3) Q-Priors to capture more complex multi-site correlations in chaotic dynamical systems. This includes identifying specific 'physically named' observables, such as helical polyspectra or other high-order phase invariants, that can be efficiently read out using the described Bell-measurement primitive to provide superior stability for classical models in long-horizon rollout scenarios.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the representational limits of current pairwise (k=2) models and provides a path to leveraging the full expressive power of the quantum Hilbert space for complex physical systems.
