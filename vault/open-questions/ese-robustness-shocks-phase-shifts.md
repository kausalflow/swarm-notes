title: Robustness under shocks and phase-shifts
---

**Background:** Equilibrium state estimation methods rely on consistent attribute data to align target variables within an ensemble of multiple interacting systems. The stability and predictive reliability of this approach when systems exhibit heterogeneous local shocks or significant temporal phase differences remain to be fully characterized.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is needed to systematically analyze the robustness of Equilibrium State Estimation (ESE) under conditions of high temporal misalignment (phase differences) and persistent heterogeneous local shocks, as these factors may disrupt the underlying statistical equilibrium assumptions and degrade cross-system predictive accuracy.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the limits of robustness under non-stationary or phase-shifted conditions is essential for extending ESE to diverse real-world systems where synchronization and shock propagation are common.
