title: EWS for high-dimensional systems
---

**Background:** Early-warning signals (EWS) for bifurcation-induced critical transitions, such as critical slowing down, are often limited by a lack of interpretability and unknown generalization to high-dimensional systems. Existing statistical measures frequently require an ensemble of trajectories or a baseline value to be meaningful, which is rarely feasible in real-world monitoring.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to determine the reliability and performance of existing temporal, spatial, and spatio-temporal EWS in truly high-dimensional systems, as it remains unclear how aggregate metrics or one-dimensional observables scale across these complex settings. Additionally, while the proposed modified escape EWS shows promise, the current reconstruction from empirical samples is subject to higher uncertainties than traditional statistical moments, necessitating refinements to the maximum likelihood estimation framework.

**Why It Matters:** Predicting critical transitions in high-dimensional systems is a fundamental challenge in complex systems research; addressing the bottleneck of interpretability and indicator reliability is essential for practical deployment.

**Evidence:** It is not clear at this stage how reliable traditional temporal, spatial and spatio-temporal indicators should perform in generic high-dimensional systems [Robinson2025]. In this paper we obtain evidence that applications of the proposed methodology to one-dimensional reductions of higher-dimensional systems can yield confident results... Further investigation in improving the accuracy of the technique is required however, as the reconstructed signal from empirical samples seem to be subject to higher uncertanties than the statistical moments counterparts.
