title: Extending FMCA to Complex-Valued Domains
---

**Background:** Functional maximal correlation algorithm (FMCA) frameworks are typically implemented using real-valued signals to estimate cross density ratios and construct multivariate feature spaces.

**Question / Future Work:** Extending FMCA frameworks to the complex-valued domain is necessary to improve spectral information processing and potentially enhance performance in tasks sensitive to phase and magnitude information.

**Why It Matters:** Expanding to complex-valued processing allows for a more comprehensive utilization of signal information, which is critical for non-stationary signals where phase and spectral characteristics are fundamentally intertwined.

**Evidence:** As future work, we plan to extend the framework to the complex-valued domain for improved spectral information processing.
