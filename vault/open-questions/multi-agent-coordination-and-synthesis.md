title: Coordinating Multi-Expert Forecasts
---

**Background:** Event forecasting often requires aggregating diverse and conflicting perspectives from specialized knowledge sources. Current methods for combining these outputs often lack mechanisms to handle disagreements and synthesize nuanced judgments.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop robust coordination mechanisms that can synthesize outputs from multiple, heterogeneous expert agents. Research is required into how systems can effectively compare and reconcile expert disagreements by evaluating the evidence, reliability, and domain coverage of each agent rather than relying on simple ensemble techniques.

**Why It Matters:** Without effective coordination, the potential benefits of utilizing specialized agents are undermined, which is a critical bottleneck for high-stakes forecasting.
