title: Observation-dependent MSMs identification
---

**Background:** Markov Switching Models (MSMs) typically assume that latent regime transitions are independent of observed variables.

**Question / Future Work:** The current identifiability theory for MSMs generally requires the latent regime process to be independent of the observed data. Extending identifiability results to systems where observations influence regime transitions—which is common in many physical and economic feedback loops—remains a major open challenge.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental limitation in the applicability of MSMs to many physical and economic systems where observation-driven regime transitions are common.

**Evidence:** For example, our identifiability theory requires that the regime variable $R_t$ is unaffected by the observations $\bm{X}_{0:T}$.
