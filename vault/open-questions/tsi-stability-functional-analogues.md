title: Stability and Functional Analogues
---

**Background:** The Topological Stability Index (TSI) and its normalized counterpart are scalar summaries of persistence barcodes that measure the dispersion of lifetime values, contrasting with persistent entropy which focuses on relative lifetime distributions.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop more stable and robust versions of the TSI or to devise formal Lipschitz-type stability bounds, as these indices currently lack continuity under the insertion of arbitrarily short bars. Furthermore, exploring time-resolved or functional analogues, such as a time-dependent TSI within the persistence-curve framework, remains a critical area for improving stability under local perturbations.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing stability properties is fundamental for the reliability of summary statistics in topological data analysis, as it dictates the sensitivity to noise and sampling variability.

**Evidence:** In particular, no Lipschitz-type stability bound... can hold. ... The persistence-curve framework suggests a natural functional analogue of the TSI ... we leave this direction for future work.
