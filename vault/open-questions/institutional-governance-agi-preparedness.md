title: Institutional Governance for AGI Preparedness
---

**Background:** The potential for rapid development of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) poses fundamental, unresolved risks to existing social, economic, and institutional structures. Policy frameworks currently struggle to balance the need for rapid adaptation with the preservation of democratic accountability and institutional stability.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining necessary institutional adaptations, decision-making speed triggers, and contingency planning to address the potential for AGI-enabled economic disruption, security threats, and political disempowerment.

**Why It Matters:** Failure to adapt governance mechanisms to the pace of AGI could lead to institutional paralysis, loss of democratic control over critical infrastructure, or irreversible societal disruption.
