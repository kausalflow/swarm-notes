title: Optimal social interaction clustering granularity
---

**Background:** Pedestrian trajectory prediction models often treat social interactions as a 'black box' by pooling neighbor information without distinguishing between different behavioral styles.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the optimal number of latent clusters required to represent the full spectrum of socially meaningful human interaction patterns remains an open challenge, as current methods typically rely on a pre-defined number of categories. Future work is needed to systematically explore how the granularity of these clusters affects the trade-off between model interpretability and predictive accuracy.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding whether a fixed number of interaction modes is sufficient or if a hierarchical/adaptive approach is necessary is critical for the scalability and robustness of social navigation systems in diverse environments.

**Evidence:** We are also going to explore the number of clusters, i.e. the social interaction modes.
