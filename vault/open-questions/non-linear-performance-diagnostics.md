title: Non-linear Multi-modal Performance Diagnostics
---

**Background:** Canonical correlation is often used as a linear heuristic to gauge the potential efficacy of multi-modal surrogate models, yet it fails to account for non-linear relationships between modalities.

**Question / Future Work:** Establishing a method to quantitatively measure non-linear relationships between data modalities is required to better predict a priori whether a multi-modal surrogate model will outperform a uni-modal alternative. Traditional metrics like linear canonical correlation and mutual information are either insufficient or theoretically problematic for this purpose in this context.

**Why It Matters:** Predictive diagnostics for multi-modal model performance are critical for practitioners to decide whether the overhead of multi-modal data collection and modeling is justified in a specific application.

**Evidence:** A method that measures non-linear relationships in order to determine a priori the performance of these multi-modal models is left for future work.
