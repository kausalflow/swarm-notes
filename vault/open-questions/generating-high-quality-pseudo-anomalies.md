title: Generating High-Quality Pseudo Anomalies
---

**Background:** Open-set anomaly detection often relies on synthetic pseudo-anomalies to represent unseen failure modes, but heuristic generation methods frequently fail to capture the full distributional breadth of novel anomalies.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an urgent need to transition from heuristic, rule-based anomaly generation to theoretically grounded or adaptive synthesis methods that can produce diverse, semantically meaningful anomalies without overfitting to seen training samples.

**Why It Matters:** The quality and diversity of pseudo-anomalies determine the generalization capability of open-set models to unseen failure modes.

**Evidence:** these methods predominantly rely on heuristic rules and pre-defined priors to synthesize anomalies, which inherently limit the generated pseudo anomalies to variations of seen patterns
