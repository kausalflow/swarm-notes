title: Standardized Biological TSC Benchmarks
---

**Background:** Biological signal classification datasets often lack temporal alignment, standardization, or representative balance, leading to fragmented evaluation metrics across clinical and behavioral domains.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need for community-driven benchmarks for biological time-series classification that prioritize structural morphological diversity (e.g., spikes vs. drifts) across modalities, incorporating standardized preprocessing and common nuisance conditions like sensor artifacts and baseline drift.

**Why It Matters:** Without such standards, it is impossible to evaluate whether model improvements are due to algorithmic innovation or idiosyncratic dataset quirks.
