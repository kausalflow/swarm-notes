title: Mitigating optical feature overlap
---

**Background:** Optical variability, characterized by statistical features derived from time-series, is commonly used to classify active galactic nuclei (AGN) into blazars and non-blazars.

**Question / Future Work:** The performance of blazar classification models is often limited by the similarity between the optical variability signatures of blazars and those of other active galactic nuclei. Future research must determine how to resolve this feature overlap, particularly if constrained to optical light curve data or by strategically incorporating multi-band observations.

**Why It Matters:** Distinguishing blazar signals from other AGN variability is a fundamental astrophysical classification bottleneck.

**Evidence:** Future works could enhance accuracy by integrating multiband data, such as colors and spectral features, to mitigate feature overlap.
