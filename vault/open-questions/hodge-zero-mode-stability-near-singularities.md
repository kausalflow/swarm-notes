title: Stability near singular regimes
---

**Background:** The zero-mode bundle construction relies on the spectral gap between the zero eigenvalue and the nonzero spectrum of the Hodge Laplacian. Near the singular set, this spectral gap can close, which challenges the stability of the smooth bundle framework.

**Question / Future Work:** While the proposed geometric descriptors like curvature and holonomy are stably computable on regular regions, they potentially become unstable near points where the spectral gap closes or the Betti number changes. Developing a more robust framework that remains meaningful in these singular regions—perhaps by incorporating information from low-energy modes or using alternative spectral regularizations—remains an unresolved challenge.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical for the practical applicability of the framework to complex, real-world data, where topological transitions and singular regimes are common.

**Evidence:** Near the singular set, however, the uniform spectral gap may close, and the stability estimates may break down.
