title: Validating LDT Asymptotics in Climate Systems
---

**Background:** Large deviation theory (LDT) provides a framework for analyzing rare events, but its application to complex, non-ergodic climate systems often necessitates empirical validation of the underlying asymptotic regimes. The determination of whether a system has reached an asymptotic state where the large deviation principle holds remains a significant practical challenge in climate research.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate the validity of the large deviation principle and the convergence of rate functions in climate dynamics, specifically focusing on the trade-offs between block length, autocorrelation time scales, and system mixing properties to ensure reliable return time estimation.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental methodological requirement for applying LDT to complex, non-stationary climate systems, as failing to establish the asymptotic regime can lead to mathematically unsound return-time predictions.
