title: Beyond direct-mapping forecasting
---

**Background:** Time series forecasting models often rely on a 'Direct-Mapping' paradigm, where a unified function is learned from historical observations to future values. This approach frequently struggles with response lag and error accumulation during system regime shifts or distribution changes.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing architectures that move beyond static direct-mapping to capture finer-grained or multi-scale dynamic shifts remains a critical challenge for reliable forecasting in non-stationary real-world environments. Future research must address how to effectively decouple long-term regime evolution from short-term value fluctuations without incurring excessive computational or structural complexity.

**Why It Matters:** It frames the transition from direct-mapping to change-aware modeling as a fundamental research bottleneck in time-series forecasting.

**Evidence:** Future work will explore finer-grained change modeling and improved robustness in broader real-world settings.
