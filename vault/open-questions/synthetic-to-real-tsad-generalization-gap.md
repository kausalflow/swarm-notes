title: Synthetic to Real TSAD Generalization
---

**Background:** Vision-Language Models (VLMs) have been increasingly adapted for time series anomaly detection by treating temporal sequences as visual inputs.

**Question / Future Work:** A critical challenge remains in closing the performance gap between models trained on algorithmically generated synthetic data and their real-world application, where complex noise, drift, and non-canonical anomaly patterns exist. Identifying robust transfer mechanisms or hybrid training strategies to ensure high-fidelity performance on non-synthetic data is an essential unresolved research bottleneck.

**Why It Matters:** Relying on synthetic training data is a common strategy to circumvent data scarcity, but verifying and bridging the gap to real-world deployment is essential for reliable anomaly detection.
