title: Long-term effects of fact-checks
---

**Background:** Community-based fact-checking systems on social media are designed to provide context to potentially misleading information through the aggregated judgments of non-expert users. While these systems effectively reduce engagement with flagged content, their ability to influence the long-term social reputation or follower retention of the content creators remains limited.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work should address the lack of long-term longitudinal data to determine if repeated exposure to community fact-checks eventually erodes audience loyalty. Additionally, there is a need for research using network-level data to identify specific user segments that are susceptible to changing their affiliation based on fact-checking, as aggregate metrics currently suggest minimal impact on follower counts.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding whether fact-checking has a cumulative effect is critical for evaluating the long-term utility of community-moderation systems in reducing the prevalence of misinformation.

**Evidence:** Second, our 21-day observation window captures short-term effects but may miss longer-term user responses. Extending the timeframe could help evaluate whether repeated fact-checks gradually erode audience loyalty.
