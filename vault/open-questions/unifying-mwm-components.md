title: Unified Mechanistic World Models
---

**Background:** Mechanistic World Models (MWMs) seek to bridge the gap between predictive forecasting and explanatory scientific discovery by organizing knowledge around reusable mechanisms rather than predictive mappings alone. While individual components such as variable, mechanism, and structure discovery have seen progress, no existing framework integrates these into a unified, scalable system.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to unify variable, mechanism, and structure discovery into a single, cohesive framework that satisfies the dual inductive pressures of parsimony and compositionality. A primary unresolved challenge is how to jointly optimize these components while maintaining scalability and addressing theoretical issues like identifiability, particularly in non-Markovian or partially observable environments.

**Why It Matters:** This question is central to the proposed paradigm, as MWMs rely on the synergy of these components to move AI from mere forecasting to autonomous scientific discovery. Without a unified integration, the benefits of interpretability, compositional generalization, and targeted adaptation cannot be realized.
