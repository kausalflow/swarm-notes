title: Calibrating Participation Response Probabilities
---

**Background:** The effectiveness of targeted resource allocation in public health networks depends on the reliability of estimates regarding the participation behavior of individuals following a personal intervention. Current operational models for scheduling often rely on assumed attendance probabilities rather than probabilities calibrated directly from outreach data.

**Question / Future Work:** Empirical research is required to estimate participation probabilities following personal invitations across different demographics and intervention settings to better calibrate planning models and optimize resource usage.

**Why It Matters:** The accuracy of decision support frameworks hinges on the quality of its inputs; without calibrated response probabilities, the model risks significant efficiency loss or unmet demand.
