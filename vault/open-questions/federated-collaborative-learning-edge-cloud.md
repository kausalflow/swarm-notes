title: Collaborative learning for edge-cloud
---

**Background:** The computing continuum paradigm seeks to optimize workload placement across distributed edge and cloud resources to manage trade-offs between latency, energy, and reliability. Current decentralized reinforcement learning approaches for this problem often struggle with balancing these conflicting objectives without centralized coordination or global system state knowledge.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is needed to extend current decentralized reinforcement learning frameworks to support federated or collaborative learning mechanisms. This would enable distributed agents to share knowledge more effectively while maintaining system scalability and data privacy, which is currently a limiting factor in maintaining high-performance policies across large-scale edge-cloud infrastructures.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing the balance between decentralized autonomy and collaborative intelligence is critical for the scalability and robustness of large-scale edge computing systems.

**Evidence:** First, future studies may directly investigate federated or collaborative learning mechanisms to allow distributed agents to share knowledge while preserving system scalability and privacy.
