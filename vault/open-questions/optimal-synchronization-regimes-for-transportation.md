title: Optimal synchronization for transportation
---

**Background:** Elevator and transportation systems often exhibit synchronized arrival times, which can be modeled as emergent phenomena from stochastic agent-based interactions. The efficiency of these synchronization states—whether in-phase or anti-phase—varies depending on external demand patterns.

**Question / Future Work:** Determine the conditions under which specific synchronization regimes (in-phase vs. anti-phase) maximize passenger throughput and minimize wait times, and investigate how these objectives shift under realistic, non-stationary passenger demand.

**Why It Matters:** Directly informs the design of smart transportation infrastructure by moving beyond mere observation of synchronization toward purposeful, performance-oriented control.
