title: Higher-Dimensional Phase Reduction Validation
---

**Background:** The development of generalized-phase dynamics for higher-dimensional oscillator systems has been limited by the requirement for strong orbital stability and specific phase-rotation properties. While current models validate the circle-map approach for two-dimensional oscillators, extending this framework to more complex, higher-dimensional dynamics remains an unresolved task.

**Question / Future Work:** The authors identify that formalizing the extension of their circle-map framework to higher-dimensional systems, where dynamics settle in the vicinity of a limit cycle with sufficient orbital stability, is a necessary direction for future research.

**Why It Matters:** Generalizing phase reduction methods to high-dimensional systems is a persistent bottleneck for modeling complex phenomena in physics and biology.

**Evidence:** In this study, we considered only two-dimensional oscillator systems in our numerical experiments... the same circle-map description is expected to hold for higher-dimensional systems... Applying the proposed method to such systems therefore constitutes an important direction for future research.
