title: Handling Unreliable Temporal Data Corruptions
---

**Background:** Sensor data from industrial cyber-physical systems often suffers from corruptions that affect both row ordering and timestamping simultaneously.

**Question / Future Work:** Automated detection and correction of time-series data corruptions where both sequence ordering and timestamps are unreliable remains a significant challenge. Differentiating between sensor-induced artifacts and genuine anomalies caused by machine behavior is critical yet currently underspecified.

**Why It Matters:** Distinguishing between noise and genuine physical anomalies is a fundamental prerequisite for robust temporal forecasting and fault detection.
