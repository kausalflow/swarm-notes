title: Integration of Broader Data Modalities
---

**Background:** Current multimodal glucose forecasting models for type 1 diabetes primarily utilize continuous glucose monitoring, insulin, and carbohydrate data. Physiological data beyond these modalities are often recorded only intermittently or are unavailable in standardized formats.

**Question / Future Work:** The inclusion of broader physiological, behavioral, and contextual data (e.g., physical activity, stress, or other wearable-derived signals) remains an unresolved challenge for enhancing predictive accuracy. Future research should investigate the utility of these heterogeneous data streams when integrated into existing standardized, multimodal frameworks.

**Why It Matters:** Expanding the feature set beyond simple insulin and carbohydrate logs is necessary to account for the complex physiological factors that drive glycemic variability in real-world environments.

**Evidence:** Behavioral, physiological (e.g., wearable-derived), and other data types are known to impact glucose levels over time, but they are frequently unavailable or recorded intermittently.
