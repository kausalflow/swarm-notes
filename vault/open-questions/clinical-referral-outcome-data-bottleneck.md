title: Clinical Referral Outcome Data Bottleneck
---

**Background:** Clinical referral decisions represent complex, multi-level processes dependent on patient risk, clinical reasoning, and local healthcare resources. Current medical datasets are often limited to primary outcomes like mortality and lack explicit labels for referral pathways and decisions.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need for high-quality, large-scale datasets that explicitly link multimodal clinical longitudinal records and sensor streams to annotated community-to-tertiary referral ground truths, facilitating the development of systems that optimize care flow rather than just clinical prognosis.

**Why It Matters:** Without explicit ground-truth referral labels, it remains difficult to train and validate AI systems that act effectively as workflow-aware assistants in clinical care coordination.

**Evidence:** The main remaining limitation is that direct community-to-tertiary referral labels are uncommon in public datasets.
