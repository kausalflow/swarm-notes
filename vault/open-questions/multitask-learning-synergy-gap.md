title: Multitask Learning Synergy Gap
---

**Background:** Multitask learning in remote sensing aims to solve multiple geographical tasks simultaneously, but frequently experiences performance trade-offs or lack of synergy compared to single-task models.

**Question / Future Work:** Jointly training models for tasks like land cover and crop-type mapping often fails to yield performance gains in current remote sensing architectures. Research is required to understand the conditions under which these tasks can benefit from shared features and to develop tailored architectures that successfully exploit multitask synergies rather than competing for representational capacity.

**Why It Matters:** Multitask learning is essential for efficient, large-scale deployment of geospatial AI models, and identifying why it fails in current remote sensing setups is critical for progress.
