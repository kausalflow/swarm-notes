title: Interaction-Aware Sentiment Forecasting
---

**Background:** Social media sentiment forecasting often treats posts as independent entities, discarding the conversational topology that links them. Existing approaches largely ignore how reply and repost structures shape collective dynamics.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop forecasting models that effectively integrate the conversational topology (e.g., reply/repost graphs) of social media alongside temporal signals. Future research should focus on architectures that treat the interaction graph as a core component rather than an exogenous side channel to improve forecasting during high-interaction periods.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the predictive influence of social interaction structures on collective opinion dynamics is essential for improving forecasting accuracy in volatile, high-interaction social media environments.
