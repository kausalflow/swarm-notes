title: Hardware validation of optical forecasting
---

**Background:** Diffractive optical forecasting models currently rely on digital simulators such as TorchOptics for performance verification.

**Question / Future Work:** The transition from simulation to physical implementation remains a critical challenge. Future research must address hardware-level issues such as phase mask fabrication precision, aperture alignment, optical noise, thermal drift, and calibration, which are currently abstracted away in software-based forecasting experiments.

**Why It Matters:** Validating the physical realization is essential to proving the viability of passive optics as a substitute for digital sequence mixing layers in time-series forecasting.

**Evidence:** The present results are simulation-based and do not claim experimental hardware validation.
