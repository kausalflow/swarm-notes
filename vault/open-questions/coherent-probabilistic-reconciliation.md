title: Coherent joint predictive distributions
---

**Background:** Forecast reconciliation is traditionally focused on point forecasts, which satisfy aggregation constraints but do not inherently provide coherent joint predictive distributions.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to extend the point-forecast reconciliation framework to generate coherent joint predictive distributions, reconciling the entire probability distributions of the forecasts across all levels of the hierarchy and variables simultaneously.

**Why It Matters:** Probabilistic coherence is essential for risk management, decision-making under uncertainty, and generating reliable confidence intervals across hierarchical structures.

**Evidence:** Beyond point forecasts, extending the framework to produce coherent joint predictive distributions would be a valuable next step.
