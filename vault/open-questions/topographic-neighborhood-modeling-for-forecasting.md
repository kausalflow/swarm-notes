title: Topographic neighborhood modeling
---

**Background:** Small-area precipitation forecasting in complex terrain is constrained by the failure of Euclidean-based neighbor definitions to account for orographic divides and flow dynamics.

**Question / Future Work:** Replacing Euclidean or great-circle distance with topographic or flow-path-based connectivity is a significant architectural challenge for improving the stability and accuracy of spatiotemporal models in complex landscapes.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the gap between spatial representation and physical landscape topology is critical for moving beyond simplistic distance-based graph constructions in climate and hydrological forecasting.

**Evidence:** A graph-based neighbourhood informed by terrain-weighted proximity — replacing great-circle distance with effective flow-path distance derived from a digital elevation model (DEM) — is the natural extension.
