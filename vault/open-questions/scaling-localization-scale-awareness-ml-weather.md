title: Scaling and Localization in ML Weather Forecasting
---

**Background:** Machine-learned probabilistic weather forecast models utilize scoring rules like the Continuous Ranked Probability Score (CRPS) and multivariate alternatives as training loss functions, but the interaction between scale awareness, localization strategies, model architecture, and resolution remains insufficiently understood.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is needed to comprehensively understand how the performance and physical realism of machine-learned weather forecast models depend on localization strategies, scale awareness mechanisms, and model architecture, particularly at higher spatial resolutions where maintaining spatial coherence becomes increasingly challenging.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the interplay between scale awareness, localization, architecture, and resolution is critical for scaling data-driven weather forecasting systems to higher grid resolutions without degrading spatial coherence or physical realism.

**Evidence:** Overall, multivariate scoring rules are a promising direction for generative modelling, but further research is needed to better understand how results depend on localization, scale awareness and model architecture.
