title: Community-Scale Disease Benchmarks
---

**Background:** Fine-grained, localized disease data is crucial for targeted public health interventions, yet such data is highly sensitive and often characterized by sparsity and noise at the community level.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved need to develop and validate privacy-preserving federated learning models using fine-grained, community-level disease dynamics, necessitating the creation of realistic, synthetic privacy-compliant datasets that preserve the structural patterns of infection spread for robust benchmarking.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing benchmarks for community-scale forecasting is vital for determining the scalability and real-world utility of federated learning in decentralized public health management.
