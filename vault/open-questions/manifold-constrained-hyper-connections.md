title: Manifold-constrained hyper-connections
---

**Background:** Residual connections in deep neural networks are often used to facilitate gradient flow, but their design can lead to trade-offs between stability and representational diversity, commonly referred to as the seesaw effect.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work should investigate manifold-constrained hyper-connections (mHC) to better regulate signal flow and identity propagation, which may otherwise be disrupted by unconstrained mapping in deep architectures.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the trade-off between expressive capacity and training stability, which is central to scaling transformer architectures.

**Evidence:** Unconstrained hyper-connections in residual mappings may disrupt identity propagation... Exploring manifold-constrained hyper-connections (mHC) offers a potential direction.
