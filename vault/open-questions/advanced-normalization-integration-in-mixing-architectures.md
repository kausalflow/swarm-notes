title: Advanced Normalization in Mixing Architectures
---

**Background:** Normalization is a critical preprocessing step in time series forecasting to address distribution shift, with techniques like Reversible Instance Normalization (RevIN) being widely used for its effectiveness in stabilizing training.

**Question / Future Work:** The effectiveness of integrating more advanced normalization techniques into multi-scale mixing architectures remains to be evaluated to determine if they offer further performance gains over standard methods like RevIN.

**Why It Matters:** Normalization strategies directly impact the model's ability to handle non-stationarity, which is a major bottleneck in long-term forecasting; exploring more adaptive methods could improve robustness across diverse datasets.
