title: Low-Frequency Pulsar Profile Deconvolution
---

**Background:** Pulsar profiles at low radio frequencies frequently evolve into complex multi-component structures or exhibit significant scatter broadening due to the interstellar medium.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing robust deconvolution-based methods to accurately estimate pulse broadening functions (PBFs) and determine intrinsic pulse shapes remains a significant challenge. Future work is required to standardize these methods and investigate the frequency dependence of PBFs, particularly to understand the impact of inner scale effects on multipath propagation.

**Why It Matters:** Accurate deconvolution of scatter broadening is essential for meaningful comparisons of pulse widths across different radio frequencies and for mapping the spatial distribution of interstellar turbulence.

**Evidence:** As well known, at low radio frequencies, many pulsar profiles tend to evolve to complex multi-component shapes... As the deconvolution-based methods make no assumption of the intrinsic pulse shapes, they are likely to yield more reliable estimates of pulse broadening times.
