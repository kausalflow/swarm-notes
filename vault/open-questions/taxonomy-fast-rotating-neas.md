title: Taxonomy of Fast-Rotating NEAs
---

**Background:** The spectral-type distribution and physical properties of small near-Earth asteroids (NEAs) with diameters smaller than 100 meters, particularly sub-minute rotators, remain sparsely constrained due to observational challenges.

**Question / Future Work:** Expanding the sample of tiny, sub-minute rotators is necessary to robustly constrain taxonomic complex trends, internal structural properties (e.g., monolithic vs. rubble-pile), and dynamical history relative to the cohesionless spin barrier.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding these trends is vital for planetary defense and characterizing the physical limits of small solar system bodies.
