title: Meaning-Preserving Time-Series Augmentation Balance
---

**Background:** Data-driven augmentation strategies for physiological time-series data are essential for training robust models, as traditional heuristic transformations often fail to preserve the underlying physiological manifold.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the optimal balance between perturbation intensity and semantic preservation when using generative models (like diffusion) for time-series augmentation remains an open research challenge.

**Why It Matters:** This is a critical bottleneck for ensuring that synthetic augmentations remain faithful to the original data's diagnostic or clinical meaning.

**Evidence:** the development of meaning-preserving transformations for physiological time-series data remains a significant and unresolved challenge.
