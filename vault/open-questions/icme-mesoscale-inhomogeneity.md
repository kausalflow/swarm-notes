title: ICME Mesoscale Magnetic Inhomogeneity
---

**Background:** Interplanetary Coronal Mass Ejections (ICMEs) often exhibit mesoscale magnetic field inhomogeneities that diverge from idealized, coherent flux-rope models when sampled by multiple spacecraft.

**Question / Future Work:** The nature and cause of mesoscale structural inhomogeneities within ICME magnetic ejecta remain incompletely understood. Future investigations are needed to determine whether these variations result from large-scale geometrical distortions of the flux rope (such as writhed or curved axes), interactions with the ambient solar wind, or localized magnetic erosion processes. Resolving these factors is essential for improving the accuracy of space-weather forecasting and fundamental heliophysics models of magnetic ejecta.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding these inhomogeneities is critical because idealized flux-rope models often fail to capture the complex, non-coherent features that directly influence the geoeffectiveness of ICMEs.

**Evidence:** These results suggest that the ME has a non-coherent magnetic structure at mesoscales across its angular extent.
