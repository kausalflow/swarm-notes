title: Robustness to Memristor Variability
---

**Background:** Volatile memristors are essential for implementing reservoir computing in hardware, but their stochastic device-to-device and cycle-to-cycle variability challenges system performance and reliability.

**Question / Future Work:** Establishing formal 'design-for-robustness' guidelines that predict and mitigate the effects of physical device non-idealities on reservoir dynamics is necessary to move beyond empirical observations and area-intensive calibration strategies.

**Why It Matters:** Reliability under hardware noise is the primary barrier to the commercial adoption of memristor-based neuromorphic computing.

**Evidence:** Although RC has been demonstrated with several memristive devices, a comprehensive evaluation of device-level requirements remains limited... robust operation... robustness to high variability is highly architecture-dependent.
