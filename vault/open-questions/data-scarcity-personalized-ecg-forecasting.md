title: Data scarcity in personalized ECG
---

**Background:** The effectiveness of personalized deep learning models in electrocardiogram (ECG) analysis is often constrained by the limited availability of patient-specific, long-term ambulatory data with precise clinical annotations.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a lack of large-scale, publicly accessible ambulatory ECG databases with atrial fibrillation (AF) onset annotations, which limits the robust evaluation and scaling of personalized forecasting frameworks. Future work is required to compile larger, longer, and more diverse datasets to rigorously assess model performance and temporal dynamics over extended periods.

**Why It Matters:** The scarcity of labeled data is a primary bottleneck for advancing personalized healthcare AI; identifying this as a central limitation is crucial for prioritizing future community-wide efforts to build standardized, high-quality, long-term medical datasets.
