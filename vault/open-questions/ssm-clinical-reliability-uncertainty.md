title: SSM Clinical Reliability and Uncertainty
---

**Background:** State-space models (SSMs) and structured global convolution (SGConv) have proven effective for modeling long-range temporal dependencies in sequence data, but their application to physiological signal processing remains limited by the need to handle noisy, multichannel, and high-resolution waveforms.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work must investigate the integration of advanced uncertainty quantification into SSM-based architectures to ensure reliability in safety-critical clinical settings, as well as the potential for pretraining these models as foundation models to improve generalization across diverse, unseen clinical tasks.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical for transitioning SSM-based time series models from research prototypes to robust, reliable tools in real-world clinical decision support where model calibration and generalizability are paramount.
