title: Extending Wavelet Threshold Models
---

**Background:** Threshold autoregressive models typically rely on the assumption that parameters, including the threshold, remain constant over time, which may be inappropriate for complex systems subject to structural changes.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is needed to extend the wavelet-based time-varying threshold framework to more complex architectures, such as multi-regime models or multivariate time series, particularly to determine optimal resolution levels for high-dimensional threshold surfaces.

**Why It Matters:** Generalizing this framework to multiple regimes and multivariate contexts is critical for modeling realistic systems where dependencies are complex and non-stationary.

**Evidence:** the approach shows promise for extension to other classes of threshold models.
