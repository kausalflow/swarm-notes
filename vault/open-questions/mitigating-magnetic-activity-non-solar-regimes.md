title: Mitigating magnetic activity in non-solar regimes
---

**Background:** Asteroseismic modelling of solar-type stars is impacted by stellar magnetic activity, which introduces biases in inferred parameters such as stellar age. While mitigation strategies exist, their performance varies across different observational windows and modeling frameworks.

**Question / Future Work:** Develop and validate mitigation strategies for magnetic activity effects in asteroseismic inferences for stars that exhibit a broad range of magnetic cycle amplitudes, periods, and structural responses, extending beyond current Sun-as-a-star observations.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding and mitigating magnetic activity is critical for achieving the high-precision stellar parameters required by the PLATO mission and other future high-precision asteroseismology surveys.

**Evidence:** It would also be worthwhile exploring non-solar regimes, as PLATO will observe broad range of solar-type stars (Goupil et al. 2024), spanning a variety of magnetic-cycle amplitudes, periods, and structural responses.
