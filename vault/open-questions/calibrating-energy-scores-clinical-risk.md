title: Calibrating energy-based diagnostic risk
---

**Background:** Energy-based models often serve as heuristic ranking mechanisms for clinical hypotheses, but they currently lack explicit population-level priors or probabilistic calibration.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is needed to integrate population-level prevalence data or rigorous uncertainty calibration into energy-based reasoning frameworks to transition from heuristic hypothesis ranking to fully calibrated clinical diagnostic assessment.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the gap between heuristic energy scores and calibrated diagnostic probability is a fundamental unresolved problem for clinical agent design.

**Evidence:** The current model uses IDF-weighted symptom frequencies as a proxy for discriminative power, but it lacks the demographic context and test reliability factors.
