title: Flexible Integration of Contextual Fields
---

**Background:** Spatio-temporal point process (STPP) models often struggle to forecast accurately in regions with sparse historical event data. Exogenous spatial context can provide useful structural information, but there is a lack of flexible, high-dimensional integration methods that maintain stability in data-scarce regimes.

**Question / Future Work:** Development of advanced STPP architectures that can incorporate high-dimensional or neural-network-derived contextual fields is needed. Crucially, research must ensure these architectures maintain reliable spatial generalization in low-data regimes while developing interpretability tools to identify which contextual signals drive spatio-temporal risk.

**Why It Matters:** Moving beyond linear integration of spatial context is essential for scaling STPPs to more complex real-world datasets where simple priors are insufficient.

**Evidence:** The next step is to develop STPP architectures that integrate high-dimensional contextual fields more flexibly, including neural models, while preserving reliable spatial generalisation.
