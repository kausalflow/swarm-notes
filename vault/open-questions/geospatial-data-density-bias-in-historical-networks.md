title: Geospatial Density Bias Analysis
---

**Background:** Topological analysis of historical transportation networks often requires dense geospatial data, which is frequently inconsistent or fragmented across different regions. The Orbis model specifically suffers from significant disparities in node and edge density between the Eastern Mediterranean and the Western provinces of the Roman Empire.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop high-fidelity, standardized geospatial datasets for regions lacking comparable density (e.g., Western Roman Empire), which is essential to determine whether observed differences in network cycle redundancy are historical realities or artifacts of incomplete mapping. Expanding coverage is critical for verifying whether topological collapse is a geographically localized phenomenon or a systemic feature of imperial decline.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental data-fidelity bottleneck that prevents comparative cross-regional validation of resilience-based historical findings.
