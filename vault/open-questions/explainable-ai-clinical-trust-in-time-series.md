title: Explainable AI for Clinical Trust
---

**Background:** Medical decision support systems often rely on opaque machine learning models, which creates a barrier to their adoption in clinical practice. Explainability is widely recognized as a critical component for clinicians to verify model reliability and understand decision-making processes.

**Question / Future Work:** The integration of explainable AI (XAI) modules within medical time-series frameworks is essential to provide clinical justifications for model outputs and increase physician trust. Future work must address how to bridge the gap between complex algorithmic predictions and actionable clinical reasoning.

**Why It Matters:** Explainability is a prerequisite for the ethical and practical deployment of AI in clinical settings. Integrating such tools is essential for transitioning models from research environments to real-world healthcare applications.

**Evidence:** Without explicit clinical reasoning, it is difficult to show how the models make decisions, which is a common barrier to earning doctors’ trust. In future work, we plan to introduce explainable AI modules to provide clinical justifications for model decisions.
