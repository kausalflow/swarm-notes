title: Feedback Component Ablation
---

**Background:** Evolutionary search processes rely on feedback from an evaluator to guide the generation of improved candidate solutions.

**Question / Future Work:** A detailed ablation study is needed to determine which components of feedback (e.g., summary statistics, performance metrics, diagnostic logs) are most influential in guiding program refinement in LLM-driven evolutionary optimization.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the relationship between specific feedback types and LLM improvement trajectories is essential for developing autonomous, stable evolutionary optimizers.

**Evidence:** A detailed ablation of which feedback components are most useful, and when they help or hinder refinement, would provide a clearer understanding of the mechanisms driving EFE’s improvements.
