title: Market-aware active learning robustness
---

**Background:** In streaming environments where data acquisition involves heterogeneous costs and rolling budget constraints, the optimal balance between model-driven utility estimation and market-driven pricing remains a significant challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to explore how different market structures—such as dynamic pricing, competitive bidding, or strategic seller behavior—interact with active learning acquisition policies. Current frameworks are often constrained by specific pricing schemes (buyer-centric vs. seller-centric), and understanding the robustness of these policies against strategic or adversarial sellers is an open problem.

**Why It Matters:** The performance of online active learning in market environments is highly sensitive to the interaction between the analyst's utility-based acquisition rule and the pricing scheme imposed by external data sellers, necessitating more generalizable and robust strategies.
