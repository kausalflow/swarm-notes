title: Real-time spectral estimation trade-offs
---

**Background:** Adaptive closed-loop neuromodulation requires real-time identification of network-level connectivity metrics. Reliable spectral estimation usually necessitates long signal windows, which complicates the latency requirements for tracking state-dependent changes in neural coupling.

**Question / Future Work:** Establish a rigorous statistical framework to quantify the trade-off between the length of the data window, signal-to-noise ratio, and the precision of high-order spectral estimators to enable real-time tracking of connectivity states.

**Why It Matters:** Determining these trade-offs is critical for moving beyond offline signal analysis toward practical, personalized closed-loop neuromodulation protocols.

**Evidence:** ...a rigorous statistical characterization, evaluating the trade-off between data length, signal-to-noise ratio, and spectral estimation techniques, is necessary to determine the most robust analytical approach for tracking connectivity states in near real-time.
