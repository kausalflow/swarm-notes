title: Efficient Mapping of Tipping Regions
---

**Background:** Reduced-order models (ROMs) act as computationally efficient surrogates for complex Earth system dynamics, yet identifying the precise initial conditions or parameter ranges that trigger abrupt transitions remains a major research hurdle. Determining how these transitions emerge from high-dimensional parameter spaces is crucial for robust climate forecasting.

**Question / Future Work:** Develop efficient strategies for mapping the transition regions in initial condition and parameter spaces to identify tipping points without relying on computationally expensive exhaustive searches. Future research should prioritize methods that can systematically characterize the mechanisms governing these abrupt climate shifts.

**Why It Matters:** Systematically identifying the trigger conditions for irreversible climate events is foundational for effective risk assessment and long-range environmental planning.
