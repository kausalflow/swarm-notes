title: Broaden Optical Regime Evaluation
---

**Background:** Biogeochemical (BGC) parameter retrieval in aquatic systems relies on region-dependent bio-optical relationships, which limits the transferability of remote sensing algorithms across diverse coastal environments.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigating the robustness of region-adaptable retrieval frameworks across global coastal optical regimes is necessary to assess model generalizability beyond localized datasets and identify potential overfitting to regional bio-optical characteristics.

**Why It Matters:** The limitation of models to specific regional optical water types remains a fundamental barrier to scaling global aquatic monitoring systems.

**Evidence:** Expanding the evaluation to include a more diverse set of coastal optical regimes would improve the robustness of the assessment.
