title: Integrating Covariates in TDA Forecasting
---

**Background:** Topological data analysis (TDA) tools, such as vineyards, offer a flexible framework for visualizing spatiotemporal dynamics in scalar fields without requiring rigid parametric assumptions. However, there is no established methodology for effectively incorporating external variables, such as demographic, socioeconomic, or policy-related factors, into TDA-based forecasting models for epidemic hotspots.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing integrated forecasting models that combine topological summaries (like maximum persistent homology) with exogenous covariates—such as drug market indicators, treatment access, demographic changes, and policy interventions—remains an open challenge for predicting future spatiotemporal hotspots in public health datasets. Current models rely primarily on historical univariate time-series data, and incorporating these complex, heterogeneous predictors could improve both the spatial localization and temporal accuracy of epidemic risk assessment.

**Why It Matters:** Current TDA forecasting methods are limited by their focus on internal topological properties; integrating domain-specific covariates is essential for moving from descriptive topological analysis to actionable, policy-relevant predictive tools.

**Evidence:** Future researchers seeking to build on this model should include demographic, drug-market, treatment-access, reporting-delay, and policy covariates.
