title: Feedback Loops in Tutoring
---

**Background:** The evaluation of human tutoring skills often relies on scenario-based training, yet it remains difficult to confirm whether these skills transfer to authentic, real-time tutoring interactions. Developing robust, automated systems to assess the longitudinal transfer of pedagogical skills from training to practice is a major focus in educational technology.

**Question / Future Work:** While the current system provides a foundation for linking training to real-life performance, it lacks the ability to offer continuous, formative feedback on real-life tutoring. Investigating how providing direct, real-time feedback during authentic sessions—rather than just retrospective analysis—impacts the long-term, gradual growth of tutor pedagogical quality is a key area for future research. Furthermore, future efforts are needed to refine and audit Large Language Model (LLM) prompts continuously to better handle the nuanced, contextual nature of pedagogical moves, moving toward more robust and representative inter-rater reliability metrics.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing whether real-time feedback can accelerate the gradual learning process of tutors is critical for making tutor training programs more effective and responsive, especially given that the current system's predictive validity was modest (0.25 SD).

**Evidence:** Hence, continuous PD with feedback might enhance tutors’ gradual improvement over time... Future iterations will focus on automated data collection and the refinement of “evaluation prompts” to better capture the nuance of effective moves.
