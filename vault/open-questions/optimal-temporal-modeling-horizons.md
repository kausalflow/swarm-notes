title: Optimal temporal modeling horizons
---

**Background:** Energy system models often utilize short or selective temporal horizons for computational efficiency, which can lead to suboptimal infrastructure planning due to the neglect of long-term inter-annual variability in renewable resources.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the optimal temporal scope for renewable-integrated energy system planning requires investigating the sensitivity of capacity investments to various modeling durations (e.g., single vs. multi-year) and seasonal reference windows (e.g., winter-to-winter vs. summer-to-summer) for capturing extreme drought events.

**Why It Matters:** Essential for improving the robustness of long-term energy transition pathways and decarbonization infrastructure planning.

**Evidence:** Future work could investigate the effects of different time horizons for energy modeling, ranging from single years to several years, yet with a particular focus on renewable drought events.
