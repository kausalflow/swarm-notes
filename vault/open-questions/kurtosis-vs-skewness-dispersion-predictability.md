title: Kurtosis vs. Skewness Dispersion Predictability
---

**Background:** Higher-order moments like skewness and kurtosis of asset returns are established factors in asset pricing, but the predictive power of their cross-sectional heterogeneity for aggregate market returns remains incompletely understood.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains unresolved whether cross-sectional dispersion in higher-order moments, specifically kurtosis, provides independent predictive information for stock market returns beyond skewness dispersion. Future work should investigate whether this relationship stems from idiosyncratic tail risks or aggregate risk adjustments and decouple the relative contributions of these moment dispersions.

**Why It Matters:** Disentangling the influence of return asymmetry (skewness) from broader tail-heavy risks (kurtosis) is critical for refining asset pricing models and identifying whether market predictability arises from behavioral or rational risk-adjustment mechanisms.

**Evidence:** Replicating the analysis using kurtosis dispersion, we demonstrate that it also exhibits strong predictive ability, though weaker than skewness dispersion.
