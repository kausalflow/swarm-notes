title: Efficient Generative Weather Inference
---

**Background:** Generative models for weather downscaling often incur high computational costs during inference, which limits their use in operational ensemble forecasting where many realizations are required. Accelerating this inference process without losing fine-scale physical realism is a major challenge for the practical deployment of AI-based weather systems.

**Question / Future Work:** Develop and evaluate more efficient inference techniques, such as model distillation or consistency-based training, to reduce the computational cost of generative super-resolution for weather forecasting while maintaining physical realism.

**Why It Matters:** Computational efficiency is a major bottleneck for the operational use of high-resolution AI-based weather models, particularly for ensemble forecasting where many realizations are required.

**Evidence:** This computational cost motivates future work on speeding up inference through distillation or consistency-based generative modeling.
