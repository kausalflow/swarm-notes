title: Velocity-tracer ansatz theoretical validation
---

**Background:** In galaxy clustering analyses utilizing the Effective Field Theory of Large-Scale Structure (EFT of LSS), a common assumption is that galaxies follow the CDM-plus-baryon velocity field. The theoretical derivation of this velocity-tracer ansatz, however, remains incomplete, especially in cosmological models with massive neutrinos.

**Question / Future Work:** A rigorous derivation and validation of the velocity-tracer ansatz (v_g = v_cb) is required to ensure the accuracy of cosmological parameter estimation in upcoming high-precision surveys. Research is needed to quantify potential deviations from this approximation and to verify its validity when modeling galaxy clustering in the presence of neutrino mass.

**Why It Matters:** The velocity-tracer ansatz is a critical foundational assumption for interpreting galaxy survey data. Biases in this assumption directly propagate into uncertainties for neutrino mass and structure growth, which are central to Stage IV surveys.

**Evidence:** While a self consistent theoretical derivation of this result is still the subject of ongoing study, we adopt this ansatz in all that follows. We plan to investigate possible deviations from this approximation in a forthcoming work.
