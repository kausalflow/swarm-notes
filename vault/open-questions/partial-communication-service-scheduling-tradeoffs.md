title: Partial-Communication Service Scheduling Tradeoffs
---

**Background:** Electric vehicle charging infrastructure scheduling often relies on varying levels of communication between stations and users, impacting the effectiveness of centralized control mechanisms.

**Question / Future Work:** Defining the optimal information exchange requirements—balancing system performance against user privacy, partial data availability, and decentralized operation—remains an unresolved challenge in mixed-intent infrastructure environments. Investigating the performance trade-offs inherent in partial-communication frameworks is critical for scaling charging and service-based networks.

**Why It Matters:** This defines the fundamental limit of centralized coordination efficiency when network information is incomplete, non-uniform, or privacy-sensitive.

**Evidence:** In this study, we therefore construct an availability fluid-queuing-based forecast that explicitly accounts for uncertainty in Electric Vehicle arrivals within a partial-communication framework.
