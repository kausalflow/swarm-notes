title: Generalizing Adaptive Quantum Control
---

**Background:** Quantum control often assumes that environmental noise properties are relatively stable or follow well-defined, stationary stochastic processes. However, realistic experimental quantum platforms frequently experience non-stationary noise characterized by slow, unpredictable drifts in environmental parameters over time.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop robust, automated learning frameworks that can generalize beyond specific, known noise models (such as Random Telegraph Noise) to handle unknown, time-varying environmental drift in arbitrary quantum devices. Current adaptive approaches rely on specific analytical predictors or specific types of training data that may not generalize to more complex or heterogeneous noise environments encountered in practice.

**Why It Matters:** Broadening the applicability of predictive dynamical decoupling to unknown, non-stationary, and multi-source noise environments is critical for the scalability of fault-tolerant quantum computing in practical hardware.
