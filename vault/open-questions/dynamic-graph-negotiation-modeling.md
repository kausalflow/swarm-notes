title: Dynamic Relational Graph Modeling
---

**Background:** Negotiation forecasting models often rely on either linguistic processing or static game-theoretic constraints, failing to account for the dynamic, time-varying nature of agent relationships and interactional strategies.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to transition from static graph topologies to dynamically evolving relational structures that account for real-time interactional signals, such as sentiment trajectories and satisfaction markers, to better support complex, human-in-the-loop negotiation systems.

**Why It Matters:** Negotiation is inherently a temporal, multi-turn process where power dynamics and trust evolve; static graphs fail to capture these crucial transitions, limiting the predictive robustness of current systems.
