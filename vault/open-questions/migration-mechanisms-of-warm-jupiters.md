title: Migration Mechanisms of Warm Jupiters
---

**Background:** The migration pathways of warm Jupiters, ranging from high-eccentricity tidal migration to smoother, disk-driven migration, remain actively debated. Identifying the dynamical history of these systems often hinges on the presence of additional low-mass companion planets that are frequently cleared by high-eccentricity mechanisms.

**Question / Future Work:** Advanced high-sensitivity observational studies are required to rigorously rule out lower-mass companion planets around warm Jupiters. Distinguishing between high-eccentricity and quiescent migration pathways requires confirming whether these planetary systems are dynamically isolated or host additional small companions.

**Why It Matters:** This is fundamental for understanding the diversity of formation and evolutionary history of gas giant exoplanets.
