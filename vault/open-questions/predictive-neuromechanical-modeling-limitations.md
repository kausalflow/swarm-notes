title: Predicting Locomotion from Neurons
---

**Background:** The inference of dynamical systems that accurately link neural activity to behavioral outputs, such as changes in body shape, remains a fundamental challenge across diverse model organisms. Despite significant theoretical and experimental progress, achieving a generalizable, low-dimensional, and predictive model that integrates behavioral dynamics remains an open problem.

**Question / Future Work:** A primary challenge is the development of an end-to-end modeling framework that is both data-efficient and interpretable, capable of predicting physical locomotion patterns from neural activity across different species and environments. Future work must address how these models can be scaled to more complex neural representations, relaxed geometric constraints, and the handling of hidden variables that are not explicitly captured in currently available experimental datasets.

**Why It Matters:** Developing such a framework is critical for transitioning from descriptive to predictive neuroscience, as it provides a necessary basis for designing optogenetic experiments and understanding the neural control of behavior in real time.
