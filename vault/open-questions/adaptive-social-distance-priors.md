title: Adaptive Social Distance Priors
---

**Background:** Socially compliant robot navigation relies on maintaining interpersonal distances, which are often governed by complex, context-dependent social norms.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing adaptive social distance priors that are dynamically conditioned on richer human-centric contexts, such as group interactions and specific social activities, remains a critical challenge.

**Why It Matters:** Static social distance metrics often fail to capture the nuance of human social norms, leading to either overly conservative or intrusive robot behavior.
