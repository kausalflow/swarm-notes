title: Stationarity limits in non-Markovian systems
---

**Background:** Stationary processes governed by Markovian stochastic differential equations exhibit growth-rate distributions that converge to a stationary distribution for sufficiently large time lags. While empirical data often display non-normal, leptokurtic behaviors, the exact conditions and universality of these deviations from normality across diverse dynamical systems remain to be fully characterized.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate the conditions under which the stationary logarithmic growth-rate distribution remains time-dependent, specifically testing whether non-Markovian dynamics with strong correlations or memory effects can be integrated into the existing analytical null-model framework.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding whether these growth-rate distribution patterns hold outside of Markovian constraints is crucial for the broader applicability of the proposed heuristic selection workflow in real-world systems.

**Evidence:** The remaining series present a linear growth of the variance. Assuming that the stationarity tests are correct, we can imagine that the system is endowed with very strong correlations produced by memory effects (the Markovian approximation is not valid) or the variance is stuck in the growing phase without reaching the saturation value (very slow dynamics).
