title: Spectrum Foundation Model Adaptability
---

**Background:** The application of general-purpose transformer-based models to real-world spectrum sensing is a developing field. While custom architectures have been proposed, they often require task-specific tuning, leaving the potential of large-scale, foundation-style models for wireless signal intelligence largely unexplored.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is required to explore the full potential of adapting general-purpose, decoder-only transformer architectures for wideband radio frequency (RF) signal forecasting. This includes determining the optimal scale of pre-training, investigating the cross-domain transferability of learned representations from linguistic or other sequential data to spectrum data, and refining tokenization strategies that can better preserve fine-grained temporal and frequency-domain dynamics.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical because establishing how foundation models can be systematically adapted for RF data could enable a paradigm shift towards general-purpose, scalable wireless signal intelligence systems for 6G and beyond.
