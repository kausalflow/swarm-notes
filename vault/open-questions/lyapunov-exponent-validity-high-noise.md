title: Lyapunov exponents in high-noise regimes
---

**Background:** The standard use of Lyapunov exponents to characterize dynamical stability and bifurcation relies on conditions such as the multiplicative ergodic theorem, which are often violated in systems with high noise intensity relative to the attractor size.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to identify the appropriate invariant quantities or metrics for evaluating bifurcation reconstruction in stochastic dynamical systems where noise amplitude is comparable to the attractor size, as the standard Lyapunov exponent definition becomes theoretically ambiguous.

**Why It Matters:** This is technically critical because Lyapunov exponents are the standard metric for diagnosing bifurcations in this framework, and their invalidity in high-noise regimes limits the assessment of predictive reliability.

**Evidence:** Under such large noise, however, it is not clear whether the assumptions required for the existence of Lyapunov exponents under the multiplicative ergodic theorem are satisfied.
