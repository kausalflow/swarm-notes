title: Spatial Prediction for Embodied AI
---

**Background:** Spatiotemporal scene graphs provide a compositional and interpretable representation of dynamic scenes where human actions transform environment states. The integration of such structured representations with spatial reasoning is a critical objective for developing robotic systems capable of planning, forecasting, and controlling interaction in dynamic environments.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to extend existing graph-based models, such as the one described, by incorporating explicit spatial prediction capabilities to enable robotic agents to reason about the nature, spatial location, and underlying causal mechanisms of scene changes. Future work aims to bridge the gap between perception and action, facilitating grounded planning and control through explicit future scene state predictions.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging structured scene representation with spatial reasoning is essential for moving from passive video analysis to active robotic control and embodied AI planning.
