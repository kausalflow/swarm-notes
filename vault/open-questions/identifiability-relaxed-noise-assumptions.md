title: Identifiability under relaxed assumptions
---

**Background:** Identifiability theory for regime-switching structural causal models often relies on strict assumptions such as exponential family noise and real-analytic functions.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing identifiability theory for regime-switching systems that relaxes the current stringent requirements on noise distributions and function classes remains an open problem. Determining the conditions under which structural identifiability is maintained under more general assumptions is essential for broader real-world applicability.

**Why It Matters:** Moving beyond these strict assumptions is crucial to validating the framework in environments where noise distributions are not strictly exponential and systems may not be real-analytic.

**Evidence:** Preserving linear independence in more relaxed settings is a challenging task that might warrant a vastly different proof strategy than the one deployed in this work.
