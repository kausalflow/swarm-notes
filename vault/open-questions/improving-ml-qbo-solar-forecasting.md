title: Enhancing ML for QBOs in Solar Forecasting
---

**Background:** The solar interior generates complex magnetic activity that manifests as periodic fluctuations in surface p-mode oscillation frequencies, which are often used to study solar dynamo processes. Although machine learning models are increasingly applied to these time-series to predict future solar cycle behavior, these approaches currently struggle to accurately capture and reproduce shorter-term phenomena like quasi-biennial oscillations (QBOs).

**Question / Future Work:** Machine learning-based forecasting frameworks often fail to consistently replicate the quasi-biennial oscillation (QBO) patterns observed in p-mode frequency shifts and other solar activity proxies. Research is required to refine these models by incorporating additional helioseismic parameters (such as mode line widths and amplitudes) and integrating a broader range of solar activity indices to improve the temporal and structural fidelity of these data-driven predictions.

**Why It Matters:** The inability to capture QBOs limits the diagnostic utility of these forecasting models for evaluating solar dynamo theories and understanding the coupling between the solar interior and its exterior, which is critical for space weather prediction.

**Evidence:** A careful examination of the scaled-forecasts in our study show that Deep learning-NBEATS method has been unable to reproduce the QBOs like the other methods, despite all showing similar qualitative behaviour.
