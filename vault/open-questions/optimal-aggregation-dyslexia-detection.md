title: Optimal Aggregation for Dyslexia Detection
---

**Background:** Eye-tracking data is often recorded at the level of individual fixation sequences, but clinical diagnosis of conditions like dyslexia frequently requires evaluation at the level of the reader.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the optimal aggregation strategy for topological features derived from eye-tracking data is essential, particularly investigating the performance differential between trial-level classification and reader-level behavioral profiling on larger cohorts to address potential population heterogeneity.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the optimal level of data aggregation is essential for moving topological dyslexia detection from a research setting to a reliable clinical screening tool.

**Evidence:** Performance gains are more pronounced at the reader level, which is expected given the aggregation of multiple trials into a more stable behavioral profile.
