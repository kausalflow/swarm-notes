title: Enhancing Computational Efficiency and Scalability
---

**Background:** Anomaly detection in multivariate time series often requires optimizing models for both computational efficiency and deployment across diverse, real-world application environments.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop methods that achieve a better balance between the computational complexity required for high-performance spatio-temporal modeling and the practical constraints of real-time deployment in industrial or large-scale systems. Future work should focus on improving the computational efficiency of the model, expanding its application scope, and exploring its practical use in real-time scenarios.

**Why It Matters:** Computational bottlenecks remain a significant barrier to transitioning complex graph-based anomaly detection models from research to real-world, time-critical operational environments.

**Evidence:** Future research can prioritize three areas: improving the computational efficiency of the model, expanding the scope of application of the model, and exploring the practical application of MSCENet in scenarios requiring accurate anomaly detection.
