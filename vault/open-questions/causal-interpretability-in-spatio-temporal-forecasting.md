title: Causal Interpretability for Period Interactions
---

**Background:** Spatio-temporal forecasting models are often constrained by short-term input windows, which frequently fail to resolve 'temporal mirage' phenomena where identical local observations lead to divergent long-term trajectories.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing more rigorous causal identification and discovery methods within cross-period interaction frameworks is needed to move beyond correlation-based pattern matching and enhance the interpretability of periodic dependencies in spatio-temporal dynamics.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing clear causal grounding for temporal dependencies is critical for improving model trust and ensuring forecasting stability in non-stationary urban environments.
