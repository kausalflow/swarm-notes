title: Long-horizon EO forecasting limits
---

**Background:** Earth Observation forecasting typically involves predicting future surface dynamics from satellite imagery conditioned on external weather forcings. Existing long-horizon simulation capabilities are limited by the duration and granularity of paired satellite and meteorological datasets.

**Question / Future Work:** Expanding probabilistic forecasting models to support multi-year or decadal simulation remains a significant challenge, requiring new approaches to handle accumulated error, evolving seasonal regimes, and slow climate trends.

**Why It Matters:** The ability to perform multi-year or decadal forecasting is critical for climate risk assessment and ecosystem modeling, moving beyond short-term seasonal prediction toward reliable long-term climate simulation.

**Evidence:** Our current setting can forecast over a seasonal window, but the limited length of paired satellite-observation and weather records makes it difficult to extend directly to multi-year or decadal simulation.
