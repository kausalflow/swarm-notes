title: Robotic Assessment Domain Shift Robustness
---

**Background:** Standardized robotic assessments of neurological impairment frequently suffer from domain shifts when applied across different hardware installations, software versions, or clinical environments. Maintaining clinical reliability in these systems often requires calibration across these diverse real-world settings.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to determine the robustness of time series foundation models when deployed across diverse, real-world clinical environments that differ from the training data, specifically addressing potential performance degradation due to variations in firmware, seating, or installation. Developing strategies for lightweight, continual finetuning or federated model updating is essential to ensure consistent, high-precision assessment across different robotic clinics.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical for the translational success of forecast-augmented assessment; without addressing domain shift, models may become biased or unreliable when moved beyond the original laboratory setting.
