title: AI-Driven Optimization Framework Applicability
---

**Background:** In the context of environmental science, management of infrastructure (such as coastal hydraulic structures) is often formulated as an optimization problem requiring expensive simulations to identify effective real-time control strategies.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigating the architectural requirements for pairing predictive deep learning models with automated evaluation tools remains a significant challenge for developing broadly effective, real-time optimization frameworks for complex physical systems.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the gap between neural prediction surrogates and decision-focused optimization is critical for moving beyond simple forecasting into actionable environmental management.

**Evidence:** The question of whether a prediction tool in tandem with an evaluation tool is an appropriate architecture for other optimization problems remains an open problem.
