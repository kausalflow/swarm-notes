title: Long-Horizon Planning in VLAs
---

**Background:** Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models often struggle with multi-stage execution due to the absence of explicit, persistent memory mechanisms that track state across extended durations.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing architectures that integrate structured perception (like affordance maps) with long-term memory to enable stable, multi-stage planning remains a primary bottleneck for embodied AI agents.

**Why It Matters:** Long-horizon manipulation is a critical frontier for moving from short-task instruction following to complex, multi-stage robotic autonomy.

**Evidence:** While AffordanceVLA excels on the Spatial, Object, and Goal suites, the margin narrows on LIBERO-Long, suggesting that extremely long-horizon sequential tasks would further benefit from explicit long-term memory.
