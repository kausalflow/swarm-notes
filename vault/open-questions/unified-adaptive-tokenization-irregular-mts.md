title: Unified Adaptive Tokenization for MTS
---

**Background:** Multivariate time series are increasingly characterized by diverse irregularities such as missing values and non-uniform sampling frequencies, which disrupt traditional synchronous temporal modeling. Although adaptive patching techniques have been introduced to manage these dynamics, there is currently no consensus on a unified approach that remains robust across different irregular data categories.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to develop a universal adaptive tokenization framework that handles heterogeneous temporal irregularities—such as missing values, non-uniform sampling, and event-driven signals—without relying on dataset-specific heuristics or static patching assumptions. Establishing a robust theoretical foundation for such tokenization remains a significant challenge for deploying time-series foundation models.

**Why It Matters:** Standardizing adaptive tokenization is critical to moving away from dataset-specific hyperparameter tuning in time-series forecasting.
