title: Economic transients of forecast governance reforms
---

**Background:** The transition from systems operating under persistent forecast bias to those utilizing unbiased forecasting regimes causes structural re-adjustments in energy pricing, storage management, and market liquidity. These transitions induce temporary economic dynamics that remain largely uncharacterized in terms of their duration and welfare distribution.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed to characterize the economic transients that occur during the shift from biased to unbiased forecasting regimes in energy markets. This includes modeling the duration of adjustment periods and identifying the distributional welfare impacts on various market participants to inform regulatory and policy interventions.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the transition path is vital for managing the economic and political risks associated with model governance reforms in energy-dependent infrastructure.
