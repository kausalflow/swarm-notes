title: Over-squashing impact on forecasting performance
---

**Background:** Spatio-temporal graph neural networks are often used for forecasting, yet they are susceptible to over-squashing, a phenomenon where long-range information is excessively compressed. While graph rewiring is a known mitigation, the formal link between architectural bottleneck alleviation and predictive forecasting performance is under-explored.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to establish a rigorous theoretical and empirical framework that links the mitigation of over-squashing in dynamic spatio-temporal graphs to quantitative improvements in forecasting accuracy across diverse network topologies.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing this link is essential for justifying structural graph interventions in predictive modeling and moving beyond heuristic architectural designs.

**Evidence:** However, they do not formalize the effect of OQ mitigation on predictive performance, and neither their work nor prior work explores it in the context of dynamic graphs, to the best of our knowledge.
