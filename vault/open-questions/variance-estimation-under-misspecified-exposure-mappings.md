title: Variance estimation under misspecified exposure mappings
---

**Background:** In design-based causal inference with interference, conservative variance estimators often depend on specific assumptions about the exposure mapping or interference structure, which may not hold under misspecification.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing methods for conservative variance estimation that remain robust when the assumed exposure mapping is misspecified remains an open and challenging problem.

**Why It Matters:** Misspecification of exposure mappings is common in applied settings, and existing design-based variance estimators can become anti-conservative if the exposure mapping is incorrectly specified.

**Evidence:** Under such misspecification [of exposure mappings], variance estimation remains a difficult problem.
