title: Mechanisms of CLV Tangency
---

**Background:** The understanding of the underlying dynamical mechanisms leading to the tangency of covariant Lyapunov vectors in complex systems remains incomplete. Existing theoretical frameworks for dominated Oseledets splitting do not account for the specific dynamical pathways that result in these geometric tangencies and their subsequent link to critical transitions.

**Question / Future Work:** The theoretical connection between the dynamical processes leading to covariant Lyapunov vector (CLV) tangencies and the onset of extreme events or critical transitions requires further elucidation, particularly beyond the adiabatic approximation. Investigating how these geometric indicators can be generalized for higher-dimensional multiscale systems remains a critical challenge.

**Why It Matters:** This question is central to establishing a rigorous, universal theoretical foundation for predicting extreme events across diverse fields, moving beyond empirical observations to a mechanistic understanding.

**Evidence:** Although tangencies between CLVs have been empirically observed as potential precursors to extreme events [5, 57], a theoretical explanation of the route to extreme events and critical transitions is missing. ... The connection between such mechanisms, extreme events, and invariant manifolds is still not well understood [62, 61, 13, 10].
