title: QRC Real Hardware Validation
---

**Background:** Quantum reservoir computing (QRC) relies on quantum dynamics to map inputs into a feature space for classical readout. Deployment on actual quantum hardware requires accounting for physical constraints, such as noise and resource limits.

**Question / Future Work:** The performance of QRC frameworks, currently evaluated through simulation, must be validated on real quantum hardware to determine the feasibility of deployment under realistic noise and connectivity constraints. Future efforts should transition from finite-shot simulations to actual hardware execution.

**Why It Matters:** Transitioning from simulation to real hardware is the critical next step for establishing QRC as a viable practical technology for edge computing, as simulations may not fully capture the complexity of physical noise models and hardware limitations.

**Evidence:** The present study is limited to a small architecture search, two random seeds, one dataset, and finite-shot simulation rather than real quantum hardware.
