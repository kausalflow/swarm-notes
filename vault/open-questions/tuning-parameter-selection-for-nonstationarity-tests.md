title: Tuning Parameter Selection for Nonstationarity Tests
---

**Background:** Distinguishing between long-memory stationarity and genuine nonstationarity is a fundamental challenge in time series due to similar low-frequency spectral behaviors.

**Question / Future Work:** The power of tests distinguishing nonstationarity from long-memory stationarity is highly sensitive to the number of low-frequency periodogram ordinates (tuning parameter 's') used. Developing an adaptive, data-driven framework for selecting 's' that optimizes detection power across diverse nonstationary processes remains a critical bottleneck.

**Why It Matters:** As the primary tuning parameter for frequency-domain tests, the arbitrary or non-optimal selection of 's' fundamentally limits the reliability of nonstationarity diagnostics.

**Evidence:** Determining an optimal, adaptive data-driven selection method for s that maximizes power across a wide variety of nonstationary processes remains an open challenge.
