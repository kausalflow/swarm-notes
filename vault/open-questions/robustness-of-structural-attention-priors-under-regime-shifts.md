title: Robustness of structural attention priors
---

**Background:** Time series attention mechanisms often rely on the assumption of stable temporal patterns to optimize computational complexity. The robustness of these learned structural priors under non-stationary conditions and regime shifts is a significant bottleneck for real-world deployment.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate how attention-based efficiency mechanisms, which decompose scores into shared patterns and residual components, perform under abrupt regime changes or significant concept drift where the stability of attention patterns is violated.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the limits of structural attention priors is essential for transitioning efficient forecasting models from public benchmarks to volatile, industrial-grade operational environments.
