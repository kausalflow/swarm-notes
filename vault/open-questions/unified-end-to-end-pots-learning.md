title: Unified End-to-End POTS Learning
---

**Background:** Partially-observed time series (POTS) are frequently encountered in real-world scenarios, but standard approaches often separate missing-value imputation from downstream predictive modeling.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a lack of comprehensive, unified frameworks that treat data imputation and downstream tasks (e.g., forecasting, classification, clustering, anomaly detection) as a cohesive end-to-end learning problem. Future research needs to establish integrated architectures and evaluation protocols that mitigate the performance losses and inefficiencies caused by fragmented workflows in POTS analysis.

**Why It Matters:** The transition from fragmented to end-to-end learning is critical for improving both the accuracy and the reproducibility of machine learning models trained on real-world, incomplete time series data.
