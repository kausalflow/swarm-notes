title: Multi-scale physiological phase locking
---

**Background:** Wearable-derived physiological signals exhibit both circadian and multi-day rhythms that potentially modulate clinical event risk. Current methods struggle to confirm the phase-locking of these multi-day rhythms due to limitations in data scale and the non-stationary nature of physiological oscillations.

**Question / Future Work:** Determine the existence and robustness of phase-locking between clinical events and multi-day physiological oscillations (14-28 days) across larger cohorts. Research must address whether seizure risk is modulated by the interaction of multiple rhythms (circadian vs. multi-day) and requires advanced non-stationary signal processing to replace static band-limited filtering.

**Why It Matters:** Moving from single-scale to multi-scale phase modeling is essential for developing clinically actionable forecasting tools that operate on biologically realistic, long-term cycles.
