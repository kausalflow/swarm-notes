title: Ensemble validation of core models
---

**Background:** Reconstructing the evolution of Earth's core surface flow over millennial timescales requires overcoming the non-uniqueness of the inversion problem, which is inherently limited by sparse and uncertain palaeomagnetic data.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research should systematically evaluate the dependency of reconstructed core flow features on the underlying numerical geodynamo prior by conducting ensemble inversions using multiple disparate dynamo simulations with varying boundary conditions and parameters. This approach is necessary to statistically distinguish between robust, data-driven features and artifacts arising from specific geodynamo priors.

**Why It Matters:** The dependency on kinematic priors is a fundamental challenge in geomagnetic data assimilation and similar inverse problems. Without systematic ensemble validation, it is difficult to quantify the reliability and physical validity of reconstructed dynamics.

**Evidence:** A comparison of the results from such a survey will provide a better constraint on what features are data-driven and not.
