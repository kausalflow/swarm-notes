title: Theoretical Limits of Temporal Adaptation
---

**Background:** While large models have been successfully adapted for time series and spatio-temporal tasks, the theoretical understanding of why and when these adaptations succeed remains limited. The field lacks a rigorous framework to explain the transferability of representations to temporal sequences, particularly regarding the effects of distribution shift and numerical precision.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need for deeper theoretical analysis to determine the conditions under which pre-trained foundation models can effectively capture temporal patterns, the limitations imposed by tokenization schemes for non-textual temporal data, and the task-specific boundaries of large models in forecasting, anomaly detection, and reasoning.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing a theoretical basis is critical for moving beyond heuristic model designs, guiding the principled development of architectures that are robust to the unique properties of temporal and spatio-temporal data.
