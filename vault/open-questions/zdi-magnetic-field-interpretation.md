title: ZDI Field Topology Interpretability
---

**Background:** Stellar magnetic field reconstructions often rely on Zeeman-Doppler Imaging (ZDI), which typically uses circular polarization (Stokes V) to map surface magnetism. However, this method is limited by the loss of small-scale flux through polarity cancellation and sensitivity to observing geometry, leading to uncertainty in how ZDI-derived topologies relate to the total physical magnetic field.

**Question / Future Work:** Clarify the physical meaning of recovered field components in ZDI reconstructions, particularly the 'toroidal' component, and determine the extent to which these topologies can serve as accurate boundary conditions for coronal and stellar wind models of exoplanet hosts. This requires further development of full Stokes (IQUV) inversions, Zeeman-intensification mapping, and direct comparisons with surface-flux-transport-based forward models.

**Why It Matters:** ZDI is the standard observational tool for mapping large-scale stellar magnetism, but its output is used as input for physics-based models where errors propagate into environmental characterizations for exoplanets.

**Evidence:** What “toroidal field” actually means physically, and to what extent recovered topologies can serve as boundary conditions for wind and coronal models of exoplanet hosts (Sect. 6), therefore remain open.
