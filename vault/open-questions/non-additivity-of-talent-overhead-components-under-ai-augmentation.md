title: Non-additivity of talent overhead components
---

**Background:** In the context of the shift from labor-time accounting to output-based talent ROI in the human-AI era, firms currently rely on an implicit assumption of additive overhead components that may no longer be valid.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is required to determine whether the components of firm-internal talent overhead become non-additive in their impact on ROI when subjected to AI augmentation and capacity heterogeneity, requiring an empirical estimation of cross-partial derivatives between overhead components.

**Why It Matters:** Standard cost accounting assumes overhead components are additive; if this assumption is invalid in the AI era, existing managerial accounting models for talent ROI are structurally flawed.

**Evidence:** Theorem 1, Overhead Decomposition. The seven contemporary components of firm-internal talent overhead do not add up. Under AI augmentation, the cross-partial derivative of return on investment with respect to any two component costs becomes non-zero for at least one component pair.
