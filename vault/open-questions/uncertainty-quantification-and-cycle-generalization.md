title: Uncertainty and Solar Cycle Generalization
---

**Background:** Solar magnetic field dynamics vary significantly across the solar cycle, yet predictive models are typically validated on limited temporal segments. Robustness under weak-field conditions, such as solar minimum, remains unverified for most current deep learning approaches.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research must evaluate model performance across complete solar cycles, including quiet-sun and solar minimum periods, and incorporate uncertainty quantification to provide confidence intervals for predicted magnetic quantities. This is particularly vital in weak-field regimes where forecasting stochasticity increases.

**Why It Matters:** Uncertainty quantification and cycle-aware generalization are prerequisites for transitioning research models to operational space weather tools.
