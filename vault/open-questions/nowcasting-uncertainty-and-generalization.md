title: Nowcasting Uncertainty and Generalization
---

**Background:** Precipitation nowcasting, the task of predicting high-resolution radar reflectivity for 0-3 hour horizons, is currently dominated by either deterministic models that suffer from blurring at longer lead times or generative models that incur high computational costs due to iterative sampling.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a persistent need for nowcasting frameworks that can balance deterministic precision with probabilistic uncertainty estimation, as well as a requirement to improve the generalization capabilities of these models across varied radar networks and heterogeneous climatological regions.

**Why It Matters:** Quantifying uncertainty is critical for high-stakes operational decision-making in disaster prevention, and generalization across diverse radar networks is a fundamental requirement for the widespread deployment of data-driven meteorological models.
