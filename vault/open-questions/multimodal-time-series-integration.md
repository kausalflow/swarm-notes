title: Integrating Qualitative Contextual Forecasting
---

**Background:** Current agentic forecasting frameworks primarily focus on orchestrating numerical workflows such as model arbitration or feature selection, often neglecting the integration of unstructured qualitative data as a core reasoning component. Time series forecasting in volatile domains remains a compromise between capturing complex statistical periodicity and understanding fundamental drivers from textual signals.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to determine the optimal methodologies for integrating unstructured textual context with numerical sequence modeling to capture both structural time-series features and qualitative causal drivers. Future work is required to develop unified frameworks that can simultaneously handle high-frequency volatile data and multi-seasonal periodicities without relying on monolithic, brittle prompting strategies.

**Why It Matters:** The current split between TSFMs (numerical) and LLMs (textual) creates a structural deficit in real-world forecasting; bridging this gap is essential for robust, interpretable decision-making in volatile environments.
