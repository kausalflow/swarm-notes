title: Integrating Latent Biological Factors
---

**Background:** Neurodegenerative disease progression is a multifactorial and heterogeneous process influenced by diverse biological, genetic, and environmental variables. Clinical forecasting models typically rely on macroscopic proxies like clinical scores or medication dosage that fail to capture the underlying non-linear physiological and pharmacokinetic complexities.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved challenge in integrating multi-modal data—such as patient-specific genetic profiles, detailed clinical subtypes, and fine-grained pharmacokinetic data—into generative frameworks to improve the predictive accuracy of localized neurodegenerative morphological changes. Future research is required to incorporate these latent biological and clinical factors to move beyond simplified proxies for treatment and disease progression.

**Why It Matters:** Current models rely on aggregated clinical metrics (like LEDD) which are insufficient for personalized forecasting; bridging this gap is essential for clinical applicability and addressing inter-patient heterogeneity.
