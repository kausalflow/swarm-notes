title: Modeling drainage at low levels
---

**Background:** Inverting the liquid level trajectory of a draining tank relies on accurate physical models of outflow dynamics, which often diverge from observed behavior due to factors like surface tension and viscosity.

**Question / Future Work:** The current physics-based models for drainage, such as those relying on Torricelli’s law, become inadequate near the end of the draining process where surface tension and viscous effects cause significant deviations from predicted trajectories. Research is required to integrate more sophisticated flow models into inverse frameworks to maintain precision at low liquid levels.

**Why It Matters:** Accurate estimation at low liquid levels is vital for environmental forensics and regulatory compliance, making the failure of standard physics-based models a key bottleneck.

**Evidence:** more sophisticated flow models are of interest for viscous fluids or larger drainage holes where Torricelli’s law may no longer apply.
