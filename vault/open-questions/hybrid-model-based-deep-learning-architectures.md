title: Hybrid Model-Based Deep Learning
---

**Background:** Hybrid approaches aim to combine the physical interpretability and sample efficiency of model-based methods with the flexibility and high-capacity learning of deep learning architectures.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing and evaluating hybrid model-based deep learning architectures that leverage established structural knowledge of physical systems while retaining the capacity to learn complex, non-linear dependencies from data, particularly in scenarios where purely data-driven models face efficiency or interpretability bottlenecks.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing the performance gap between purely model-based and purely data-driven methods is essential for real-world applications where systems are complex enough that they are not perfectly captured by linear models, yet structured enough that ignoring domain knowledge leads to inefficient learning.
