title: Generalizing Behavior Forecasting Representations
---

**Background:** Motion forecasting models often rely on category-specific priors, limiting their applicability to diverse species and morphologies. A generalizable representation for behavior, independent of specific agent appearance or scene context, remains a significant challenge in visual intelligence.

**Question / Future Work:** The research highlights the need for generalizable behavior forecasting that transcends category-specific 3D models or object-specific priors. While point trajectories are proposed as a scalable visual token, future work is required to explore how these representations can further capture complex, non-rigid behavior across even more diverse and open-world environments, potentially moving towards a more foundational predictive model of animate motion.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing a category-agnostic behavior forecasting framework is essential for moving beyond narrow, domain-specific models towards true predictive visual intelligence. Understanding the limits and extensions of this trajectory-token approach is crucial for scaling motion understanding in unconstrained, real-world settings.
