title: Intraday Contagion Prediction Validation
---

**Background:** Financial contagion modeling often relies on historical price movements rather than real-time semantic analysis of news, leaving a gap in understanding how information propagates through market networks before price reactions are observed.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains to be determined whether the lead-time between a detected news-driven contagion signal and the realized market return compresses toward the 20–60 minute interval established in previous literature when tested on institutional-grade intraday return tapes. Transitioning from daily return analysis to high-frequency intraday evaluation is required to validate the model's performance in environments where microsecond-scale architecture is most relevant.

**Why It Matters:** Validating the predictive utility of microsecond-scale architectures at high frequencies is critical to bridge the gap between architectural performance and market-impact relevance.
