title: Systematic Preprocessing Design Methodology
---

**Background:** In reservoir computing, the choice of preprocessing method for non-temporal data significantly impacts system performance by determining how input features are mapped into temporal dynamics.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a lack of a systematic design methodology to determine optimal preprocessing parameters (e.g., segmentation, parity, encoding dimensionality) that balance classification accuracy against physical hardware constraints such as area and power. Current methods remain largely empirical and task-specific.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing a formal design framework for input-to-temporal transformation would enable more efficient deployment of physical reservoir computing systems across diverse, non-temporal input tasks.

**Evidence:** The choice of preprocessing method directly determines how the image is temporally encoded... the preprocessing strategy to be tailored to the desired accuracy–area tradeoff.
