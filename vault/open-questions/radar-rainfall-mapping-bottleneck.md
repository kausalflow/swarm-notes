title: Radar-to-Rainfall Mapping Bottlenecks
---

**Background:** Rainfall estimation models often rely on radar reflectivity, which requires bias-prone empirical conversions (e.g., Z-R relations) to accurately estimate surface rainfall intensities. These mapping relationships are context-dependent and spatially complex.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing better methods for mapping radar-based moisture measurements to ground-level rainfall intensities is a critical bottleneck, as simple pixel-level alignment often fails due to the physical discrepancies between the two data sources.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing the physical misalignment between remote sensing and surface observations is essential for reliable high-resolution weather forecasting.

**Evidence:** As the mapping between radar intensity and surface rainfall is complex, non-local, and context-dependent, naive pixel-level alignment can be misleading.
