title: Mechanisms of Trunk Thermo-hydraulic Coupling
---

**Background:** Tree trunks function as large water-rich structures that exhibit thermal buffering against atmospheric variability, yet the relative contributions of passive thermal inertia and active hydraulically mediated heat transport to this process remain incompletely resolved.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research requires integrating simultaneous measurements of sap velocity, xylem water potential, and radial trunk temperature profiles to disentangle passive thermal buffering from hydraulically driven heat transport and quantify the specific role of sap-mediated energy redistribution.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing this is critical to move from correlative evidence of thermo-hydraulic coupling to a mechanistic understanding of internal temperature regulation in response to climate fluctuations.

**Evidence:** Future studies combining direct sap-flow measurements, xylem water potential, soil moisture, and radial trunk-temperature profiles would allow the hydraulic and thermal contributions to be separated more explicitly.
