title: Endpoint anticipation in spontaneous dialogue
---

**Background:** Spoken dialogue systems often face latency challenges in open-domain conversational settings due to the inherent unpredictability of speech patterns.

**Question / Future Work:** Current endpoint anticipation models struggle with unstructured conversational phenomena, such as mid-turn backtracking and late-arriving critical information, which cause false anticipation triggers. Resolving these failures is essential for achieving human-parity latency in general-purpose conversational agents.

**Why It Matters:** Generalizing anticipation to spontaneous, unstructured speech is a prerequisite for robust, low-latency, real-world conversational AI.

**Evidence:** Future work will explore semantic edge cases, such as mid-turn backtracking and late-arriving critical information.
