title: Expanding EHR representation benchmarks
---

**Background:** Electronic health records (EHRs) can be represented through various paradigms, including multivariate time-series, event streams, and textual event streams, each offering distinct trade-offs for clinical predictive modeling.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to expand current cross-representation benchmarks for electronic health records to encompass a broader range of clinical environments and tasks to ensure better robustness, generalizability, and output calibration for clinical decision support.

**Why It Matters:** Evaluating clinical models across a wider variety of settings and tasks is critical for determining the true general-purpose utility of different EHR representation methods and for identifying environment-specific performance bottlenecks.

**Evidence:** Future works include extending the benchmark to emergency and general inpatient settings, broadening the task suite, and calibrating model outputs to support uncertainty analysis across EHR representation methods.
