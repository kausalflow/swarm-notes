title: Generalizing Selective Surveillance Systems
---

**Background:** Wastewater-first monitoring involves a trade-off between the low cost of environmental signals and the need for high-reliability clinical data to confirm community burden, often within fixed-input or generic feature acquisition frameworks.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved need to generalize the Bayesian Selective Latent Inference framework to handle nonstationary evidence costs, incorporate richer source-policy signals, and support online recalibration of abstention thresholds in dynamic, multi-pathogen surveillance environments.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing how selective inference systems adapt to evolving public health reporting environments and diverse pathogen characteristics is essential for moving beyond static, benchmark-constrained evaluations to robust, scalable deployments.

**Evidence:** Real deployments may require nonstationary evidence costs, richer source-policy signals, different abstention thresholds, and online recalibration as reporting pipelines change.
