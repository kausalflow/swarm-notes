title: Universality of helicity threshold
---

**Background:** The ratio of current-carrying to total relative magnetic helicity is frequently proposed as a proxy for identifying the onset of solar eruptivity, with a proposed threshold value often cited near 0.29.

**Question / Future Work:** The universality of the current-carrying helicity ratio threshold remains unclear due to the variability in active region properties and the distinct evolutionary pathways that result in eruptive versus stable configurations. Future research is required to determine whether reliable thresholds exist or if eruptive criteria must be case-specific based on magnetic geometry and boundary conditions.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing objective metrics for solar eruption forecasting is a fundamental challenge; determining the limits of current helicity-based proxies is critical for advancing space weather prediction.

**Evidence:** However, the extent to which |HJ|/|HV|gtrsim 0.3 represents a universal eruptivity criterion remains an open question.
