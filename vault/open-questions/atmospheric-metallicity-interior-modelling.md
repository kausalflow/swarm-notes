title: Atmospheric Metallicity and Interior Modelling
---

**Background:** Characterizing the bulk atmospheric metallicity and internal heat of gas giant exoplanets involves significant degeneracies between tidal dissipation, envelope enrichment, and core properties. Improved observational constraints on these parameters are needed to refine interior structure models.

**Question / Future Work:** High-resolution atmospheric spectroscopic measurements are required to constrain bulk atmospheric metallicity and resolve degeneracies in planetary interior models. This is essential for understanding the relative contributions of tidal inflation versus formation-driven metal enrichment in warm Jupiters.

**Why It Matters:** Breaking these modeling degeneracies is critical for accurate inference of planetary formation conditions and long-term dynamical evolution.
