title: Human-AI Audit Workload Optimization
---

**Background:** Financial institutions require auditable, evidence-backed reports derived from high-stakes predictive models to support human monitoring of sparse, critical events. Maintaining numerical fidelity and preventing unsupported claims in automated reporting remains a persistent challenge that necessitates robust analyst-in-the-loop workflows.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved need to optimize human-in-the-loop audit protocols to balance human review labor with the requirement for rigorous verification of machine-generated narratives. Future research is required to evaluate how structured evidence reporting workflows scale across diverse financial segments and whether specific audit protocols can effectively detect explanation errors while optimizing oversight capacity.

**Why It Matters:** Essential for establishing accountability and trust in high-stakes financial surveillance systems where the efficiency of human oversight is a bottleneck.
