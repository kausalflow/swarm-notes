title: Scenario Processing Efficiency for Scheduling
---

**Background:** Generating multiple future scenarios for resource allocation introduces significant computational overhead and decision complexity.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing computationally efficient methods to condense or rank a set of generated scenarios is critical for real-time scheduling. Research is needed into automated scenario reduction, clustering-based selection, and distributionally robust scheduling objectives that maintain decision robustness without full scenario enumeration.

**Why It Matters:** The 'curse of dimensionality' in scenario space is a primary bottleneck for deploying generative-AI-assisted scheduling in real-time environments.

**Evidence:** Multiple generated scenarios can expose the scheduler to uncertainty-aware information, but they also raise the question of how this information should be exploited efficiently.
