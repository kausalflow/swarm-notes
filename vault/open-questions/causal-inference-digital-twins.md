title: Causal Inference in Digital Twins
---

**Background:** Clinical predictive models for chronic disease progression often rely on observational data, which limits the ability to infer the effects of hypothetical interventions. Current what-if analysis tools in these frameworks typically provide model-based scenario exploration rather than true causal inference.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop frameworks that transition from observational what-if scenario exploration to causal inference, allowing for a more accurate estimation of how interventions might influence disease progression trajectories in patient-specific digital twin models.

**Why It Matters:** Moving from observational to causal scenario analysis is critical for clinical decision support, as it would allow clinicians to better understand the potential impact of different treatment paths on patient outcomes.

**Evidence:** feature-level what-if perturbations provide model-based scenario exploration rather than causal intervention effects.
