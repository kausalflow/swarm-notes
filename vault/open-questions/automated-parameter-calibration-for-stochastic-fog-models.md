title: Automated Calibration of Fog Models
---

**Background:** Stochastic-deterministic models based on the Ising model are used to simulate subgrid-scale processes like cloud formation, but their reliance on parameterized external forcing and atmospheric variables often requires manual tuning for specific geographical locations.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop automated, data-driven methods to determine the key Ising-model parameters—such as local interaction strength and external potential—to improve the model's scalability and its ability to adapt autonomously to diverse geographic regions with varying meteorological regimes.

**Why It Matters:** Manual parameter tuning significantly limits the operational utility and generalizability of physical-stochastic hybrid forecasting models.

**Evidence:** These parameters were verified to accurately predict fog onset and dissipation by trial and error through comparison with observations.
