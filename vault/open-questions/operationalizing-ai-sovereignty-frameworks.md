title: Operationalizing AI Sovereignty Frameworks
---

**Background:** The precise requirements and infrastructure configurations for achieving AI sovereignty in different sectors remain undefined. Currently, policy makers lack a clear operational framework to determine which application areas necessitate domestic sovereign control versus reliance on international commercial providers.

**Question / Future Work:** Establishing a framework that defines AI sovereignty across different risk tiers and application sectors. This includes determining the criteria for domestic development versus procurement, identifying required technical and security assurances, and resolving the balance between public and private sector roles in ensuring sovereign access to compute and models.

**Why It Matters:** Without clear operational definitions of AI sovereignty, nations risk misallocating resources on unscalable projects or creating dangerous strategic dependencies, leaving them vulnerable to geopolitical coercion.
