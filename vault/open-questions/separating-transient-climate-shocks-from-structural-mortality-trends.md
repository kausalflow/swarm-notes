title: Separating Climate Shocks from Trends
---

**Background:** Climate–mortality models often struggle to differentiate between the short-term impact of extreme weather events and long-term structural changes in mortality. Determining the most effective ways to separate these transient, climate-driven spikes from structural mortality shifts remains a significant challenge for predictive accuracy.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is needed to develop robust methodologies that can explicitly disentangle transient, climate-induced mortality shocks from long-term demographic shifts, preventing bias in insurance liability valuation and Solvency II risk assessments.

**Why It Matters:** The separation of structural from shock-driven trends is essential for both interpretability and the accuracy of long-term longevity-linked liability projections.

**Evidence:** A further limitation of mortality forecasting models is their limited ability to distinguish between structural mortality changes and temporary deviations... Such effects may bias long-term mortality forecasts used in Solvency II and related risk assessment frameworks.
