title: Temporal Alignment of Media and Disaster Data
---

**Background:** Temporal alignment between international news coverage and formal disaster databases is often complicated by reporting delays, variations in definitions of disaster onset, and the tendency of news to revisit past events.

**Question / Future Work:** Establishing robust temporal alignment between news-based event detection and disaster databases remains a challenge, as databases often use specific onset dates that may not correspond with the primary windows of international media attention. Further research is required to develop refined, content-aware alignment methods that account for retrospective news coverage, recurring discussions of the same disaster, and the inherent inconsistencies in how disasters are registered across different sources.

**Why It Matters:** Temporal misalignment hinders the automated validation of news datasets against authoritative disaster records, limiting the ability to draw reliable conclusions about how specific disaster characteristics drive international media attention.

**Evidence:** Many cases are ambiguous and 4 are incorrect for S2iD; only 4 of the 14 aligned are clear matches. Some news events should genuinely not align temporally with database entries because they refer back to prior events, such as the Brumadinho disaster.
