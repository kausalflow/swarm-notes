title: Modeling dynamic disaggregation proportions
---

**Background:** In probabilistic top-down hierarchical forecasting, historical disaggregation proportions are typically modeled under an assumption of approximate stationarity to simplify estimation. Accounting for more complex temporal dynamics in these proportions remains an open challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigating methods to incorporate time-varying parameters, trends, or seasonality into the modeling of disaggregation proportions within probabilistic top-down sampling is necessary to capture dynamic changes in how lower-level series contribute to upper-level aggregates over time.

**Why It Matters:** The current assumption of near-stationarity limits the method's ability to adapt to non-stationary environments, which are common in retail and supply chain contexts where demand patterns shift significantly over time.
