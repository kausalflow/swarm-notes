title: Functional Dependence in Drift-Based Ergodicity
---

**Background:** Functional dependence measures provide a framework for analyzing time series stability, but their applicability to non-contractive nonlinear autoregressive models remains complex. Many such models are proven ergodic through methods such as Foster–Lyapunov drift conditions and minorization arguments rather than simple contractivity.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate whether the natural innovation representation is compatible with functional dependence techniques for nonlinear autoregressive models where ergodicity is established via Foster–Lyapunov drift conditions and minorization, extending the applicability of these measures to threshold autoregressive processes.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing if common ergodic models support natural innovation-based dependence measures is crucial for extending functional dependence methods to a broader class of practical nonlinear time series models.

**Evidence:** Whether the natural innovation representation is compatible with functional dependence techniques in this broader context appears to be an interesting open problem.
