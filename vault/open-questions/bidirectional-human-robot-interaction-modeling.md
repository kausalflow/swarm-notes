title: Bidirectional Human-Robot Interaction Modeling
---

**Background:** Autonomous navigation among humans in 3D environments often assumes that human motion is independent of the robot's actions.

**Question / Future Work:** Extending navigation models to explicitly account for bidirectional human-robot interactions—where the human’s trajectory adapts to the robot’s behavior—remains an open challenge for full 3D body motion.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental challenge for truly social, proactive robot navigation in crowded environments, as the independence assumption is often violated in realistic human-robot encounters.

**Evidence:** Also, our framework assumes that human motion is unaffected by the MAV’s behavior. While this assumption simplifies planning, humans may react to nearby robots and adapt their motion. Modeling such bidirectional human-robot interactions has only been studied for 2D root trajectory generation [42] and remains an open challenge for full 3D body motion.
