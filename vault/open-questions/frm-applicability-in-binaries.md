title: FRM Applicability in Binaries
---

**Background:** The Frequency Ratio Method (FRM) is a standard diagnostic tool used to identify pulsation modes in γ Doradus stars based on the ratios of detected oscillation frequencies. Standard implementations assume the pulsating star is a single, isolated, slowly rotating object.

**Question / Future Work:** The applicability of the Frequency Ratio Method for mode identification in stars within interacting close-binary systems—particularly those characterized by fast rotation and mass transfer—remains poorly understood and lacks validated theoretical frameworks. The distortion of pulsation spectra by tidal forces and mass exchange complicates the identification of non-radial oscillation modes, leading to non-unique solutions and potential inaccuracies in determining the internal physical state of such stars.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing this limitation is crucial for advancing asteroseismology in the context of binary stars, as it prevents the accurate characterization of interior structures in the most complex and physically interesting stellar systems.
