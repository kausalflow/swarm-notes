title: Impact of SED Template Incompleteness
---

**Background:** Template-based photometric redshift estimation methods rely on finite libraries of spectral energy distribution (SED) templates, which may not fully capture the diversity of real galaxy spectra, leading to systematic errors in redshift inference.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate the extent to which the incompleteness of existing SED template libraries contributes to systematic photometric redshift failures, particularly for faint galaxies where narrow-band signals are weak and the impact of template-prior degeneracies is amplified. This includes assessing how template mismatch influences outlier rates and redshift accumulation points.

**Why It Matters:** Template-prior degeneracies and SED library incompleteness are fundamental bottlenecks in current photo-z algorithms, especially critical for high-resolution narrow-band cosmological surveys.

**Evidence:** Another contributing factor is galaxies with extreme and complex SEDs, for which the template library provides potentially imperfect matches. ... In this study, we have not isolated this effect [emission-line modelling mismatch] in the present analysis, but it may act in combination with the prior-driven redshift focussing.
