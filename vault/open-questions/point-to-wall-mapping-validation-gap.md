title: Point-to-wall mapping validation gap
---

**Background:** Remote sensing models for land cover classification are often validated using point-level samples, which mask the challenges of deploying models over continuous geographic areas.

**Question / Future Work:** The transition from point-level validation to wall-to-wall thematic mapping remains a critical bottleneck for operational reliability, as it requires addressing spatial consistency and large-scale validation beyond sparse ground-truth samples.

**Why It Matters:** This identifies a fundamental operational gap between proof-of-concept models and the utility of models in real-world large-scale environmental monitoring projects.

**Evidence:** Wall-to-wall mapping requires applying the model to image grids and validating the resulting map with independent field or visual interpretation data.
