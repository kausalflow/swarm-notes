title: Incorporating Granular Road Attributes
---

**Background:** Traffic forecasting models often struggle with cross-city generalization due to variations in urban road structures and traffic behavior. While modern models incorporate static road masks, they often lack sufficient granular information to ensure long-term physical consistency of predictions.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate the integration of granular, semantically rich road attributes—such as traffic signal dynamics, regulatory speed limits, and specific lane-level properties—to improve the physical grounding and structural fidelity of city-wide traffic movie predictions across heterogeneous urban environments.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses a core bottleneck in traffic modeling: how to move beyond generic spatial masks to leverage structured urban regulatory data for more physically and logically sound forecasts.
