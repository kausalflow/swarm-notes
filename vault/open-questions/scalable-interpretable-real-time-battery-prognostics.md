title: Scalable and Interpretable Battery Prognostics
---

**Background:** Reliable battery State of Health (SoH) estimation is increasingly dependent on the ability to process raw, irregularly sampled sensor time-series data without resorting to lossy feature engineering or sequence truncation.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is required to enhance the scalability, interpretability, and real-time performance of deep sequence models for battery health monitoring, specifically in resource-constrained environments like battery management systems.

**Why It Matters:** Transitioning to more efficient, real-time-capable models is a critical bottleneck for deploying deep learning in edge-deployed battery management systems.

**Evidence:** Overall, TIDSIT establishes a strong foundation for reliable battery health monitoring and paves the way for future research into scalable, interpretable, and real-time prognostics using deep sequence models.
