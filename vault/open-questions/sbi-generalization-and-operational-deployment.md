title: Generalization and operational deployment of SBI
---

**Background:** Amortized simulation-based inference (SBI) methods, such as neural posterior estimation (NPE), have shown potential for rapid Bayesian calibration of epidemiological models. However, the generalizability of these learned approximations compared to MCMC across different model structures, pathogens, and sequential, near-real-time forecasting settings remains an open challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to evaluate the generalizability of SBI across diverse compartmental models, data sources, and infectious disease scenarios, particularly in the context of maintaining parameter identifiability and posterior stability in sequential, near-real-time forecasting.

**Why It Matters:** This question addresses the critical need to validate the robustness and operational utility of machine learning-based inference in public health, moving beyond retrospective analyses to active, sequential forecasting.
