title: Improving Dynamic Hypergraph Stability
---

**Background:** Spatiotemporal forecasting tasks often model dynamic spatial dependencies, yet current methods frequently struggle with the non-stationarity of these interactions over time.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing stable dynamic graph construction mechanisms that can effectively decouple intrinsic functional node similarity from external, time-varying noise remains a key bottleneck in achieving reliable long-horizon forecasting.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing stability in learned dynamic graphs is critical for the generalization and interpretability of spatiotemporal models in non-stationary environments.
