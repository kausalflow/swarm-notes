title: Impact of fragmented health data
---

**Background:** Predicting future disease trajectories from electronic health records requires addressing observational gaps where events occurring outside a specific health system network remain unobserved. Longitudinal completeness remains a fundamental challenge in models relying on fragmented healthcare data.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to determine how to better account for or integrate data from multiple, non-connected health systems to improve long-range predictive accuracy in the presence of incomplete longitudinal patient histories.

**Why It Matters:** Data fragmentation is a persistent, systemic barrier to the performance and generalizability of clinical forecasting models.
