title: MGIC threshold and validation
---

**Background:** Model selection in multidimensional Gaussian Processes (multi-GPs) lacks a universally established framework for determining the optimal combination of ancillary time-series variables used to constrain target signals.

**Question / Future Work:** The determination of precise thresholds for the information-theoretic criteria remains an open problem, as does the relationship between these specialized model selection metrics and traditional cross-validation performance. Further research is required to provide a rigorous formal investigation of these penalty thresholds and their statistical consistency in multi-variate Gaussian process settings.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing formal thresholds and clarifying the relationship with cross-validation is necessary to validate the robustness and statistical consistency of the model selection framework across diverse scientific applications.

**Evidence:** A formal investigation of the MGIC_rv threshold as well as its relationship with cross-validation in multi-GP settings is left for future work.
