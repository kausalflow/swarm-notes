title: Hybridizing Mechanistic and ML Models
---

**Background:** Epidemiological modeling often relies on compartmental systems that frequently lack integration with high-dimensional data streams such as climate variability, fine-scale urban infrastructure, and human mobility patterns.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a critical need to develop hybrid modeling frameworks that integrate machine learning techniques with mechanistic compartmental models to improve predictive accuracy for disease transmission. Future work should focus on incorporating high-dimensional datasets—including climate, urban infrastructure, and fine-scale human mobility—to enable dynamic model calibration and forecasting that is more adaptive to changing environmental and social conditions.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing these gaps is essential for transitioning from static, population-level predictions to adaptive, precision-based public health interventions that can account for the heterogeneity observed in urban disease spread.
