title: Multidimensional Hybrid Thermal Forecasting
---

**Background:** Lithium-ion batteries are prone to thermal runaway under abusive conditions, and while physics-informed machine learning models have shown promise in predicting these events, they typically rely on simplified thermal models or fixed physical parameters.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to extend physics-informed forecasting frameworks to include higher-dimensional heat transfer modeling and hybrid electrochemical-thermal representations, which would enhance predictive accuracy under spatially distributed thermal gradients and complex failure modes.

**Why It Matters:** Current models often simplify thermal dynamics to 1D representations, which may fail to capture the complex, spatially distributed thermal gradients and coupled electrochemical-thermal failure mechanisms present during real-world thermal runaway.
