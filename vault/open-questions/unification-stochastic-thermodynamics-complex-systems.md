title: Unification of stochastic thermodynamics
---

**Background:** Stochastic thermodynamics provides a framework for quantifying irreversibility and thermodynamic dissipation in fluctuating, non-equilibrium systems, but its traditional foundations, which assume local detailed balance, encounter significant limitations when applied to macroscopic or complex systems where these properties decouple.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains an open question whether a unified formulation of stochastic thermodynamics exists that can provide robust and quantitatively meaningful insights for macroscopic or non-physical systems, particularly where the connection between statistical irreversibility and energy dissipation is no longer direct. Researchers are tasked with determining if the thermodynamic concepts developed for small, fluctuating machines can be rigorously extended to complex systems across diverse disciplines, or if these applications require fundamentally new theoretical foundations.

**Why It Matters:** This question addresses the fundamental scope and validity of stochastic thermodynamics as it attempts to move from a niche subfield of statistical physics to a general framework for complex systems. Establishing whether 'irreversibility' serves as a useful proxy for 'dissipation' in macroscopic, non-physical contexts is essential for the future of the field.

**Evidence:** Whether a unified formulation encompassing these diverse extensions exists remains an open question. In particular, it remains to be seen to what extent methods from stochastic thermodynamics can provide robust and quantitatively meaningful insights in macroscopic or non-physical contexts, where the connection to energy dissipation is no longer direct.
