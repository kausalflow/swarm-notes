title: Dynamics of Higher-Order Dependencies
---

**Background:** Hypergraph representation learning methods typically rely on prior knowledge to construct graph structures, which is not always available in multivariate time series applications. Integrating higher-order structural information from time series in an automated, data-driven manner remains a significant challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop more robust methods for deriving dynamic hypergraph representations from time series without relying on prior knowledge. Current methods show varying performance depending on the stochasticity and the extent of higher-order dependencies in the data, necessitating further research into identifying when and why higher-order structural relationships are more informative than pairwise ones.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the conditions under which hypergraph-based approaches provide predictive advantages over simpler, graph-based or purely temporal models is essential for broader adoption in time series forecasting.
