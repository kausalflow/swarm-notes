title: NISQ Hardware Stability in Hybrid Forecasting
---

**Background:** Time-series analysis often relies on heuristic-based order selection and parameter optimization, which may be improved by incorporating quantum-assisted principles. However, bridging the gap between theoretical quantum-inspired algorithms and their execution on noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) hardware remains a major challenge in hybrid modeling.

**Question / Future Work:** Evaluate the stability, performance, and convergence of hybrid quantum-classical forecasting models when deployed on physical NISQ hardware, accounting for noise, decoherence, and connectivity constraints not present in simulation backends.

**Why It Matters:** Practical viability of quantum-enhanced forecasting is contingent on moving beyond simulation-based assumptions to address hardware-level noise and architecture limitations.
