title: Higher-order technological convergence prediction
---

**Background:** Innovation is conceptualized as the recombination of existing technological elements into novel combinations, a process bounded by an \"adjacent possible\" that expands as new combinations are realized. Predicting these future combinations remains a central challenge in science and policy.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is needed to develop methods for modeling multi-way technological convergences involving three or more technologies, as the simultaneous alignment of multiple technological vocabularies may offer a more complete map of the technological frontier than bilateral analysis.

**Why It Matters:** Moving from bilateral to multi-way analysis is essential for capturing the complex, systemic nature of innovation where synergistic interaction between multiple components drives progress.

**Evidence:** we have restricted attention to pairwise combinations, whereas innovation frequently involves higher-order assemblies of three or more technologies whose joint actualization cannot be reduced to their pairwise precursors.
