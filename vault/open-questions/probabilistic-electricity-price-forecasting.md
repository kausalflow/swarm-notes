title: Probabilistic electricity price forecasting
---

**Background:** Electricity price forecasting models typically rely on point predictions, which provide single-value estimates for future electricity prices. However, these models struggle to quantify the uncertainty associated with rare and extreme price spikes, particularly in markets undergoing structural changes.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research should shift from point forecasting toward developing probabilistic forecasting frameworks that better characterize the uncertainty and volatility of electricity prices, especially under stressed market regimes where traditional point estimates are most prone to failure.

**Why It Matters:** Current models, while accurate on average, are consistently vulnerable to extreme, fast-moving price spikes that cause significant operational and financial risk. Probabilistic forecasting is essential for risk management and robust decision-making in volatile energy markets.
