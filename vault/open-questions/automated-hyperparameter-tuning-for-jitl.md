title: Automated hyperparameter tuning for JITL
---

**Background:** Many adaptive forecasting frameworks, such as just-in-time learning (JITL), rely on manually configured hyperparameters for neighbor retrieval, clustering, and local model adaptation.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing automated, data-driven approaches to configure retrieval and adaptation hyperparameters is essential for ensuring scalability and generalization across diverse temporal domains. Future research should investigate meta-learning or dynamic optimization techniques to replace fixed heuristic choices in adaptive forecasting pipelines.

**Why It Matters:** Manual hyperparameter tuning limits the robustness and transferability of adaptive models to new operating regimes or diverse experimental datasets.
