title: Scalability of action-based forecasting
---

**Background:** Financial markets exhibit extreme non-stationarity and complex dependencies on heterogeneous information sources. While models that unify qualitative reasoning with quantitative forecasting exist, their ability to scale to high-frequency data, broader asset classes, and long-horizon macro-financial shifts remains limited.

**Question / Future Work:** It is unclear how to extend action-based reasoning frameworks to accommodate high-frequency trading data, diverse asset classes, and multi-asset dependencies while maintaining robust uncertainty estimation and interpretability. Defining richer, more flexible action spaces that capture market regimes and macroeconomic shifts is a central, unresolved challenge.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing these limitations is critical for evolving from narrow stock-specific forecasting toward comprehensive, cross-domain financial market modeling using LLMs.
