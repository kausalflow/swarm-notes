title: Empirical Validation of Higher-Order Interactions
---

**Background:** Higher-order interactions (HOIs) are often inferred from time-series data by extending generalized Lotka-Volterra models with non-linear terms. However, structural non-identifiability can lead to effective pairwise models that reproduce the observed dynamics while misidentifying the underlying ecological mechanisms.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop empirical strategies beyond simple time-series analysis to reliably validate the existence of higher-order interactions. This involves identifying the conditions under which higher-order descriptions are essential for predicting system responses to perturbations, and determining how to integrate auxiliary ecological priors to resolve model ambiguities.

**Why It Matters:** Misidentifying HOIs as pairwise (or vice-versa) can lead to failed predictions when communities are subjected to novel stressors or management interventions.

**Evidence:** Establishing the ecological reality of HOIs therefore requires going beyond time series. Our results advocate for coupling models with targeted empirical data... Ultimately, synthesizing these independent sources of information will answer whether we truly need a higher-order description for a given system.
