title: Robustness of Sewer Forecasting Models
---

**Background:** Urban sewer management systems frequently rely on data-driven models that must maintain performance despite fluctuating sensor availability, missing data, and potential infrastructure failures.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop more comprehensive robustness frameworks that evaluate the performance of forecasting models under non-ideal conditions, specifically addressing the impacts of noisy data, sensor outliers, and systemic data gaps in real-time sewer monitoring applications.

**Why It Matters:** Sewer overflow management is safety-critical; understanding how forecasting models degrade or recover in the presence of real-world sensor anomalies is essential for reliable decision-making.
