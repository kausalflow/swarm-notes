title: Universal Pretrained Scheduling Models
---

**Background:** Deep time series forecasting models typically rely on fixed mapping strategies, which decouple the forecasting process from evolving contextual dynamics, whereas recent research reformulates this as an adaptive scheduling task.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of large-scale, universal scheduling models pretrained on diverse time-series data remains an open challenge to enable zero-shot forecasting capabilities across new, unseen scenarios without dataset-specific retraining.

**Why It Matters:** Moving from domain-specific models to universal, pretrained models is a significant bottleneck in improving the scalability and generalization of deep time series forecasting.
