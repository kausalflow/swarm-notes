title: Unobserved land-surface state integration
---

**Background:** Earth surface dynamics are driven by both observable meteorological forcing and unobserved land-surface states such as soil moisture or sub-surface irrigation. Current models are often restricted by this partial observability when predicting outcomes under varying weather conditions.

**Question / Future Work:** Integrating hidden land-surface variables as known conditioning signals requires developing methods to assimilate ground-station measurements or sub-surface proxies into latent world models, which is fundamental to closing the gap between weather forcing and land-surface response.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing unobserved state variables is essential for improving the fidelity of forcing-response mappings in drought and agricultural yield modeling.

**Evidence:** In addition, several land-surface states remain unobserved or only partly observed, including soil moisture, irrigation, and vegetation type. These limitations also suggest useful directions for future work.
