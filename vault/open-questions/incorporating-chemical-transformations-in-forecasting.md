title: Incorporating Chemical Transformations
---

**Background:** Air quality forecasting models increasingly integrate physical transport processes with deep learning, yet these frameworks often treat species as inert tracers, ignoring the reactive nature of atmospheric pollutants. Integrating complex physical phenomena into these hybrid models remains an active challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research should investigate methods to embed chemical reaction kinetics and secondary pollutant formation into physics-informed forecasting architectures without sacrificing computational efficiency or differentiability.

**Why It Matters:** Capturing chemical transformations is critical for moving beyond simple transport modeling to accurate prediction of secondary pollutants in urban environments.

**Evidence:** The framework could be extended to incorporate additional physical mechanisms, such as chemical transformations or secondary pollutant formation, to enhance realism.
