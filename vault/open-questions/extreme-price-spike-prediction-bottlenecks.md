title: Predicting Extreme Price Spikes
---

**Background:** Electricity price forecasting (EPF) in markets with high renewable penetration is characterized by frequent, volatile, and extreme price spikes that are difficult for existing models to capture accurately.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing models that can reliably predict extreme price spike events remains an open challenge, as current loss functions (e.g., MSE, MAE) tend to prioritize average performance over rare, high-magnitude deviations. There is a need to explore methodologies that specifically address tail-end risk and extreme value dynamics in electricity price series.

**Why It Matters:** Improving the prediction of extreme price spikes is crucial for market participants to manage financial risks effectively, as these events significantly impact operational planning and profitability.

**Evidence:** During high-volatility periods, predictions tend to underestimate extreme values, which may be attributed to the use of loss functions such as MAE and MSE that prioritize average performance over rare extreme deviations.
