title: Bridging sim-to-real gaps
---

**Background:** Machine learning-based controllers in power electronics are typically trained in simulated environments, creating a potential discrepancy between the performance observed in simulation and the performance realized in physical hardware.

**Question / Future Work:** The transition from simulation-based training to deployment in physical power converters remains a significant, unresolved technical bottleneck. Future work is required to develop systematic methodologies for bridging this 'sim-to-real' gap, particularly by ensuring that stability, constraint satisfaction, and performance robustness hold true under the non-ideal, time-varying dynamics of real-world electrical systems.

**Why It Matters:** Without bridging this gap, learning-based control for power converters cannot be trusted for critical infrastructure where performance must be guaranteed despite model inaccuracies and hardware limitations.
