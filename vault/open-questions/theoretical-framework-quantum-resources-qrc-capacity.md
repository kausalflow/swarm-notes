title: Theoretical framework for QRC capacity
---

**Background:** Quantum reservoir computing has demonstrated performance gains in processing multivariate time series, yet the fundamental relationship between quantum mechanical properties and computational capacity remains theoretically incomplete.

**Question / Future Work:** A rigorous theoretical framework that establishes a definitive causal or functional link between specific quantum mechanical properties (such as entanglement or squeezing) and the computational performance of multivariate QRC systems is currently missing. Understanding this connection is essential for moving beyond empirical observations of correlations to predictive design of quantum reservoirs.

**Why It Matters:** Developing this framework is critical for the systematic design and optimization of QRC systems, allowing researchers to engineer specific quantum properties to achieve desired computational outcomes in high-dimensional temporal tasks.

**Evidence:** Nevertheless, a rigorous theoretical framework that links quantum correlations to computational capacity remains an open challenge.
