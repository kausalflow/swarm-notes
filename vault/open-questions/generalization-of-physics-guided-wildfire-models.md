title: Generalization of PGML Wildfire Models
---

**Background:** Physics-guided machine learning (PGML) approaches incorporate domain-specific governing equations, such as mass conservation or kinematic constraints, as soft penalties within the loss function to improve prediction stability. The effectiveness of these surrogate frameworks is often limited to the specific simulation environments, fuel types, or spatial scales used during training.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to systematically evaluate the generalization capabilities of physics-guided spatiotemporal fuel density models across broader environmental conditions. This requires validation against extended-length fire sequences, diverse fuel types, varying topographies, and additional environmental variables to determine if the embedded physical constraints remain robust under complex, real-world wildfire scenarios.

**Why It Matters:** Validating these models on diverse landscapes is critical for transitioning from simulated environments to practical, real-world fire management applications where constraints may interact differently with environmental complexity.

**Evidence:** Future work includes evaluating the proposed method on extended-length fire sequences and on diverse fuel types and topography.
