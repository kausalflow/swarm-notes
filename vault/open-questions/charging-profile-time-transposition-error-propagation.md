title: EV Charging Profile Time Transposition
---

**Background:** Electric vehicle (EV) charging profile forecasting requires mapping State-of-Charge (SoC) trends to power-versus-time curves, a process dependent on heterogeneous information availability such as battery capacity, arrival/departure times, and SoC. The impact of this information and the compounding errors in the time transposition process remain underexplored.

**Question / Future Work:** The time transposition of charging profiles involves converting power versus State-of-Charge (SoC) curves into power versus time curves. While variables such as battery capacity and arrival/departure SoC are known to influence this transposition, the cumulative impact of estimation errors across these variables is complex and not fully understood. Future work is needed to systematically analyze this error propagation and improve the robustness of time-domain transformations.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding error propagation in time-domain mappings is critical for the development of reliable energy management systems in electric vehicle infrastructure, as inaccurate projections lead to inefficient power scheduling and grid management.
