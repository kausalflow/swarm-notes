title: Extreme Wind Forecasting Robustness
---

**Background:** Marine wind forecasting models typically perform well in average conditions but struggle to maintain accuracy during extreme wind events at longer lead times.

**Question / Future Work:** While current transformer-based models effectively reduce errors in average marine wind conditions, they exhibit performance degradation relative to physical NWP models during high-wind intensity scenarios at lead times beyond 18 hours. Future work is required to develop techniques such as event-weighted training, oversampling of extreme events, or probabilistic architectures to improve the robustness and reliability of these corrections during extreme weather conditions.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical for operational applications where accurate extreme wind forecasting is necessary for safety, maritime navigation, and risk management.

**Evidence:** Improving performance under extreme conditions through event-weighted training or oversampling remains an important direction for future work.
