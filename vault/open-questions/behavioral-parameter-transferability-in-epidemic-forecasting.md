title: Behavioral Parameter Transferability
---

**Background:** Epidemic models frequently struggle to maintain predictive accuracy when encountering policy-induced distribution shifts due to human behavioral adaptations. While structured decomposition and physical constraints improve robustness, the transferability of these learned behavioral functions across diverse population contexts remains unresolved.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate the systematic transferability of learned behavioral compliance functions across varying geographical regions, cultural contexts, or different epidemic types, specifically addressing how to bridge the cold-start problem in limited-data scenarios without retraining from scratch.

**Why It Matters:** Improving the generalization of behavioral parameters is a key bottleneck for deploying epidemic models to real-world, novel outbreaks where initial data is sparse.
