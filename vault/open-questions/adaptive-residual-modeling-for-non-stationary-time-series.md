title: Adaptive Residual Modeling for Non-Stationary Time Series
---

**Background:** In transformer-based time series forecasting, single-stage models often exhibit performance ceilings caused by systematic residual biases that remain even after convergence, often being treated as irreducible noise.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains unclear how to adaptively refine the residual-learning process to handle complex non-stationary temporal dynamics or evolving external covariates, especially when these patterns are highly non-linear or shift over time. Extending the framework to incorporate exogenous variables and dynamic sensitivity to evolving data properties is a critical next step.

**Why It Matters:** Adapting residual modeling to dynamic environments and external drivers is critical for ensuring the robustness of time series forecasting in real-world scenarios where data properties are non-stationary.
