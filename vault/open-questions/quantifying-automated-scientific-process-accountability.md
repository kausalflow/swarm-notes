title: Quantifying Automated Scientific Accountability
---

**Background:** AI scientists use language models to perform scientific workflows including literature review, idea generation, and experimental validation. The reasoning connecting these steps often remains implicit within model inference, making it difficult to audit the scientific process.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop quantitative process metrics to assess the accountability of automated scientific discovery, specifically metrics like the 'claim-drift rate' (where runnable artifacts fail to support the originally claimed mechanism) and 'attribution completeness' to ensure scientific conclusions are robustly linked to prior evidence.

**Why It Matters:** Without rigorous process metrics, it is difficult to determine if automated research agents are producing genuine scientific knowledge or merely plausible-sounding conclusions decoupled from empirical truth.

**Evidence:** Future work should therefore strengthen claim-level provenance, develop quantitative process metrics such as claim-drift rate and attribution completeness, and evaluate the harness across broader and less curated research settings.
