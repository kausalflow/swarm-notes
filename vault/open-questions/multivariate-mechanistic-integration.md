title: Multivariate and Mechanistic Forecasting
---

**Background:** Epidemic forecasting often utilizes univariate time series from isolated locations, failing to capture the rich underlying structure of epidemiological systems that include spatial coupling and multivariate data streams.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is required to develop forecasting baselines that effectively ingest heterogeneous data types, such as spatial dependencies and explicit mechanistic constraints, to improve biological consistency and decision support accuracy.

**Why It Matters:** This represents a critical gap in advancing beyond black-box time-series forecasting toward scientifically robust, mechanistic public health decision support.
