title: Scalable Sensing for Collaborative Tutoring
---

**Background:** Adaptive learning systems often rely on specialized sensors like eye-trackers to model collaborative cognitive states, which creates barriers to real-world, large-scale deployment.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to determine if adaptive models of dyadic regulation can maintain their predictive and instructional effectiveness using more scalable, non-intrusive signals, such as interaction logs or ambient sensing, as alternatives to specialized eye-tracking infrastructure.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the major deployment bottleneck for AI-driven collaborative systems in educational and industrial environments.
