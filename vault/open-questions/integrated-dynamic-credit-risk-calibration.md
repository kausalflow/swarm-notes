title: Calibrating integrated risk models
---

**Background:** Credit risk stress-testing frameworks often decouple loan production from credit risk assessment, limiting their ability to model complex systemic interdependencies. Incorporating dynamic interactions between loan attributes and macroeconomic covariates remains an open challenge for industry-scale risk management.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is needed to integrate complex interdependencies between loan attributes (e.g., correlations between principal amount, interest rate, and risk level) and incorporate more flexible receipt structures, such as partial repayments, re-advances, and re-capitalizations. Furthermore, calibrating this simulation-driven approach to real-world data by replacing synthetic distributions with models driven by systematic macroeconomic covariates is essential for moving toward industry-ready application.

**Why It Matters:** Current credit risk models often simplify these dynamics, leading to inaccurate stress-test results in real-world retail portfolios. Bridging the gap between simulation-based theoretical frameworks and empirical, data-driven applications is critical for robust financial risk management.
