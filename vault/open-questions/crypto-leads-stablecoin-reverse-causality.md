title: Reverse Causality Testing and Safe-Haven
---

**Background:** Cryptocurrency markets exhibit complex dynamics where the movement of liquidity, often held in stablecoins, acts as a leading indicator for price changes in the main digital assets.

**Question / Future Work:** The current analysis demonstrated that stablecoin upside volatility and volume lead cryptocurrency volatility, supporting the "dry powder" hypothesis. However, the symmetric expectation—that stablecoin downside volatility should lead cryptocurrency upside volatility as dry powder is liquidated for rallies—was not observed, suggesting a limitation in the daily data frequency. Future work should test the reverse direction of causality to ascertain if and how cryptocurrency market conditions lead stablecoin market dynamics, specifically looking for evidence of the safe-haven effect where cryptocurrency downside volatility leads to an increase in stablecoin upside volatility.
