title: Microdata Inflation Forecasting Limits
---

**Background:** Macroeconomic forecasting models using granular micro-level data often contend with the structural mismatch between high-dimensional features and limited, non-stationary aggregate observations.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the optimal threshold and conditions under which microeconomic heterogeneity provides consistent predictive value for aggregate macroeconomic indicators remains a significant challenge. Future research is needed to generalize the utility of these features across varying shock magnitudes and to establish robust selection criteria that avoid overfitting in non-stationary economic regimes.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the trade-off between utilizing big-data micro sources and the statistical limits of macroeconomic forecasting, crucial for model reliability in real-world policy contexts.
