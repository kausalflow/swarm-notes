title: Improving Physical Interpretability in SSM Models
---

**Background:** Machine learning models for surface soil moisture (SSM) retrieval from SAR imagery are often data-driven and lack explicit physical interpretability, limiting their robustness and ability to generalize across diverse environments. Current methodologies frequently struggle to separate contributions from varying soil textures, vegetation covers, and surface roughness within complex, heterogeneous landscapes.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to develop more physically informed or interpretable machine learning frameworks for SSM retrieval. Specifically, there is a requirement to explore the integration of pretrained foundation models—such as self-supervised models for radar and multimodal EO data—with fine-tuning on specific ground-truth datasets to improve the transferability, robustness, and physical consistency of SSM estimation across diverse environmental conditions.

**Why It Matters:** Standard machine learning approaches in remote sensing often suffer from overfitting to local training data. Addressing this through physical constraints or foundation models is critical for creating scalable, operational monitoring systems that can function in unseen or varied geographical settings.
