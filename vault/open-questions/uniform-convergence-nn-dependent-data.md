title: Uniform convergence for NN radii
---

**Background:** Nearest-neighbor methods are typically analyzed for independent data, but many real-world applications involve dependent data sequences. It remains to be determined whether and how a global, uniform convergence result for nearest-neighbor radii can be established across the entire support of the distribution under dependent sampling.

**Question / Future Work:** The current research focuses on pointwise convergence and moment bounds at fixed points under mixing conditions. A substantial open research question is the generalization of these results to uniform convergence over the entire support, which would require more advanced empirical process theory to provide global guarantees for k-NN estimators and graph-based models under dependence.

**Why It Matters:** Uniform convergence is a core prerequisite for establishing global consistency and risk bounds for nonparametric machine learning estimators operating on dependent data sequences.

**Evidence:** Extending the theory to uniform convergence over the support is interesting... we leave the general uniform theory to future work.
