title: Architectural vs Feature Design Limitations
---

**Background:** Train delay prediction performance is typically evaluated using heterogeneous datasets, task formulations, and protocols, which complicates cross-study comparisons.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a lack of consensus on the most effective architectural approach for train delay prediction, as competitive learning-based models show regime-specific strengths, suggesting that further progress depends on better feature design and problem-specific modeling choices rather than architecture alone.

**Why It Matters:** Identifying whether progress in this field is bottlenecked by model architecture or by data/feature limitations is crucial for guiding future methodological investments in logistics forecasting.

**Evidence:** the strongest learning-based models remain close to each other in absolute performance... architectural changes alone do not lead to dramatic gains on this benchmark.
