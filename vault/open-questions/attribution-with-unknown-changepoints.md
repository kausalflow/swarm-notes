title: Attribution with unknown change-points
---

**Background:** Attribution procedures for multivariate change-point detection are typically designed under the assumption that the true number of change-points is known. In practical scenarios, however, both the number and locations of change-points must be estimated from data.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing and theoretically analyzing attribution procedures that remain robust to errors in the initial estimation of the number of change-points remains an open problem. The impact of model selection uncertainty on the validity of post hoc attribution has not been fully characterized.

**Why It Matters:** Since true change-point counts are rarely known a priori, attribution methods that do not account for the uncertainty of the detection phase may lead to unreliable interpretations.
