**Background:** Quantum machine learning models, such as quantum autoencoders, are increasingly explored for semi-supervised anomaly detection. However, the practical utility of these models on real quantum hardware remains constrained by noise and scalability.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a significant gap between the performance of quantum autoencoders in simulated environments and their viability on real-world quantum hardware, necessitating further research to address noise sensitivity, hardware-native scalability, and the development of robust error mitigation strategies.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the gap between simulated and hardware-based performance is crucial for the field of Quantum Machine Learning (QML) to move from theoretical demonstration to practical, real-world utility.
