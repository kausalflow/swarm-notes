title: Physical drivers of turbulence forecasting variability
---

**Background:** Optical turbulence forecasting performance varies between sites, and current baseline methods show significantly higher error at sites with greater atmospheric variability. The exact physical mechanisms driving observed differences in baseline forecast performance between top-class observatory sites remain unquantified.

**Question / Future Work:** While observational data indicates that forecasting performance for optical turbulence parameters differs across sites, there is no established physical model explaining why turbulence or forecast performance stability varies between specific observatories. Identifying the physical drivers of these inter-site differences is necessary to improve the generalizability of forecasting models and to understand the limitations of baseline performance thresholds at different geographical locations.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the physical drivers of forecast performance variability is critical to determine if site-specific forecasting improvements are possible or if performance is fundamentally limited by local atmospheric dynamics.

**Evidence:** As a first guess, one might assume that the explanation for what we observe is that turbulence at VLT is more stable than at LBT, but this is merely a hypothesis, and it would be interesting to identify a physical explanation for such a difference.
