title: Realistic Thermodynamics in CME Models
---

**Background:** Coronal mass ejection (CME) models currently utilize simplified thermodynamic descriptions, typically assuming a polytropic relation with a constant index to approximate the energy transport in the solar corona and heliosphere.

**Question / Future Work:** Incorporating more realistic, non-adiabatic thermodynamic processes and energy balance equations into magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) frameworks is necessary to improve the accuracy of simulated solar wind properties and CME evolution. This includes moving beyond polytropic assumptions to better model the heating, cooling, and expansion dynamics of the solar wind.

**Why It Matters:** Refining the thermodynamic treatment is critical for accurately modeling the background solar wind environment through which CMEs propagate, as it directly impacts the density, temperature, and velocity structures that influence CME kinematics and shock formation.

**Evidence:** For the thermodynamic description, we currently adopt a simple polytropic assumption, in which the energy (temperature) equation is closed by choosing a specific value for the ratio of specific heats γ. Different values of γ are used in the three simulation domains... More realistic thermodynamic treatments will be incorporated in future model developments.
