title: Inferring latent system state
---

**Background:** Gravitational instabilities in heterogeneous geomaterials evolve from distributed damage to catastrophic failure, but the internal mechanical state driving these transitions is not directly observable. Monitoring and modeling efforts currently rely on indirect proxies, such as seismic activity and surface deformation, to infer system state and predict failure.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a lack of established, universal methods for translating indirect, multi-sensor observables into reliable estimates of the latent system state. Developing techniques to synthesize heterogeneous datasets—which are often influenced by non-unique physical mechanisms—into a robust, real-time assessment of a system's proximity to instability remains a key, unresolved methodological challenge.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the gap between observed signals and the underlying latent state is the fundamental bottleneck for transitioning from descriptive hazard monitoring to reliable, proactive failure forecasting in natural systems.
