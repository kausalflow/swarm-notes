title: Standardizing Metric-Based Statistical Significance
---

**Background:** The statistical significance of performance improvements in time series forecasting models often depends on the specific choice of evaluation metrics and the forecast horizon. Current empirical evaluations frequently lack a rigorous, unified statistical framework to validate these improvements across diverse datasets.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to elucidate the behavior of various evaluation metrics and to rigorously investigate the statistical significance of including geometric patterns, such as those derived from visibility graphs and graph neural networks, across a broader and more diverse range of time series datasets. Establishing robust, standardized criteria for evaluating model improvements remains an open challenge.

**Why It Matters:** The study identifies that statistical outcomes are highly sensitive to metric and horizon choices, indicating that current methods for validating model superiority may be dataset-dependent or inconsistent; this limits the generalizability of findings in financial time series forecasting.

**Evidence:** Future research endeavors will concentrate on elucidating the behavior of evaluation metrics and exploring the statistical significance of the inclusion of geometric patterns across diverse time series datasets.
