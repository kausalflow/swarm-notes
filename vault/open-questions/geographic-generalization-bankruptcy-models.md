title: Geographic generalization of bankruptcy models
---

**Background:** Corporate financial distress prediction often encounters challenges regarding the geographic generalizability of models due to variations in accounting standards, ownership structures, and regulatory insolvency regimes.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the extent to which distress prediction models developed on a specific regional dataset (such as the Visegrád Group) can generalize and maintain performance across economies with distinct financial and legal environments remains an open research question.

**Why It Matters:** Assessing geographic robustness is critical for establishing the reliability and utility of financial foundation models in cross-border investment and regulatory applications.

**Evidence:** Although these countries provide a diverse regional setting, models trained or selected on V4FinBench may not transfer to economies with substantially different accounting standards, reporting practices, ownership structures, or insolvency regimes.
