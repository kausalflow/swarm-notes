title: Nonlinear noisy network transitions
---

**Background:** For general high-dimensional network dynamics driven by noise, the system may operate in either equilibrium or non-equilibrium steady states (NESS) depending on the symmetries of the connection and noise covariance matrices. The relationship between the steady-state probability current, effective potential, and the breaking of time-reversal symmetry remains a fundamental challenge in non-equilibrium statistical physics.

**Question / Future Work:** Higher-order nonlinear effects in noisy network dynamics can lead to multiple coexisting stable fixed points and non-Gaussian behavior in the steady-state distribution. A systematic investigation into how these multiple distinct steady states relate to equilibrium or non-equilibrium phase transitions is needed.

**Why It Matters:** This is a critical area because the provided framework is limited to the linearized regime around a single stable fixed point. Understanding non-Gaussian, multi-stable regimes is essential for extending the theory to more realistic, complex systems like biological or social networks.
