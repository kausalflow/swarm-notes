title: Variable Graph Size Forecasting
---

**Background:** Network traffic is inherently dynamic, often resulting in graphs with varying numbers of nodes and edges over time.

**Question / Future Work:** Current graph-based forecasting models for network flows often rely on fixed graph structures, which may fail to capture the non-static nature of network traffic. Developing mechanisms capable of supporting variable graph sizes is essential to better represent the evolving connectivity patterns in real-world network environments.

**Why It Matters:** Most real-world network traffic is dynamic; restricting models to fixed graph sizes limits their practical applicability in production environments where node and edge counts fluctuate significantly.
