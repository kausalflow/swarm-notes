title: Refining ERB Modeling for ALP Detection
---

**Background:** Axion-like particles are a leading dark matter candidate, and their radiative decay into photon pairs is significantly enhanced by stimulated emission in the presence of ambient radiation fields, such as the Cosmic Microwave Background and the Extragalactic Radio Background.

**Question / Future Work:** The extragalactic radio background (ERB) is a critical component for determining the stimulated decay rate of axion-like particles; however, its modeling, particularly the extrapolation of empirical radio luminosity-star formation rate relations and the treatment of potential anisotropic fluctuations, introduces uncertainties that require further refinement to improve the fidelity of the inferred ALP decay signal.

**Why It Matters:** The accuracy of the ERB model directly affects the sensitivity projections for the ALP-photon coupling parameter, making the refinement of this background model essential for the reliability of cross-correlation intensity mapping techniques.

**Evidence:** The fidelity of our ERB modeling can be further improved by incorporating more sophisticated galaxy evolution prescriptions and high-redshift radio luminosity functions.
