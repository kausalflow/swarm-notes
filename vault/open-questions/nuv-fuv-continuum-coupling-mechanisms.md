title: NUV and FUV Continuum Coupling
---

**Background:** The solar chromosphere emits radiation across multiple wavelengths, including NUV and FUV, which are often observed to be spatially and temporally correlated during flares. However, the common physical origin of this behavior is not yet fully constrained by hydrodynamic simulations.

**Question / Future Work:** Further work is required to investigate the physical origin of the observed spatial and temporal correspondence between NUV and FUV continuum enhancements, using hydrodynamic flare simulations to determine the coupled formation mechanisms.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding this shared formation is essential for accurately modeling energy transport in the solar chromosphere.

**Evidence:** Flare simulations have not yet compared the formation mechanisms of the FUV and NUV continua. We plan to investigate this in future work using hydrodynamic simulations of the flare candidates in our study.
