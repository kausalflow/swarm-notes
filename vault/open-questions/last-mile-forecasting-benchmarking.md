title: Benchmarking Last-Mile Forecasting Performance
---

**Background:** Last-mile forecasting involves transforming a statistically plausible baseline forecast into a decision-ready forecast using weakly structured business context such as holidays, external disruptions, and expert judgment.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing specialized benchmarks for last-mile forecasting that evaluate systems beyond numerical accuracy, focusing instead on the correctness of revisions, the interpretability of the revision trace, and the efficiency of the decision support process.

**Why It Matters:** Standardizing evaluation is essential to track progress in agentic forecasting frameworks that require reliability, auditability, and context-awareness in operational settings.

**Evidence:** A natural next step is to build a benchmark specifically for last-mile forecasting, where success depends not only on raw numerical accuracy but also on revision correctness, interpretability, and user efficiency.
