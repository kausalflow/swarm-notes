title: Real-world absenteeism model validation
---

**Background:** Individual-level absenteeism prediction models are currently developed using simulated longitudinal data due to the lack of publicly available, real-world organizational records.

**Question / Future Work:** The most critical research need is the validation of time-series classification frameworks on actual, large-scale longitudinal organizational attendance records to confirm findings derived from synthetic proxies. Such real-world empirical validation is a necessary prerequisite for deployment in high-stakes operational environments.

**Why It Matters:** Without large-scale real-world data validation, absenteeism models remain trapped within the limitations of synthetic data benchmarking, preventing production-grade confidence.

**Evidence:** Validation on real organisational attendance data remains the most important direction for future research, and constitutes a prerequisite for deployment in production workforce management systems.
