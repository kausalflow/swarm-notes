title: Stellar radial-velocity noise floor
---

**Background:** Evolved stars exhibit radial-velocity jitter due to phenomena such as granulation, magnetic activity, and stellar pulsations, which complicate the detection of low-mass planetary companions. Even with dedicated observing strategies, the intrinsic stellar noise floor for these targets remains an open challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Further investigation is needed to quantify the respective contributions of granulation, p-mode oscillations, and long-term magnetic activity to the radial-velocity noise budget of low-luminosity giant stars. Disentangling these mechanisms is essential for pushing precision beyond the current 5 m/s floor and improving the sensitivity to smaller planetary signals in this stellar population.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the noise floor is critical for defining the detection limits of exoplanet surveys targeting evolved stars and for assessing whether observed long-period signals are indeed planetary or manifestations of stellar magnetic cycles.

**Evidence:** The extra component on the error budget can be due to the instrument or intrinsic stellar RV stability, and the most likely contributor is stellar p-mode oscillations. ... One cannot fully exclude that these signals are false positives, in particular created by long-term magnetic cycles.
