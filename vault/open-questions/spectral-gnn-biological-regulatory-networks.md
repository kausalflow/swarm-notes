title: Spectral GNNs for Regulatory Networks
---

**Background:** Spectral graph convolutions are often used in biological systems where transcription factors and target genes form star-like, hub-centered regulatory topologies.

**Question / Future Work:** Systematic investigation is required to determine whether spectral GNN architectures outperform standard message-passing schemes in modeling the hub-centered and potentially heterophilous structure of gene regulatory networks.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the efficacy of spectral versus spatial GNNs for regulatory networks is critical for optimizing graph-based models in biological domains.

**Evidence:** One possible explanation is related to the heterophilous and hub-centered structure of gene regulatory networks... This hypothesis will be investigated more systematically in planned future work on this project.
