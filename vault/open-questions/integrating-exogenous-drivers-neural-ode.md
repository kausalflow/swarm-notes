title: Integrating exogenous neural-ode drivers
---

**Background:** Predictive models for dynamical systems often lack explicit mechanisms to account for exogenous causal drivers, which limits their performance when external conditions undergo structural changes. Integrating heterogeneous exogenous variables into continuous-time architectures is a key challenge for improving realism in data-driven forecasting.

**Question / Future Work:** Integrating exogenous policy interventions, macroeconomic shocks, or sudden systemic shifts into purely data-driven Neural ODE architectures remains an unresolved technical hurdle for ensuring predictive reliability during periods of structural transition.

**Why It Matters:** Without explicit integration of external drivers, models remain vulnerable to structural breaks and provide limited utility for causal inference or policy counterfactual analysis.
