title: Scaling stage-aware disease progression modeling
---

**Background:** The AT(N) framework organizes Alzheimer's disease progression into amyloid, tau, and neurodegeneration, but these biomarkers are often measured inconsistently across longitudinal clinical datasets. Establishing robust causal dependencies between these biological processes remains a challenge due to limited data overlap in late-stage disease.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is required to enhance the stability of estimating AT(N)-constrained structural models by integrating more diverse longitudinal cohorts and refining latent pseudotime estimation methods to accommodate heterogeneously measured biomarkers.

**Why It Matters:** Improving the generalizability and robustness of stage-aware causal modeling is necessary for practical deployment in clinical diagnostics beyond specific experimental cohorts.
