title: Expanding Lattice Connectivity Dynamics
---

**Background:** The connectivity of nodes in network-based epidemic models, such as those defined on a 2D lattice, is fundamentally limited by the chosen topology, which may not capture the complexity of real-world contact networks.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to explore higher-dimensional grid models or alternative network topologies that allow for more flexible node connectivity beyond the nearest-neighbor limitations of a 2D lattice. This extension aims to better model the potential infection capacity of individual agents without complicating the computational or visualization constraints of the system.

**Why It Matters:** Lattice-based models are computationally efficient but potentially underestimate the reproduction rate due to restricted neighborhood connectivity, making it vital to find a balance between model complexity and realism.
