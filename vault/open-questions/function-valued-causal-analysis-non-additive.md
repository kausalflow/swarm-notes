title: Extending function-valued analysis frameworks
---

**Background:** Nonlinear additive autoregressive models frequently use scalar edge scores to summarize causal relationships in time series, projecting complex causal response functions into low-dimensional summaries.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing generalized methods for function-valued causal analysis that extend beyond additive, contribution-decomposable architectures remains a challenge, as current techniques rely on the additivity assumption to isolate causal responses. Extending these to models with complex cross-variable interactions or non-additive architectures is critical for broader applicability.

**Why It Matters:** This is technically important because many high-performance machine learning models for time series do not satisfy the additivity constraint, making the current function-valued framework inapplicable to a wide class of modern architectures.

**Evidence:** Extending function-valued causal analysis to non-additive architectures is an important direction for future work.
