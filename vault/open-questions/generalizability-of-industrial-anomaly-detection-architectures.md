title: Generalizability of industrial anomaly detection architectures
---

**Background:** Industrial time-series data often originates from complex, non-periodic, multi-stage processes that deviate from the assumptions of stationarity and temporal alignment found in standard benchmarks. Evaluating model robustness across diverse industrial operational settings remains a challenge due to the lack of generalized findings and reliance on proprietary datasets.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is required to determine whether architectural preferences (such as the robustness of TCNs over recurrent/variational models) generalize across a broader range of industrial domains and varying degrees of process-induced complexity, particularly to establish universal performance rankings or a refined architectural taxonomy that accounts for non-periodicity and variable phase ordering.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing generalized architectural guidelines is critical for shifting industrial anomaly detection from bespoke implementations to reliable, robust deployment frameworks for diverse manufacturing environments.
