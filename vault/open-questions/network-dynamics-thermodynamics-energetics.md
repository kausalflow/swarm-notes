title: Network dynamics energetics and thermodynamics
---

**Background:** Fluctuation-Dissipation Relations (FDR) link system response to fluctuations in equilibrium, but their generalization to non-equilibrium steady states (NESS) is complex and often depends on the specific network structure and noise characteristics. Identifying the thermodynamic properties of such systems is crucial for understanding energy dissipation and entropy production.

**Question / Future Work:** A theoretical derivation is required to relate the energetics, such as heat, work, and entropy production rates, for general linearized noisy networks at NESS to measurable quantities like network structural properties and time correlation functions.

**Why It Matters:** This is important because while the authors provide an FDR for the NESS, the exact mapping of these dynamics to fundamental thermodynamic quantities (heat, work, entropy production) at the level of individual nodes remains an active, unresolved area of research.
