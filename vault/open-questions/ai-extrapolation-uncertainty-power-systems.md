title: Robustness and extrapolation limitations
---

**Background:** The deployment of black-box artificial intelligence models in safety-critical power-converter systems raises concerns regarding their behavior outside the data distributions used during training.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an urgent need to address the inherent risks of AI models in power-converter systems, specifically regarding their inability to reliably handle scenarios outside their training data distributions. Future work should focus on methods that provide robust uncertainty quantification and formal verification, ensuring that AI-driven designs and control policies maintain safety and stability even when confronted with unprecedented or extreme operating conditions.

**Why It Matters:** Extrapolation failure in power systems can lead to catastrophic hardware damage or grid instability; defining the operational boundary of an AI model is essential for safety certification.
