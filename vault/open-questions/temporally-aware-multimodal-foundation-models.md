title: Temporally Aware Multimodal Foundation Models
---

**Background:** Current multimodal time series forecasting frameworks often rely on vision-language models pre-trained for natural image-text tasks rather than specialized temporal reasoning. Consequently, these models lack deep, domain-specific semantic understanding of fine-grained temporal patterns.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop temporally aware VLMs that are specifically designed and pre-trained to bridge the modality gap between temporal dynamics and semantic textual context, rather than relying on adaptations of general-purpose Vision-Language Models. This involves constructing large-scale multimodal datasets that pair time series with rich, contextually relevant textual and visual annotations to provide the necessary inductive bias for complex forecasting tasks.

**Why It Matters:** General-purpose VLMs currently serve as a foundation for multimodal time series, but their performance is constrained by a lack of temporal-specific semantic alignment. Developing specialized multimodal foundation models for time series is essential to unlock the full potential of semantic-temporal integration.

**Evidence:** Future research could focus on constructing large-scale multimodal datasets that pair time series data with rich textual and visual annotations, enabling the development of models specifically optimized for time series forecasting.
