title: Validating Dynamic Radio Maps
---

**Background:** Dynamic radio maps aim to capture transient propagation conditions for improved network adaptation, yet current validation relies heavily on simulator-based labels.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to establish more rigorous validation benchmarks for dynamic radio map forecasting, specifically by bridging the gap between simplified blockage simulators and real-world signal propagation environments. This is essential to ensure that proactive beam and rate adaptation strategies generalize to complex, non-ideal urban deployments.

**Why It Matters:** Validation of predictive network maps is essential for moving beyond simulation-based optimization and identifying failure modes in real-world ISAC deployments.
