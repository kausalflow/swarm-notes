title: Mechanisms of Note Disappearance
---

**Background:** Community-based fact-checking systems on social media platforms rely on consensus-based algorithms to determine the visibility of annotations, yet these systems face significant challenges regarding the stability and longevity of displayed content.

**Question / Future Work:** While consensus-based algorithms effectively surface helpful notes, it remains unclear whether the observed disappearance of notes is primarily driven by inherent low quality or by post-display rating dynamics such as polarized feedback, strategic downvoting, or coordinated adversarial behavior. Future research is required to disentangle these mechanisms by combining behavioral trace data with survey or experimental methods to determine the exact causality behind note disappearance.

**Why It Matters:** Distinguishing between organic disagreement and strategic manipulation is critical for designing more resilient moderation algorithms that can maintain high-quality fact-checking while resisting polarized or coordinated attacks.
