title: Supporting heterogeneous application QoS
---

**Background:** Edge-cloud computing environments often handle diverse application types, each with varying quality-of-service (QoS) requirements and computational resource needs. Most existing reinforcement learning formulations rely on simplified workload models that do not capture the semantic heterogeneity of real-world IoT applications.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is required to extend workload offloading frameworks to accommodate heterogeneous application classes with distinct quality-of-service requirements and resource profiles. Developing models that can adapt to the diverse operational needs of different IoT service categories is essential for the practical deployment of these frameworks in real-world, multi-tenant IoT ecosystems.

**Why It Matters:** Standardizing performance metrics across diverse IoT services is a major bottleneck in deploying unified workload management strategies.

**Evidence:** Second, extending the framework to support heterogeneous application classes with different quality-of-service requirements and resource profiles would further enhance its applicability to real-world IoT ecosystems.
