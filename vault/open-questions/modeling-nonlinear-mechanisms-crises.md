title: Modeling Nonlinear Crisis Mechanisms
---

**Background:** Standard macroeconomic baseline models are often calibrated as local, linear, or log-linear approximations optimized for normal economic conditions, limiting their efficacy during systemic stress or nonlinear regime transitions.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a methodological bottleneck in incorporating nonlinear amplification mechanisms—such as collateral spirals, fire sales, and regime-dependent dynamics—into a structural forecasting framework while maintaining causal interpretability.

**Why It Matters:** Structural models that fail to capture nonlinearities during crises are a major failure point for central bank forecasting during systemic events.

**Evidence:** Baseline models ... are local approximations calibrated for normal times. In calm periods this is often adequate; in stress episodes it can become misleading.
