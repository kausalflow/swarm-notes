title: Safety and Uncertainty-Aware RRM
---

**Background:** Predictive digital twins for vehicular networks rely on accurate channel and environment representations to enable proactive radio resource management (RRM). Incorporating prediction uncertainty and safety constraints into these frameworks remains a significant research challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to move beyond deterministic predictive twin outputs by integrating uncertainty-aware channel and trajectory modeling, as well as formal safety-constraint injection into the joint resource management objective function.

**Why It Matters:** Reliability in safety-critical vehicular RRM cannot rely on point-estimates alone, making the integration of uncertainty into the twin's loop essential.

**Evidence:** For future work, the uncertainty and confidence level of predictions will be considered for improving the reliability of AdaPTwin for vehicular networks. Furthermore, we will consider safety awareness in RRM.
