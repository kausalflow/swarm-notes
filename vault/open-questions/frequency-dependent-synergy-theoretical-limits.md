title: Frequency-dependent Synergy Theoretical Limits
---

**Background:** Multi-modality forecasting often assumes cross-modality synergy, but these interactions may vary in coherence or reliability across the frequency spectrum.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains an open challenge to develop theoretical frameworks that rigorously quantify the frequency-dependent synergy between disparate modalities, specifically to define optimal boundaries for when cross-modality integration improves predictions versus when modality-specific isolation is more reliable. Future research is needed to analyze how non-stationary or heterogeneous spectral profiles impact the consistency of cross-modality coherence, particularly at higher frequencies.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing the limits of frequency-dependent synergy is essential for preventing negative transfer and improving robustness in multimodal time series systems.
