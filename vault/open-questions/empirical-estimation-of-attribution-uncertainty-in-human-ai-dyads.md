title: Attribution uncertainty estimation in human-AI dyads
---

**Background:** Firms are increasingly utilizing AI-augmented labor, creating significant attribution uncertainty between human cognitive input and AI-generated outputs, a phenomenon termed attribution uncertainty.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing empirical methods to quantify the attribution gap in real-world professional environments using production tool logs is essential for validating the extent to which output-based evaluation can mitigate agency costs in human-AI collaboration.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the real-world magnitude of attribution uncertainty is critical for evaluating the agency costs inherent in human-AI collaboration and testing theoretical predictions regarding output-based evaluation.

**Evidence:** In the human-AI dyad, the multitask structure intensifies because the dyad introduces a third actor whose contribution is difficult to attribute. We denote this attribution uncertainty γ ∈ [0, 1]... Firm-level γ estimation from production AI tool logs remains a complementary future path.
