title: RSG mass-loss modeling limitations
---

**Background:** Current 3D radiative-hydrodynamics (RHD) models of red supergiant (RSG) stars are limited by their inability to fully reproduce observed pulsation properties and atmospheric extensions, often relying on simplified physical assumptions that fail to match the observed complexity of mass-loss initiations.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a critical need for developing 3D RHD models for RSGs that can simultaneously account for wavelength-dependent opacities and multi-mode pulsation behavior to better match interferometric observations. Current models generally fail to capture the observed extreme mass-loss events and the precise phase-dependent atmospheric structures, necessitating the development of next-generation grids that incorporate more realistic energy transport and molecular formation physics to resolve the discrepancy between observed and synthetic atmospheric extensions.

**Why It Matters:** These models are essential for understanding the triggering mechanisms of mass loss in RSGs and their subsequent contribution to the chemical enrichment of the interstellar medium, which remains a poorly constrained process.

**Evidence:** For RSGs, there are no models that can fully reproduce the pulsation curves. ... The discrepancy in molecular bands of H2O may be due to the gray Rosseland opacity approximation in the models... This shows the necessity to properly take into account wavelength-dependent opacity... to achieve better agreement with the observations.
