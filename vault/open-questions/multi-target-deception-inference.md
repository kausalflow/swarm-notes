title: Multi-Target and Deceptive Inference
---

**Background:** Aerial target classification and intent prediction systems typically focus on single-target engagement scenarios, whereas real-world combat environments often involve targets operating as coordinated groups or engaging in deceptive maneuvers.

**Question / Future Work:** Expanding belief-based frameworks to account for multi-target environments and identifying deceptive behaviors is necessary for enhancing tactical inference in complex combat scenarios, requiring a shift beyond the one-versus-one engagement paradigm.

**Why It Matters:** Generalizing these systems to multi-target and deceptive scenarios is essential for operational relevance, as real-world aerial combat rarely involves isolated, non-deceptive targets.
