title: Parametrizing PDSPL Systematic Noise
---

**Background:** Strong lensing tomography is a powerful geometric probe for dark energy, but its precision is fundamentally limited by the mass-sheet degeneracy (MSD) and the rarity of double-source plane lenses (DSPLs). Pseudo double-source plane lenses (PDSPLs) mitigate observational scarcity, but their constraining power relies on accurately characterizing systematic noise from deflector dissimilarity.

**Question / Future Work:** The constraining power of PDSPLs is limited by an irreducible 'noise floor' arising from physical differences between paired deflectors. Further research is required to determine the functional dependence of this noise on deflector properties and to develop data-driven self-calibration frameworks that do not rely on simulation-based priors.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the primary systematic uncertainty in the PDSPL framework, which if unmodeled, could introduce significant bias in dark energy parameter estimation.

**Evidence:** However, we note that the systematic effects discussed in Section 6 are not yet incorporated into these forecasts and will need to be addressed in future work.
