title: Unified Gravitational-Wave Calibration Convention
---

**Background:** Gravitational-wave detector calibration frameworks utilize disparate conventions between observatories, such as inverted representations of strain bias and uncertainty ratios between Virgo and LIGO/KAGRA.

**Question / Future Work:** Establish a unified, network-wide convention across the LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA collaboration for representing detector strain bias and uncertainty ratios (such as $h_{rec}/h_{inj}$ versus its inverse) to streamline joint astrophysical analyses and parameter estimation.

**Why It Matters:** Essential for eliminating systematic reporting discrepancies in multi-detector gravitational-wave astronomy and joint parameter estimation pipelines.

**Evidence:** Finally, we must remind that the convention used by Virgo to provide the h(t) uncertainty information is the inverse of that adopted by LIGO and KAGRA. A common convention across the detector network is planned for future observing runs, with an implementation expected after 2027.
