title: Uniform inference for sieve deconvolution
---

**Background:** The identification and estimation of random coefficient distributions in short panels often rely on deconvolution techniques that are ill-posed, particularly when nuisance parameters are estimated from data.

**Question / Future Work:** Establishing valid uniform inference is critical for testing structural hypotheses about the heterogeneity of coefficients in random coefficient models, yet rigorous theoretical foundations for uniform inference in two-step sieve deconvolution estimators remain underdeveloped. This requires extending existing bootstrap and moment-inversion tools to settings where moment conditions depend on regressor-dependent nuisance estimators.

**Why It Matters:** It is a central technical bottleneck for applying random coefficient models to characterize parameter heterogeneity in panel data.
