title: Disentangling Sunspot Wave Signatures
---

**Background:** The sunspot atmosphere exhibits complex magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) oscillations characterized by multi-frequency spectra that vary across different layers of the photosphere and chromosphere. The mapping of these oscillations using spectral diagnostics is often complicated by model-dependent formation heights and line-specific responses to underlying physical perturbations.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to cleanly disentangle the contributions of atmospheric stratification, magnetic geometry, and line-specific diagnostic sensitivities in shaping observed multi-frequency sunspot wave signatures. Future work requires more accurately constrained formation heights—potentially through full-Stokes inversions and detailed radiative-transfer response functions—to determine whether the observed line-to-line differences in frequency power are primarily driven by geometric height stratification or by the inherent diagnostic response of the spectral lines.

**Why It Matters:** Unresolved bottlenecks in diagnostic height calibration and sensitivity limit the ability to map observed wave power to specific MHD modes and resonance structures, which is critical for understanding energy transport in the solar atmosphere.
