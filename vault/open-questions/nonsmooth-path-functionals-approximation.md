title: Approximation Theory for Nonsmooth Functionals
---

**Background:** Path signatures provide a canonical feature representation for path-valued covariates, but existing approximation theory largely focuses on smooth functionals of Itô diffusions.

**Question / Future Work:** Extend the current approximation theory for path signatures beyond smooth functionals of Itô diffusions to encompass broader path-dependent quantities such as barriers, optimal stopping rules, occupation times, and other nonsmooth functionals.

**Why It Matters:** Extending signature approximation theory to nonsmooth functionals is technically crucial for broader simulation-based pricing, option valuation, and optimal stopping applications where payoffs or constraints are discontinuous.

**Evidence:** First, the approximation theory could be extended to more general path functionals, including barriers, stopping rules, occupation times, and other nonsmooth path-dependent quantities.
