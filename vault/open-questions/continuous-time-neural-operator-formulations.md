title: Continuous-time operator extensions
---

**Background:** Markov transition operators are conventionally discrete-time models, yet many real-world stochastic processes occur or are observed at irregular intervals.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research should aim to extend the currently discrete-time neural Markov framework into continuous-time formulations, such as through neural stochastic differential equations, to handle irregular observation intervals and improve theoretical grounding for dynamical systems.

**Why It Matters:** Continuous-time formulations are theoretically better suited for handling high-frequency data and irregular sampling, which are pervasive in complex stochastic processes.

**Evidence:** A longer-term direction is to extend the transparent operator view into continuous time (e.g., via neural stochastic differential equations), allowing for irregular observation intervals and a more natural treatment of market microstructure.
