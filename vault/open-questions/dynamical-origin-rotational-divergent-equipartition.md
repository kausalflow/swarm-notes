title: Dynamical origin of rotational-divergent equipartition
---

**Background:** Oceanic flow contains both balanced motions, such as geostrophic eddies, and unbalanced motions, such as internal waves, which co-exist across various spatial and temporal scales. Distinguishing between these two regimes remains challenging, particularly in surface observations where Doppler shifting and spatial sparsity complicate traditional Eulerian filtering.

**Question / Future Work:** The dynamical origin of the observed near-equipartition of rotational and divergent kinetic energy in mean surface flows at spatial scales below O(1) km remains unclear. Future research is required to determine whether this phenomenon is driven by submesoscale frontogenesis, Ekman forcing, or other mechanisms, and to investigate whether this signature is robust or a numerical artifact of the decomposition process.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding submesoscale vertical transport is critical for oceanographic climate modeling; identifying if this equipartition represents significant vertical energy exchange or analytical noise is essential for robust parameterization.

To clarify the dynamical origin of this intriguing equipartition, more observational/modeling data and process-oriented studies are required.
