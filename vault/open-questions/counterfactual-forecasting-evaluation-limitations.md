title: Evaluating Counterfactual Forecasting Quality
---

**Background:** Evaluating time series forecasting models under counterfactual conditions, where future events deviate from historical data, is hindered by the lack of ground-truth future time series.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to develop robust, model-based evaluation metrics that can accurately assess the quality of counterfactual forecasts across different time series lengths, moving beyond the current limitations of semantic consistency metrics which may not fully capture temporal accuracy or varying dynamics.

**Why It Matters:** This is a central bottleneck in counterfactual forecasting; without reliable ways to evaluate performance when ground truth is absent, it is difficult to systematically improve model reliability for real-world decision-making.

**Evidence:** While there are still limitations, particularly in evaluating forecasts of varying lengths with model-based metrics, our work opens new avenues for modeling and assessing counterfactual forecasts under complex conditions.
