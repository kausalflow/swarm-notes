title: Long-term Air Traffic Forecasting
---

**Background:** Air traffic flow prediction in terminal airspace typically relies on short-term forecasting horizons due to operational requirements, but long-term planning requires extending these capabilities.

**Question / Future Work:** Extending state-to-flow microscopic modeling frameworks to longer predictive horizons (e.g., 30-90 minutes) is necessary for strategic congestion management and planning.

**Why It Matters:** Increasing prediction horizons is vital for the transition from tactical air traffic management to strategic flow control.

**Evidence:** Extending the framework to longer forecasting horizons, such as 30-minute-ahead and 90-minute-ahead prediction tasks, is both natural and an important direction for future research.
