title: Identifying Causal Leading Indicators
---

**Background:** Healthcare systems face challenges in accurately forecasting patient demand due to fragmented, non-stationary data and the need to integrate diverse, noisy contextual indicators. Current models often struggle to maintain temporal stability and calibration, which are essential for high-stakes operational decision-making.

**Question / Future Work:** It is currently unresolved how to identify and quantify the specific exogenous indicators that provide the most reliable, actionable signals for anticipating shifts in healthcare demand. Establishing a robust methodology for feature selection and validation in this context is essential to prevent the destabilization of predictive models by noisy or irrelevant contextual information.

**Why It Matters:** This is a bottleneck for moving from purely reactive, short-horizon forecasting to proactive, decision-aware capacity planning in healthcare. Without understanding which signals are causal or truly leading, researchers risk including noisy variables that degrade rather than improve model performance.
