title: Optimal Causal Lag Selection
---

**Background:** Time-series causal discovery methods often require defining a maximum lag parameter to capture lagged dependencies between variables. Improperly setting this parameter can lead to either missing true causal links or increasing computational complexity.

**Question / Future Work:** Systematic exploration is needed to determine the optimal strategy for lag selection in time-series causal discovery. Future research should investigate criteria or adaptive mechanisms to automatically identify the appropriate maximum lag order without requiring extensive manual hyperparameter tuning.

**Why It Matters:** Choosing the correct maximum lag is critical for both the accuracy of recovering causal structures and the computational efficiency of the discovery process.
