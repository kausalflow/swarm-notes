title: Modeling Complex Systemic Shocks
---

**Background:** Biomedical count time series are often subjected to abrupt, systemic external shocks that disrupt established pre-shock trends and increase forecast volatility. Standard methods for modeling these dynamics often rely on simplistic statistical assumptions or fail to account for the interplay between autoregressive patterns and sudden regime changes.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to refine model-selection and validation protocols for count time-series data to better handle multi-wave shocks or heterogeneous timing of interventions, which currently remain limited to pre-specified, singular shock proxies. Future research should also address the integration of more sophisticated causal identification strategies and hierarchical, multi-site modeling frameworks that can capture center-level heterogeneity and within-week variability in healthcare activity series.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical because simple intervention proxies often fail to capture the nuanced, multi-phasic nature of large-scale healthcare shocks, which in turn limits the reliability of long-term predictive models and the ability of policymakers to accurately assess the resilience of medical systems.
