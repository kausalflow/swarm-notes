title: Adapting contrastive learning for forecasting
---

**Background:** While self-supervised learning for time series has advanced significantly, most contrastive learning frameworks are currently designed for classification and clustering, which prioritize discriminative features over fine-grained dynamics.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining how to adapt or modify contrastive self-supervised learning objectives to capture the fine-grained temporal dynamics necessary for accurate forecasting remains an unresolved challenge.

**Why It Matters:** This is a critical limitation for bridging the gap between representation learning and forecasting in time-series analysis.

**Evidence:** Extending Di-COT to such tasks may require a fundamentally different learning objective and is left for future work.
