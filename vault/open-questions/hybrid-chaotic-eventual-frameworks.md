title: Hybrid chaotic-eventual frameworks

**Background:** Traffic forecasting models typically assume consistent dynamic regimes, but urban traffic often experiences sudden transitions between chaotic and non-chaotic behaviors.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop hybrid chaotic-eventual frameworks capable of modeling both standard chaotic traffic flow and abrupt, unpredictable traffic events or anomalies simultaneously.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing sudden traffic anomalies is critical for moving beyond steady-state forecasting and improving system-wide robustness in real-world intelligent transportation systems.
