title: Integrating Exogenous Factors for Forecasting
---

**Background:** Numerical forecasting models often rely on endogenous history, whereas complex real-world markets are driven by external influences. Integrating these external signals into forecasting-oriented tabular question answering remains an open challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Develop robust methods for incorporating exogenous signals (e.g., macroeconomic indicators, policy shifts) into agent-based forecasting frameworks to improve predictive performance beyond simple historical extrapolation.

**Why It Matters:** This is a critical gap for domain-specific forecasting where internal historical data is insufficient to account for external market volatility.
