title: Reducing SEP False Alarms
---

**Background:** Solar energetic particle (SEP) forecasting models often face significant class imbalance, leading to high false alarm rates that limit operational reliability. Improving the specificity of these warnings remains a key bottleneck for space weather monitoring systems.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to develop robust methods for reducing false-positive rates in SEP event prediction, including advanced loss function formulations to handle class imbalance, better data augmentation strategies, and event-level forensic analysis of failed predictions to identify systemic error drivers.

**Why It Matters:** High false alarm rates reduce trust and operational efficiency in real-time safety-critical space weather applications.
