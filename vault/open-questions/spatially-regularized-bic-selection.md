title: Spatially Regularized Neighborhood Selection
---

**Background:** The neighborhood selection process in high-dimensional autoregressive models often relies on BIC applied independently to each spatial location, ignoring the spatial continuity of dependencies.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing a spatially regularized neighborhood selection criterion requires solving discrete spatial labeling problems with efficient tuning of smoothness parameters, which remains an open computational and theoretical challenge.

**Why It Matters:** Spatially regularized selection is crucial for improving the interpretability of learned spatio-temporal dependencies and reducing noise in local structure estimation where dependency patterns exhibit spatial continuity.
