title: Generalizing observation-guided flood surrogates
---

**Background:** Hybrid surrogate modeling frameworks for scientific simulation often rely on high-fidelity numerical solvers, which are computationally expensive. Integrating localized point-based observations into these surrogates without introducing training-data leakage remains a challenge for achieving robust, operationally reliable mapping.

**Question / Future Work:** The framework requires extension to multiple gauges, independent flood-extent observations, comprehensive ablation studies, and formal validation against remotely sensed or field-observed flood products to demonstrate broad generalization beyond the single-gauge proof-of-concept.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing the generalizability and robustness of observation-guided surrogates is necessary for their deployment in real-world large-scale environmental monitoring.

**Evidence:** The current study is intentionally limited to one gauge and one local region; broader validation with multiple gauges, independent flood-extent observations... is required before operational claims.
