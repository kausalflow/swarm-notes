title: Scalability of kernel methods
---

**Background:** Kernel methods for machine learning, PDEs, and statistics often face significant computational bottlenecks due to the complexity of operating in high-dimensional Hilbert spaces as dataset size increases.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed to develop more scalable kernel-based algorithms to overcome the typical super-linear complexity in the number of samples, specifically targeting large-scale scientific and generative modeling applications.

**Why It Matters:** Computational complexity is a primary barrier preventing the widespread adoption of kernel methods in modern large-scale AI, making this a critical unresolved bottleneck.
