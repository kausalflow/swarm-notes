title: Generalizing RRM Domain Domains
---

**Background:** Return Risk Measures (RRMs) currently rely on the algebraic structure of AM-algebras to define geometric convexity, which restricts their application to spaces of essentially bounded random variables. Expanding this framework to general function spaces requires addressing the definition of geometric powers for elements where the standard algebraic closure properties fail.

**Question / Future Work:** Identify minimal structural requirements in algebraic domains beyond AM-algebras (such as general L^p spaces or f-algebras) that permit the consistent definition of geometrically convex return risk measures while maintaining functional stability for financial return data.

**Why It Matters:** Critical for applying RRM theory to financial datasets characterized by heavy tails and varying integrability requirements.

**Evidence:** Hence, the problem of extending beyond the framework of AM-algebras while maintaining GG-convexity well-defined remains open.
