title: Scalogram ridge stability under spectral interference
---

**Background:** Scalogram ridges serve as a fundamental tool for nonstationary signal analysis, yet their stability and behavior under noisy conditions or during instantaneous frequency crossovers remain theoretically challenging. Theoretical frameworks currently struggle to bridge the gap between idealized ridge definitions and practical, robust extraction algorithms in complex environments.

**Question / Future Work:** Extending the theoretical framework of scalogram ridges to account for scenarios with spectral interference, such as when instantaneous frequencies of harmonic components are close or crossover, is essential for robust signal processing. This research also requires future integration between these theoretical bounds and the practical performance of ridge extraction algorithms in non-ideal noise conditions.

**Why It Matters:** These questions represent the primary bottlenecks for applying rigorous scalogram ridge analysis to real-world signals where components often interfere or cross, moving beyond theoretical isolation.
