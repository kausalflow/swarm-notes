title: Partitioning for Complex Reasoning
---

**Background:** Health generation tasks often involve complex clinical reasoning, such as diagnosis or treatment planning, where the logic governing the output is less stable than in descriptive reporting.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigating how the partitioning of deterministic computation and neural generation scales to tasks requiring complex reasoning or personalized treatment planning, where output rules are less structured and more dynamic.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical because the current results are limited to descriptive health reporting; verifying whether the 'code-first' design principle scales to high-stakes clinical decision-making is necessary for determining the scope of this architecture in broader healthcare applications.
