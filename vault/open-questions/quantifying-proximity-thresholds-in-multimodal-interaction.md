title: Quantifying Proximity Thresholds for Multimodal Interaction
---

**Background:** Multimodal analytical interfaces often rely on spatial proximity to disambiguate user intent, yet there is no established methodology for determining the interpretive thresholds that define these proximity relationships.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to develop systematic approaches for quantifying the 'interpretive thresholds' that determine whether multimodal elements (such as sketches, annotations, and text) are perceived as sufficiently proximal to trigger a deictic reference binding. This includes identifying how these thresholds vary based on screen resolution, visual density, user experience, and the semantic nature of the data being explored.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing these thresholds is critical for building robust multimodal systems that can reliably resolve ambiguous user queries without being overly restrictive or too inclusive.

**Evidence:** a proximity binding is a referential relationship between two elements... whose measured distance in a common space falls within an interpretive threshold i.e., the two objects appear ‘close’ together.
