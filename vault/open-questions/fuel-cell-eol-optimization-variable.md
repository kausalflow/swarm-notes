title: End-of-life as optimization variable
---

**Background:** Fuel cell degradation in maritime hybrid power systems is currently modeled using fixed performance thresholds to define end-of-life conditions.

**Question / Future Work:** The current practice of defining fuel cell end-of-life as a fixed 10% performance drop is arbitrary and does not account for the specific dynamics of maritime operational profiles. Further research is required to treat the performance drop as an optimization variable within energy management systems rather than a fixed parameter.

**Why It Matters:** Defining end-of-life as an optimization variable rather than a fixed constant would allow energy management strategies to dynamically trade off component longevity against operational costs in a more flexible and realistic manner.

**Evidence:** the fixed 10% performance drop for EOL definition of FCs is to be questioned for this application and instead be replaced by an optimization variable.
