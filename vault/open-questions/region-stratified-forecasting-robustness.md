title: Region-Stratified Forecasting Evaluation
---

**Background:** Forecasting models often demonstrate varied performance across different geographic regions due to inherent disparities in monitoring station density and infrastructure quality.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to shift from global aggregate evaluation to a stratified framework that assesses model robustness specifically across diverse, under-instrumented, and over-instrumented geographic regions. Future work should focus on developing techniques that ensure reliable predictive performance in regions with sparse, high-missingness observational networks, rather than just optimizing for overall average performance.

**Why It Matters:** Region-stratified evaluation is essential for environmental equity and ensuring that air quality forecasting technologies remain reliable and useful in developing regions where they are often most needed but least supported by infrastructure.
