title: Quiet-Sun normalization in Ca II K
---

**Background:** Full-disk observations of solar Ca II K intensity are essential for long-term historical reconstructions of solar irradiance and magnetic activity. However, these data are inherently heterogeneous due to significant variations in instrumental passbands and spatial resolutions across different historical and modern observatories.

**Question / Future Work:** The determination of a truly representative quiet-Sun (QS) intensity level, which is necessary for the robust normalization of Ca II K observations, remains a significant challenge due to the limited fields of view typically available in high-resolution solar datasets. Developing methods to define or extract an accurate, field-independent quiet-Sun baseline from non-ideal data is critical for improving the quantitative precision of cross-calibration efforts.

**Why It Matters:** The quiet-Sun intensity serves as the universal normalization factor for these observations; therefore, systematic errors in its determination propagate directly into the homogenized time series and subsequent irradiance reconstructions.

**Evidence:** One of the main challenges is determining a representative QS level within the limited FOV of the available datasets... The region used for normalization in the SUSI data likely corresponds to weak plage rather than perfectly QS. This introduces a systematic uncertainty in the absolute contrast scale.
