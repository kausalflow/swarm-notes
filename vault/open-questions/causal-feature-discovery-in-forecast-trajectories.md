title: Causal discovery on forecast trajectories
---

**Background:** Forecasting tropical cyclone (TC) intensity involves complex, multiscale, and non-linear physical interactions that are often poorly captured by semi-empirical feature selection methods.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to derive causally selected predictors directly from dynamical forecast output along storm-following trajectories rather than relying on analysis-time reanalysis fields, to better capture the evolution of atmospheric conditions during the forecast lead time.

**Why It Matters:** Moving from analysis-based to trajectory-based causal discovery could significantly reduce forecast uncertainty at longer lead times, representing a key bottleneck in operational hurricane prediction.

**Evidence:** A promising next step is to derive causally selected predictors directly from dynamical forecast output and along forecast trajectories.
