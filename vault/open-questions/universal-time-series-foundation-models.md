title: Universal Foundation Models for Time-Series
---

**Background:** Pre-trained Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly adapted for time-series analysis, yet general-purpose models often struggle with domain-specific characteristics like non-stationarity and irregular sampling. Developing universally applicable models that can transfer knowledge across diverse temporal domains without extensive, task-specific fine-tuning remains a significant open challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** While various frameworks aim to unify time-series tasks, there is a lack of models that truly excel as universal forecasters capable of handling diverse temporal patterns without requiring specialized architecture or extensive dataset-specific adjustments. Future work is required to develop architectures that achieve robust, generalized cross-domain transferability while maintaining strict efficiency constraints suitable for edge deployment.

**Why It Matters:** The development of a truly universal, foundation-level model for time series would drastically reduce the engineering burden for deployment in diverse fields where data is often fragmented and scarce.
