title: Comparative Evaluation of AI and Physics Weather Forecasts
---

**Background:** Operational weather forecasting systems often consist of both physics-based numerical weather prediction (NWP) models and emerging data-driven machine learning (ML) models. Comparing the relative skill of these two paradigms across various spatio-temporal scales and weather variables remains an active area of research.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is required to evaluate the performance of AI-based and physics-based forecasting systems for critical weather variables beyond 10-m wind speed, such as 100-m wind speed and solar irradiance, while accounting for seasonal variability and incorporating advanced location-specific post-processing techniques. Additionally, continuous comparative assessment is necessitated by the rapid development cycles of both IFS and AIFS architectures.

**Why It Matters:** As weather services transition toward hybrid forecasting systems combining NWP and AI, it is critical to determine the strengths and weaknesses of each approach across diverse operational scenarios to guide future model development and integration strategies.
