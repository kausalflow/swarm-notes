title: Channel-wise Anomaly Localization Benchmarks
---

**Background:** Industrial anomaly detection often requires localizing specific sensors or channels responsible for a fault for improved root-cause attribution, but existing benchmarks are predominantly restricted to timestamp-level binary labels which provide insufficient information for evaluating spatial or channel-wise localization capabilities.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of standardized, high-quality benchmarks with precise channel-wise anomaly annotations remains an open challenge. Current datasets lack the necessary ground truth to systematically evaluate and compare the interpretability and spatial diagnostic performance of different anomaly detection frameworks, hindering progress in developing more actionable and explainable industrial diagnostic tools.

**Why It Matters:** Channel-wise localization is critical for operational stability and maintenance. Tracking this across new research is vital for moving towards more interpretable and diagnostic AI in infrastructure management.
