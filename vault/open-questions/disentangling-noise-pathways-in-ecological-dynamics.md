title: Disentangling Noise Pathways in Ecological Dynamics
---

**Background:** In consumer-resource systems, environmental noise can either act directly on consumer growth rates or indirectly through fluctuating resource availability. The resulting patterns of species abundance correlations depend on the specific pathway through which this noise enters the system.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the relative contributions of direct consumer-level noise and resource-mediated noise, especially in systems where resources have structured, co-evolved response patterns, is essential for accurate ecological inference. Developing a framework to disentangle these sources from empirical time-series remains an open, unresolved problem.

**Why It Matters:** Empirical data typically show a mix of direct and indirect environmental forcing. Without a way to distinguish these pathways, observers cannot correctly attribute the observed abundance correlations to either niche overlap or yield-depletion mismatch, leading to potentially incorrect biological conclusions.
