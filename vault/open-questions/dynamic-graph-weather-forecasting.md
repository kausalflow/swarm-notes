title: Dynamic Graph Weather Forecasting
---

**Background:** Weather forecasting models often rely on predefined or static graph structures to represent spatial relationships between geographic regions. These static approaches struggle to adapt to the non-linear, time-varying spatial dependencies inherent in evolving atmospheric systems.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is required to develop dynamic graph construction methods that can autonomously update edges and spatial connectivity in response to evolving weather conditions, moving beyond the current static modeling limitations in Graph Neural Network-based forecasting.

**Why It Matters:** Static graph structures fail to capture the transient nature of weather patterns, limiting the model's ability to maintain high precision during complex or rapidly changing atmospheric events.
