# Accuracy-Realism Trade-off in Forecasting

> *Auto-generated stub. Edit this file to add more details.*

The mathematical trade-off between point-wise accuracy (MSE) and marginal distribution realism in multi-step time series forecasting.


## Why It Matters

Formalizes a fundamental conflict between minimizing mean squared error and preserving realistic data distributions in long-horizon forecasting, which is a major bottleneck in the field.

## Evidence

> no deterministic predictor can simultaneously minimize MSE and match the marginal distribution of realized futures. This establishes a fundamental, model-agnostic trade-off between point accuracy and marginal realism in MSF evaluation.
