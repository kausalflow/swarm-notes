# Asymmetric Generalization in Financial Markets

> *Auto-generated stub. Edit this file to add more details.*

A phenomenon where predictive models transfer effectively from high-liquidity to low-liquidity domains, but suffer degradation in the reverse direction.


## Why It Matters

Identifies a directional transfer property in financial data that contradicts naive model-sharing assumptions.

## Evidence

> we reveal an asymmetric generalization phenomenon: models trained on more liquid markets or products transfer well to less liquid ones, whereas the reverse transfer leads to substantial performance degradation.
