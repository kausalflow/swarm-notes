# A New Member of the Fast and Furious Family: A Relativistic and Time-Variable UV Outflow in a Luminous Quasar

**Authors**: Lucas M. Seaton, Patrick B. Hall, Liliana Flores, Paola Rodríguez Hidalgo, Marianna Veltri, Zi Zhu, Javier Serna, W. Niel Brandt, Scott Anderson, Roberto J. Assef, Eduardo Bañados, C. J. Grier, Y. Homayouni, Sean Morrison, C. Alenka Negrete, Amy L. Rankine, Jessie Runnoe, Donald P. Schneider, Yue Shen, Matthew Temple, Benny Trakhtenbrot, Jonathan R. Trump, Erik Weiss
**Date**: 2026-06-04
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.06226](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06226)

## Summary

This paper identifies and characterizes a relativistic UV outflow in the radio-quiet quasar SDSS J231854.31+243954.2, which reaches velocities of 0.3c. Multi-epoch spectroscopic observations reveal the outflow's evolution over two years, providing evidence for monotonic strengthening of absorption features. The study demonstrates that this outflow provides sufficient kinetic power to potentially impact host galaxy evolution, necessitating refinements in current quasar feedback models regarding gas acceleration and ion preservation at relativistic speeds.

## Key Contributions

- Identified SDSS J231854.31+243954.2 as the fastest quasar UV outflow ever detected, reaching velocities between -77,000 km/s and -90,000 km/s.
- Characterized the outflow's evolution over a ~2.2 rest-frame year period, showing monotonic strengthening of C IV absorption features.
- Demonstrated that the kinetic luminosity ratio (L_kin/L_bol >= 0.75%) of this outflow reaches the threshold for significant feedback on the host galaxy, providing empirical constraints for quasar feedback models.
