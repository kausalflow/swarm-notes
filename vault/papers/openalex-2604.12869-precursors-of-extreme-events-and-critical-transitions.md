# Precursors of extreme events and critical transitions

**Authors**: Riccardo Consonni, Luca Magri
**Date**: 2026-04-14
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.12869](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12869)

## Summary

This paper introduces a dynamical systems theory to explain and predict extreme events and critical transitions in nonlinear fast-slow systems. By analyzing the spectral and geometric properties of covariant Lyapunov vectors (CLVs), the authors identify a characteristic three-phase cascade—slow, transition, and critical—that precedes such events. Based on these insights, they propose two new precursors that achieve perfect predictive performance (100% precision and recall) in numerical experiments across varying system dimensions. The work provides a rigorous, theoretically grounded approach to early warning systems for critical transitions.

## Key Contributions

- Developed a dynamical systems theory identifying a three-phase cascade of events (slow, transition, and critical) that precede extreme events in fast-slow nonlinear systems.
- Proposed two theoretically grounded precursors derived from the spectral and geometric alignment of covariant Lyapunov vectors to forewarn extreme events.
- Demonstrated 100% precision and recall for predicting extreme events and critical transitions on both low- and higher-dimensional numerical systems.
