# Artificial Intelligence and Market Entrant Game Developers

**Authors**: Sungho Jo, Woo-Sung Jung, Jisung Yoon, Hyunuk Kim
**Date**: 2026-06-23
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2509.14907](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.14907)

## Summary

This paper investigates the impact of generative AI on creative industries by analyzing AI disclosure data from indie and non-indie developers on the Steam platform. Using time-series modeling, the study reveals that the availability of generative AI has led to a statistically significant surge in new market entrants among indie developers, while non-indie entry remains consistent with historical trends. Further analysis shows that AI adoption is heavily skewed toward image generation, suggesting that these tools primarily lower barriers by accelerating the rapid development and implementation of creative concepts.

## Key Contributions

- Provides empirical evidence that the introduction of generative AI accelerated market entry for indie game developers on the Steam platform.
- Demonstrates through time-series forecasting that the surge in indie developer entry significantly exceeds projections based on historical trends, unlike non-indie market entry.
- Identifies that generative AI adoption among developers is predominantly concentrated in image generation, with significantly lower utilization for music, voice, and programming tasks.
