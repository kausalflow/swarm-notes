# Simplifying preference elicitation in local energy markets: Combinatorial clock exchange

**Authors**: S. P. Singhal, Lesia Mitridati
**Date**: 2026-07-14
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2510.27306](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.27306)

## Summary

This paper addresses the complexity of preference elicitation in local energy markets by proposing a combinatorial clock exchange mechanism for prosumers. By allowing participants to express preferences through intuitive package reporting at posted prices, the mechanism removes the need for complex bidding or price forecasting. Machine learning is leveraged to enhance the speed of price discovery, achieving convergence in around 15 iterations while maintaining transparent linear pricing.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a multi-product market mechanism for distributed energy resources using combinatorial clock exchange to simplify prosumer participation.
- Integrates machine learning techniques to accelerate price discovery and converge to clearing prices within approximately 15 iterations.
- Ensures market transparency and participant interpretability through a linear pricing rule.
