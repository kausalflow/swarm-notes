# CORALIE radial-velocity search for companions around evolved stars (CASCADES) V. Three planetary companions and achievable precision

**Authors**: P. Figueira, E. Fontanet, J. P. Faria, M. Esseldeurs, E. Fridén, A. Leleu, R. Luque, G. Ottoni, D. Ségransan, M Stalport, S. Tavella, S. Udry
**Date**: 2026-06-08
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.09072](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09072)

## Summary

This paper presents a long-term radial velocity (RV) study of three evolved stellar hosts using the CORALIE spectrograph, resulting in the detection of three massive planetary companions. The authors utilize the Bayesian inference tool kima to characterize the RV signals and distinguish planetary signatures from stellar activity. Additionally, they propose and validate an observing strategy designed to mitigate the impact of stellar pulsations, achieving sub-5 m/s precision for red giant stars.

## Key Contributions

- Identified three massive planetary companions around evolved stars using 15-18 years of radial velocity data from the CORALIE spectrograph.
- Demonstrated that targeted stellar pulsation mitigation strategies can reduce RV noise to below 5 m/s in low-luminosity red giants.
- Expanded the census of planetary companions around massive stellar hosts by populating previously sparse regions of stellar mass and orbital period parameter space.
