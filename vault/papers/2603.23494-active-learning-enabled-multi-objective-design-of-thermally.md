# Active learning-enabled multi-objective design of thermally conductive and mechanically compliant polymers

## Summary

This work addresses the challenge of discovering polymers with high thermal conductivity (TC) and low mechanical modulus by introducing an active learning (AL) framework built upon multi-objective Bayesian optimization (MOBO). The methodology employs Deep Kernel Learning (DKL) surrogate models trained on initial data from a Molecular Dynamics (MD) pipeline to predict both TC and modulus along with their uncertainties. The search is guided by the parallel noisy expected hypervolume improvement (qNEHVI) acquisition function, which iteratively selects new polymer candidates for simulation evaluation to refine the models and explore the objective space. Ultimately, the framework successfully identified six polymers on the Pareto front, offering a data-efficient method to guide the design of multifunctional polymers.

## Key Contributions

- Development of an active learning framework based on Multi-Objective Bayesian Optimization (MOBO) to simultaneously optimize for high thermal conductivity (TC) and low bulk modulus in polymers.
- Integration of a high-throughput Molecular Dynamics (MD) simulation pipeline with Deep Kernel Learning (DKL) surrogate models to predict properties and guide the sequential data acquisition process.
- Successful identification of six polymer candidates residing on the Pareto front, demonstrating optimal trade-offs between the typically conflicting properties of thermal conductivity and mechanical compliance.
- Post-optimization interpretability analysis that linked specific molecular features to the observed property trade-offs, providing actionable guidance for future material design.

## Key Concepts

- [Active Learning for Multi-Objective Optimization](../concepts/active-learning-multi-objective-design.md): A framework that uses Bayesian Optimization and Deep Kernel Learning to iteratively discover materials that optimize competing objectives (high thermal conductivity and low modulus).
- [Deep Kernel Learning (DKL) Surrogate Models](../concepts/deep-kernel-learning-surrogate.md): Surrogate models used within the active learning loop to predict thermal conductivity and bulk modulus, while also quantifying predictive uncertainty.
- [Parallel Noisy Expected Hypervolume Improvement (qNEHVI)](../concepts/qnehvi-acquisition-function.md): The acquisition function used to guide the active learning search by identifying the most promising next set of samples to evaluate based on the current Pareto front approximation.

## Limitations

The performance is inherently dependent on the accuracy and computational cost of the underlying high-throughput Molecular Dynamics (MD) pipeline used for initial data generation and evaluation. The study's assessment of synthesizability, while supportive, is not a full experimental validation.
