# Foveated Diffusion: Efficient Spatially Adaptive Image and Video Generation

## Summary

This work introduces Foveated Diffusion to address the computational bottleneck of high-resolution image and video generation by exploiting the eccentricity-dependent acuity of human vision. The core methodology involves using a gaze-dependent mask to allocate tokens non-uniformly, prioritizing high token density for the foveal region and lower density for the periphery. A key technical contribution is a mechanism for constructing these mixed-resolution tokens from high-resolution ground truth, allowing established diffusion models to be efficiently post-trained for this sparse token setting. The resulting generation is perceptually equivalent to full-resolution output but demands significantly fewer tokens and computation time, demonstrating foveation as a scalable efficiency optimization.

## Key Contributions

- Proposed Foveated Diffusion, a technique that uses a gaze-aware, non-uniform token density mask to drastically reduce the token count during image/video generation.
- Developed a principled mechanism for constructing mixed-resolution tokens directly from high-resolution data, enabling post-training of existing diffusion models.
- Achieved perceptual quality indistinguishable from full-resolution generation while significantly reducing computational cost.
- Validated the approach via extensive analysis and a user study demonstrating the scalability and efficacy of foveation for efficient generation.

## Key Concepts

- [Foveated Diffusion](../concepts/foveated-diffusion.md): A method for efficient image and video generation that leverages the non-uniform resolution sensitivity of human vision by assigning higher token density to the gaze location (fovea) and lower density to the periphery.

## Limitations

The approach relies on the availability or accurate estimation of the user's gaze location (eye tracking).
