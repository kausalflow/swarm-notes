# An Integrated Techno-Economic Framework for Optimal Microgrid Design: An Australian Case Study

**Authors**: Mohamed Atef, Sanath Alahakoon, Umme Mumtahina, Peter Wolfs, Tamer Khatib, Moslem Uddin
**Date**: 2026-06-02
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.03783](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03783)

## Summary

This paper presents an integrated techno-economic framework designed to optimize the configuration of hybrid renewable-based microgrids for residential communities. The framework incorporates time-series simulation, dispatch-based operation, and comprehensive lifecycle costing to evaluate various combinations of photovoltaic, wind, battery, diesel, and hydrogen storage systems. Through a rigorous sensitivity analysis, the study highlights how financial and technical drivers induce nonlinear shifts in system design, offering clear planning guidance for resilient and low-emission microgrid deployment.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces an integrated techno-economic framework that links time-series simulation, dispatch-based operation, and lifecycle costing for microgrid design.
- Identifies nonlinear shifts and economic breakpoints in optimal microgrid configurations through systematic sensitivity analysis of financial, technical, and policy drivers.
- Provides a comparative analysis between battery-centric and hydrogen-enabled microgrid solutions for a 1000-household residential community.
