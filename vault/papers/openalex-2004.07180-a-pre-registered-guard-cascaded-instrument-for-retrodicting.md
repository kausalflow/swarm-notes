# A pre-registered, guard-cascaded instrument for retrodicting cross-field fusions in the arXiv corpus: nine artifact classes and a verdict-grade null for abstract-semantic fusion precursors

**Authors**: Arman Cohan
**Date**: 2026-07-13
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2004.07180](https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.07180)

## Summary

This paper investigates whether semantic proximity in embedding spaces can forecast cross-field scientific fusions by applying a strictly pre-registered retrodiction test. The study finds that naive approaches to this problem are often driven by statistical artifacts, which the author categorizes into a taxonomy of nine classes. To address these, the paper introduces a 'guard-cascaded' instrument that systematically vets results, ultimately demonstrating that abstract-level proximity does not carry a reliable prospective signal for fusion events. The work provides a durable, disciplined framework for producing trustworthy negative results in science-mapping research.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a guard-cascaded instrument for identifying and neutralizing nine common artifact classes in science-mapping and cross-field fusion forecasting.
- Demonstrates through a pre-registered test that abstract-level semantic proximity fails to serve as a prospective precursor for cross-field scientific fusion events.
- Establishes a rigorous methodology for science mapping, highlighting the necessity of pre-registration to avoid bias and false-positive forecasting claims.

## Key Concepts
