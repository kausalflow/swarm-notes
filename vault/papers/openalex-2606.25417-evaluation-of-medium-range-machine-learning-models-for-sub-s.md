# Evaluation of medium range machine learning models for sub-seasonal prediction

**Authors**: Catherine de Burgh-Day, Chen Li, Debra Hudson, Lu Shi, Harrison Cook, Robin Wedd, Griffith Young
**Date**: 2026-06-24
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.25417](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25417)

## Summary

This paper evaluates the performance of GraphCast and FourCastNetV2 for sub-seasonal forecasting, assessing their ability to capture climate drivers like the Madden-Julian Oscillation and Southern Annular Mode. The authors employ a dual-period hindcast methodology to navigate the challenge of limited independent evaluation data. Results indicate that these ML models, originally designed for medium-range weather forecasting, demonstrate surprising skill at sub-seasonal timescales, performing comparably to traditional physics-based coupled models.

## Key Contributions

- Evaluates the sub-seasonal prediction performance of GraphCast and FourCastNetV2 against physics-based seasonal prediction systems like ACCESS-S2.
- Demonstrates that medium-range ML models exhibit competitive skill for sub-seasonal forecasts compared to physics-based ensembles.
- Introduces a dual-period evaluation strategy (38-year and 2.5-year hindcasts) to address limited independent data availability for sub-seasonal assessment.
