# A Comparative Study on how Data Normalization Affects Zero-Shot Generalization in Time Series Foundation Models

**Authors**: Ihab Ahmed, Denis Krompaß, Cheng Feng, Volker Tresp
**Date**: 2026-04-21
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2512.02833](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.02833)

## Summary

This paper investigates the critical role of input normalization in Time Series Foundation Models (TSFMs) to mitigate scale variation and non-stationarity across domains. The authors perform a systematic evaluation across four distinct TSFM architectures, showing that RevIN provides superior zero-shot generalization efficiency. The study reveals that optimal normalization strategy is intrinsically linked to the model's architectural design and training objective, particularly when scaling to unseen datasets.

## Key Contributions

- Systematic empirical analysis of input normalization methods for Time Series Foundation Models (TSFMs) across diverse architectures.
- Demonstrates that RevIN achieves significant zero-shot generalization improvements, reducing zero-shot MASE by 89% relative to raw baselines.
- Highlights that the effectiveness of normalization is highly dependent on architectural design choices, optimization objectives, and model-specific forecast paradigms.
