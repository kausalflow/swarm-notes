# Recurrence and anti-recurrence patterns reveal an antiperiodic fingerprint that survives into chaos in the Duffing--Holmes oscillator

**Authors**: Arturo C. Marti, Edson D. Leonel
**Date**: 2026-07-06
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2607.05323](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05323)

## Summary

This paper introduces the anti-recurrence plot (anti-RP) to detect discrete sign-reversal symmetries in dynamical systems that are otherwise hidden from standard recurrence analysis. By measuring the cross-recurrence between a trajectory and its point-reflected image, the method successfully identifies antiperiodic signatures in the Duffing-Holmes oscillator. These signatures remain detectable even in highly chaotic two-well regimes, providing a compact, model-free framework for classifying diverse dynamical states.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the anti-recurrence plot (anti-RP) as a robust, model-free probe for detecting antiperiodic symmetry in non-linear dynamical systems.
- Demonstrates that the antiperiodic fingerprint of the Duffing-Holmes oscillator persists into the chaotic two-well regime, enabling effective regime classification.
- Shows that the ratio of anti-recurrence rate to standard recurrence rate acts as a distinct indicator for symmetry-respecting dynamics, distinguishing between one-well and two-well motion in both periodic and chaotic states.
