# Probabilistic Physics-Aware Machine Learning Predictions of Electric Truck Energy Consumption with Field Data

**Authors**: Hannes Nilsson, Rafael Basso, Balazs Kulcsar, Morteza Haghir Chehreghani
**Date**: 2026-07-21
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2607.19054](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19054)

## Summary

This paper presents a probabilistic, physics-aware machine learning framework for predicting electric truck energy consumption using field data by explicitly modeling various sources of vehicle energy losses. The authors show that integrating first-principle physics into Bayesian linear regression, neural networks, and gradient boosted regression trees substantially improves forecasting accuracy and reliability compared to standard data-driven baselines. Additionally, the proposed framework successfully estimates prediction uncertainty in the form of standard deviations across all tested models.

## Key Contributions

- Incorporates first-principle physics accounting for energy loss sources into data-driven vehicle energy consumption prediction models.
- Demonstrates that Bayesian linear regression with a physics-aware model improves expected energy consumption reliability over standard linear regression.
- Shows that complex machine learning models (neural networks and gradient boosted regression trees) integrated with the physical model significantly outperform their standard counterparts in forecasting accuracy.
- Develops an uncertainty estimation framework predicting standard deviation, showing reliable uncertainty quantification across all evaluated models.
