# Generative Adversarial Learning from Deterministic Processes

**Authors**: Joris C. Kühl, Hanno Gottschalk
**Date**: 2026-05-18
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.18425](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18425)

## Summary

This paper provides a theoretical analysis of generative adversarial learning (GAL) applied to data derived from chaotic dynamical systems, moving beyond the standard i.i.d. assumption. The authors prove that an infinite-dimensional GAL model can successfully learn the invariant distribution of a chaotic system from a single, deterministically evolving time series. Furthermore, the study establishes explicit convergence rates for this process measured by the Jensen-Shannon divergence.

## Key Contributions

- Proves that generative adversarial learning (GAL) can recover the invariant distribution of chaotic dynamical systems using a single deterministic time series.
- Provides explicit convergence rates for the learned distribution towards the target invariant measure in terms of Jensen-Shannon divergence.
- Extends generative adversarial training theory to non-i.i.d. regimes relevant to physical sciences and turbulence modeling.

## Key Concepts

- [[generative-adversarial-learning-from-deterministic-processes]]: A theoretical framework for training generative adversarial models on single-trajectory time series generated by chaotic dynamical systems instead of i.i.d. samples.
