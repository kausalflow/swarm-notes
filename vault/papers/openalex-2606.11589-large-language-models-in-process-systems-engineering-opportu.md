# Large Language Models in Process Systems Engineering: Opportunities, Architectures, and Industrial Deployment Challenges

**Authors**: R. Bhushan Gopaluni, Vidya Kotamraju, Syon Bhushan
**Date**: 2026-06-10
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.11589](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11589)

## Summary

This survey provides a systematic review of the integration of Large Language Models (LLMs) into Process Systems Engineering (PSE). The authors categorize existing literature into seven functional areas, ranging from process design and molecular synthesis to time-series forecasting and fault detection. While the study highlights significant potential for LLMs in knowledge synthesis and documentation querying, it explicitly identifies critical limitations in real-time execution, constraint satisfaction, and safety, which remain substantial hurdles for industrial deployment.

## Key Contributions

- Provides a comprehensive systematic review of LLM applications across seven key domains of Process Systems Engineering (PSE).
- Critically assesses current LLM capabilities versus aspirational claims, identifying a clear divide between unstructured knowledge synthesis and mission-critical tasks like real-time control.
- Maps out future research directions specifically addressing the limitations of LLMs regarding constraint satisfaction, safety guarantees, and industrial deployment in PSE environments.
