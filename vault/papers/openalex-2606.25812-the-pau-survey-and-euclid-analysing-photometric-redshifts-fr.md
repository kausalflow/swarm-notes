## Summary

This paper presents an analysis of photometric redshift estimation using realistic simulations of the Physics of the Accelerating Universe Survey (PAUS) based on the Euclid Flagship 2 catalogue. The authors validate their simulation framework against observational data and investigate the impact of photometric noise, depth, and the fusion of narrow-band and broad-band data. Results indicate that combining PAUS narrow-band photometry with Euclid data substantially improves redshift accuracy, particularly for faint galaxies, providing critical insights for future wide-area cosmological surveys.

## Key Contributions

- Developed a realistic simulation framework for the Physics of the Accelerating Universe Survey (PAUS) using the Euclid Flagship 2 catalogue.
- Validated that the PAUS-like simulation achieves a photometric redshift scatter of σ_NMAD/(1+z) = 0.011 for a spectroscopic validation sample, consistent with observational data.
- Demonstrated that joint analysis with Euclid photometry significantly improves photometric redshift precision and reduces outlier fractions for faint galaxy populations.
