# Price as Focal Point: Prediction Markets,Conditional Reflexivity, and the Politics of Common Knowledge

**Authors**: Maksym Nechepurenko
**Date**: 2026-04-27
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.24147](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24147)

## Summary

This paper shifts the analysis of prediction markets from purely forecasting devices to public coordination mechanisms, examining how they influence elite behavior in political contexts. Using transaction-level data from the 2024 U.S. presidential election, the study introduces the Signal Credibility Index (SCI) to measure when market price movements effectively coordinate real-world actions. Results indicate that the social authority of a market signal is largely independent of its predictive accuracy, challenging conventional evaluation metrics. The findings have significant implications for the regulation of prediction markets as critical information infrastructure in democratic societies.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the Signal Credibility Index (SCI) to evaluate the behavioral impact of prediction market price signals beyond simple forecasting accuracy.
- Demonstrates that prediction market influence is driven by signal persistence, participant breadth, and cross-platform consensus rather than raw trading volume.
- Reveals a systematic decoupling between social authority and epistemic robustness in political prediction markets, where high visibility often correlates with poor predictive accuracy.
