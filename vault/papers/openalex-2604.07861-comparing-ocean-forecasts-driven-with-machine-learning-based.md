# Comparing Ocean Forecasts Driven with Machine Learning-based and Physics-based Atmospheric Forcings

**Authors**: Xiaobing Zhou, Frank Colberg, Debra Hudson, Yonghong Yin, Griffith Young, Christopher Bladwell, Catherine Deburgh-Day
**Date**: 2026-04-09
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.07861](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07861)

## Summary

This paper investigates the viability of using machine learning-based atmospheric models, specifically the AIFS, as drivers for operational ocean forecasting systems instead of traditional physics-based numerical weather prediction (NWP) models. By running comparative 10-day forecasts using the NEMO ocean model (GOSI9 configuration) driven by either AIFS or the ACCESS-G3 NWP model, the researchers evaluated the impact on key ocean surface variables. The study finds that ocean forecasts driven by ML-based atmospheric data exhibit predictive performance comparable to or better than those driven by conventional physics-based forcing, suggesting a paradigm shift toward AI-integrated oceanographic prediction.

## Key Contributions

- Compares ocean forecast skill using ML-based atmospheric forcing (ECMWF AIFS) against traditional physics-based NWP forcing (ACCESS-G3) within the NEMO-GOSI9 configuration.
- Demonstrates that ML-based AIFS atmospheric forcing yields ocean forecast skills for sea surface temperature, salinity, sea level, and currents that are comparable or superior to physics-based NWP systems.
- Provides evidence that ML-based atmospheric models can effectively replace traditional NWP forcing for operational ocean forecasting, providing both increased accuracy and computational efficiency.
