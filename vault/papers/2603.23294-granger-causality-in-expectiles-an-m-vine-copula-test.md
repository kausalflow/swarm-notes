# Granger Causality in Expectiles: an M-vine copula test

**Authors**: Roberto Fuentes-Martínez, Irene Crimaldi
**Date**: 2026-03-24
**ArXiv**: [2603.23294](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23294)

## Summary

This paper introduces a novel, model-free measure of Granger causality defined in terms of expectiles, which generalizes the traditional mean-based approach to capture distributional dependencies across the conditional distribution. The authors develop a multivariate test statistic for $d+1$ time series utilizing M-vine copula models to account for complex, non-linear, and non-Gaussian dependencies without imposing specific parametric forms on the joint distribution. Strong consistency of the test is proven, and finite-sample simulations confirm accurate size control and increasing power with sample size. A significant advantage demonstrated is the ability to jointly test for causality, revealing relationships undetectable by simpler pairwise methods, as validated through applications to international stock market indices.

## Key Contributions

- Introduction of a model-free measure for Granger causality based on expectiles, allowing for analysis across the entire conditional distribution rather than just the mean.
- Development of a multivariate Granger causality test for $d+1$ series using M-vine copula models, which handles non-linear and non-Gaussian dependence without parametric assumptions on the joint distribution.
- Establishment of the strong consistency of the proposed test statistic under specific regularity conditions.
- Demonstration of the framework's capability to detect multivariate causal relationships that are missed by pairwise tests, both theoretically and empirically.

## Limitations

The test is subject to regularity conditions that must be met for the strong consistency proof, and performance in very small finite samples should be assessed carefully.
