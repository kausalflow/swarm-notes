# Granger Causality in Expectiles: an M-vine copula test

**Authors**: Roberto Fuentes-Martínez, Irene Crimaldi
**Date**: 2026-03-24
**ArXiv**: [2603.23294](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23294)

## Summary

This paper introduces a model-free measure of Granger causality defined over expectiles, generalizing traditional mean-based causality to arbitrary quantiles of the conditional distribution, leveraging the desirable properties of expectiles as coherent and elicitable risk measures. The authors develop a statistical test based on this measure that employs M-vine copula models to accurately capture multivariate, non-linear, and non-Gaussian dependencies without requiring specific parametric forms for the joint distribution. They rigorously establish the strong consistency of their test statistic and empirically demonstrate its superior power in detecting multivariate causal effects compared to pairwise tests. The framework is validated through applications to international stock market indices, illustrating its relevance for financial econometrics.

## Key Contributions

- Proposal of a model-free measure for Granger causality specifically in expectiles, which are coherent and elicitable risk measures.
- Development of a statistical test for multivariate Granger causality using M-vine copula models that handles non-linear and non-Gaussian dependence without imposing parametric assumptions on the joint distribution.
- Establishment of the strong consistency of the proposed test statistic under specified regularity conditions.
- Demonstration of the test's capability to detect joint causal relationships that might be missed by pairwise tests, both theoretically and empirically.

## Limitations

The strong consistency proof relies on certain regularity conditions that would need to be explicitly stated and verified for practical applications. The performance in finite samples, while showing accurate size control, is explicitly stated to increase with sample size, suggesting potential challenges with smaller datasets.
