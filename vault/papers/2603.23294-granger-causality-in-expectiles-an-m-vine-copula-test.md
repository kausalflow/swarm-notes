# Granger Causality in Expectiles: an M-vine copula test

**Authors**: Roberto Fuentes-Martínez, Irene Crimaldi
**Date**: 2026-03-24
**ArXiv**: [2603.23294](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23294)

## Summary

This paper introduces a novel, model-free measure for Granger causality in expectiles, extending standard mean-based causality to any quantile of the conditional distribution. The authors leverage M-vine copulas to construct a statistical test for multivariate Granger causality among $d+1$ time series, which robustly handles complex, non-linear, and non-Gaussian dependencies without requiring parametric assumptions on the joint distribution. Theoretical analysis confirms the strong consistency of the resulting test statistic, and simulations indicate good finite-sample performance. A key practical advantage is the method's ability to detect joint causal structures that are otherwise invisible to simpler pairwise analyses.

## Key Contributions

- Proposal of a model-free measure for Granger causality specifically tailored to expectiles, generalizing traditional mean-based causality to arbitrary points in the conditional distribution.
- Development of a statistical test for multivariate Granger causality involving $d+1$ series based on M-vine copula models, which handles non-linear and non-Gaussian dependence without parametric assumptions.
- Establishment of the strong consistency of the proposed test statistic under stated regularity conditions.
- Demonstration that the proposed framework enables joint testing, allowing for the detection of complex causal relationships missed by pairwise tests, both theoretically and empirically.

## Limitations

The test is developed under certain regularity conditions that must be satisfied for the strong consistency proof to hold. Finite sample performance relies on the accurate modeling capabilities of M-vine copulas for the underlying multivariate dependence structure.
