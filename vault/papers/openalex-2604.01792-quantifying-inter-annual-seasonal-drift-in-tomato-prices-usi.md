# Quantifying Inter-Annual Seasonal Drift in Tomato Prices Using Dynamic Time Warping: Evidence from Kolar Market

**Authors**: Patil, Manojkumar, Achoth, Lalith, Vedamurthy, K. B., Umesh, K. B., Siddayya, Gowda, M. N. Thimme
**Date**: 2026-04-02
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.01792](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01792)

## Summary

This study investigates the recurring, yet temporally unstable, seasonal patterns of tomato prices in the Kolar market from 2010 to 2024. Using descriptive statistics and Dynamic Time Warping (DTW), the authors demonstrate that while seasonal cycles exist, the year-to-year alignment of these patterns fluctuates significantly, often due to extreme volatility events. The analysis reveals that relying on fixed seasonal indices leads to misestimations in such markets. Ultimately, the paper provides evidence for the need to incorporate flexible, drift-aware mechanisms into forecasting models for agricultural commodities.

## Key Contributions

- Quantified the inter-annual seasonal instability of Kolar market tomato prices using Dynamic Time Warping, revealing significant temporal variance not captured by standard seasonal indices.
- Demonstrated that extreme market events (high price/arrival volatility) coincide with structural temporal shifts, as evidenced by high DTW distances between 2021-2022.
- Established that fixed seasonal patterns are inadequate for modeling volatile commodity markets, necessitating forecasting models capable of adaptive temporal alignment.
