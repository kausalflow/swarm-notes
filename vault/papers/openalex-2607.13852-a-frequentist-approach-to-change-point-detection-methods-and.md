# A Frequentist Approach to Change Point Detection: Methods and Applications

**Authors**: Debanjana Datta
**Date**: 2026-07-15
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2607.13852](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13852)

## Summary

This paper introduces a frequentist framework for identifying change points in functional time series data, applicable to both sparse and dense observation supports. The approach formulates the problem through the maximization of the conditional probability of the change point given other parameters. Theoretical analysis demonstrates the consistency of the resulting estimator, offering a rigorous tool for detecting structural shifts in mean and volatility.

## Key Contributions

- Develops a frequentist change point detection methodology for functional time series that accommodates both sparse and dense support.
- Addresses the detection of both mean shifts and process volatility changes within functional data structures.
- Establishes theoretical consistency for the proposed change point estimator based on conditional probability maximization.
