# RadioAxion results on the search for axion dark matter under Gran Sasso

**Authors**: C. Broggini, Giuseppe Di Carlo, Luca Di Luzio, D. Piatti, Claudio Toni
**Date**: 2026-06-15
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2602.18392](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.18392)

## Summary

The RadioAxion experiment investigates axion dark matter by monitoring periodic modulations in the alpha decay of 241Am via its 59.5 keV γ-ray emission. Conducted at the Gran Sasso Laboratory, the experiment minimizes cosmic-ray-induced noise to facilitate highly sensitive time-series analysis of decay rates. No periodic modulation was detected, allowing the authors to establish significant new bounds on the axion decay constant within the 10^-21 eV to 10^-9 eV mass range.

## Key Contributions

- Presents the first results from the RadioAxion experiment, an underground search for axion dark matter via radioisotope decay modulation.
- Performs a high-precision time-series analysis of the 59.5 keV γ-ray line from 241Am decay in a shielded environment.
- Sets new experimental constraints on the axion decay constant across the mass range from 10^-21 eV to 10^-9 eV.
