# Strong Lensing Tomography: Double and pseudo multi-source plane strong gravitational lensing to constrain dark energy

**Authors**: P. Sharma, Simon Birrer, Narayan Khadka, Timo Anguita, Adam Bolton, Sydney Erickson, Phil Holloway, Tian Li, Phil Marshall, Dieu D. Nguyen, Geoffrey Smith, Crescenzo Tortora, Bryce Wedig, the Strong Lensing Science Collaboration, T K ND TPC collaboration
**Date**: 2026-07-01
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2607.01005](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01005)

## Summary

This paper introduces Pseudo Double-Source Plane Lenses (PDSPLs), a novel statistical framework for strong gravitational lensing tomography that avoids the scarcity and mass-sheet degeneracy issues of traditional double-source plane lenses. By treating pairs of independent single-source lenses as a coherent statistical sample, the method scales to the vast number of galaxy-galaxy lenses expected in upcoming wide-field surveys. The authors use a hierarchical forecasting approach to demonstrate that large photometric datasets achieve competitive constraints on dark energy parameters, outperforming smaller, highly precise spectroscopic samples.

## Key Contributions

- Introduced the PDSPL framework to generalize strong lensing tomography using independent single-source plane lenses.
- Demonstrated that statistical volume from upcoming large photometric surveys (LSST/Euclid/Roman) dominates over the per-pair precision of small spectroscopic samples for dark energy constraints.
- Forecasted $σ(w_0) \sim 0.29$ for the $w_0w_a$CDM cosmology when combining LSST 10-year samples with standard cosmological priors.
