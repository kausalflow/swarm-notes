# VLTI-GRAVITY measurements of cool evolved stars: II. Pulsation properties and mass-loss process of the Mira star R Car and the red supergiant VX Sgr

**Authors**: D. Jadlovský, M. Wittkowski, A. Chiavassa, K. Kravchenko, B. Freytag, S. Höfner, J. Krtička, C. Paladini, G. Rau, M. Brož, T. Granzer, M. Weber
**Date**: 2026-04-23
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.21621](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21621)

## Summary

This study leverages 7 years of VLTI-GRAVITY interferometric observations to analyze the atmospheric dynamics and mass-loss processes of the AGB star R Car and the red supergiant VX Sgr. By tracking the time-dependent angular diameter and multi-layer atmospheric extension, the authors demonstrate that mass loss in Miras follows stable fundamental mode (FM) pulsations, whereas RSGs experience episodic extreme mass-loss events linked to changes in pulsation modes. The observational data is validated against CO5BOLD hydrodynamical simulations, providing new insights into the link between stellar pulsation and mass-loss.

## Key Contributions

- Constructed the largest VLTI-GRAVITY time-series dataset of evolved stars, spanning 54 snapshots over 7 years for R Car and VX Sgr.
- Quantified phase-dependent photospheric and CO layer radii, demonstrating that maximum radii occur near visual brightness minima.
- Identified a distinct mechanism for mass-loss in RSGs, showing it is driven by extreme events linked to shifts in pulsation modes rather than the steady FM pulsations observed in Miras.
