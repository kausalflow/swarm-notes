# Unifying concepts in information-theoretic time-series analysis

**Authors**: Annie G. Bryant, Oliver M. Cliff, James M. Shine, Ben Fulcher, Joseph T. Lizier
**Date**: 2026-05-20
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2505.13080](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.13080)

## Summary

This paper addresses the fragmentation of information-theoretic time-series analysis by providing a unified framework of semantic definitions, standardized notation, and cohesive visualizations. By mapping disparate measures onto a shared conceptual space, the authors clarify the relationships between theoretical foundations and practical interpretability. The framework is validated through an fMRI case study, demonstrating its effectiveness in characterizing complex system dynamics and promoting interdisciplinary methodological consistency.

## Key Contributions

- Develops a unified semantic and mathematical framework to integrate fragmented information-theoretic time-series measures across scientific disciplines.
- Provides a mapping of disparate measures onto a common conceptual space to clarify theoretical foundations and computational formulations.
- Demonstrates the utility of the unified framework through a case study on functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) data, characterizing complex neural dynamics.
