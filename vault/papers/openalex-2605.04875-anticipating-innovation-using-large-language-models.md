# Anticipating Innovation Using Large Language Models

**Authors**: Enrico Maria Fenoaltea, Filippo Santoro, Giordano De Marzo, Segun Taofeek Aroyehun, Andrea Tacchella
**Date**: 2026-05-06
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.04875](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04875)

## Summary

This paper addresses the challenge of forecasting technological innovation by modeling the emergence of new technological combinations through the lens of patent language. The authors introduce TechToken, a transformer-based model that represents International Patent Classification (IPC) codes as tokens to learn the latent language of technological evolution. By analyzing the linguistic convergence of code embeddings, the model successfully identifies early signals of future combinations decades before they manifest. The findings suggest that technological innovation is a collective linguistic phenomenon rather than an individual effort, and the proposed model consistently outperforms state-of-the-art representations on patent-related tasks.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces TechToken, a transformer-based architecture that embeds patent classification codes to model the evolution of technological combinations.
- Demonstrates that predictive signals for future technological combinations exist within the collective linguistic patterns of patent descriptions decades in advance.
- Shows that linguistic convergence, measured by context similarity between code embeddings, acts as an accurate leading indicator for first-time technological combinations.
