# Why is Seasonal Climate Predictable Beyond the Limit of Deterministic Predictability set by Chaos?

**Authors**: Vladimir Garcı́a-Morales, Devabrat Sharma, Shruti Tandon, B. N. Goswami, R. I. Sujith
**Date**: 2026-06-23
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.24358](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24358)

## Summary

This paper investigates why certain climate systems, specifically the Indian Summer Monsoon Rainfall (ISMR), demonstrate predictability far exceeding their theoretical Limit of Deterministic Predictability (LDP) governed by chaotic fluctuations. The authors demonstrate that this phenomenon arises from lag synchronization between strongly coupled oscillatory media, such as ISMR and the Global El Nino-Southern Oscillation (G-ENSO). By modeling these interactions using coupled Complex Ginzburg-Landau Equations, the study explains how stable correlations persist in aperiodic time series over long lead times. These findings offer a fundamental mechanism for improving seasonal climate forecasting by leveraging inter-system synchronization rather than relying solely on individual system dynamics.

## Key Contributions

- Identifies that long-range predictability in climate systems, such as the Indian Summer Monsoon Rainfall, persists beyond their Limit of Deterministic Predictability (LDP) due to lag synchronization with strongly coupled predictors.
- Develops a conceptual model using coupled Complex Ginzburg-Landau Equations to represent the spatiotemporal evolution of oscillatory climate systems.
- Provides a theoretical basis for why slow-changing climate averages can be forecasted accurately at lead times (e.g., 18 months) that exceed the chaotic threshold of individual high-frequency weather components.
