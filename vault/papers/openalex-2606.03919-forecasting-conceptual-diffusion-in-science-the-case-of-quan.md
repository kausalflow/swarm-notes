# Forecasting Conceptual Diffusion in Science: The Case of Quantum Computing

**Authors**: Thomas Maillart, Thibaut Chataing, David Dosu, Paul Bagourd, Julian Jang-Jaccard, Alain Mermoud
**Date**: 2026-06-02
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.03919](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03919)

## Summary

This paper develops a predictive model for tracking scientific concept evolution by analyzing co-occurrence networks derived from OpenAlex citation data. By training LightGBM models on diversity-aware and distributional features, the authors accurately forecast exogenous diffusion entropy across multiple scientific fields, including quantum computing and robotics. The findings demonstrate that conceptual diffusion follows stable structural regularities, providing a new methodological toolkit for technology foresight and science policy analysis.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a predictive framework for scientific conceptual change, distinguishing between endogenous reinforcement and exogenous diffusion using temporally resolved co-occurrence networks.
- Demonstrates that exogenous diffusion is highly predictable (R^2 up to 0.78) across diverse scientific domains using upstream heterogeneity and citation breadth.
- Reveals that entropy dynamics in conceptual diffusion serve as leading indicators for scientific frontier emergence and technological convergence.
