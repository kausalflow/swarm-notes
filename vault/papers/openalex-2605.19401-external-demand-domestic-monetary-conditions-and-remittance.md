# External Demand, Domestic Monetary Conditions, and Remittance Dynamics in Nepal

**Authors**: Sahaj Raj Malla
**Date**: 2026-05-19
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.19401](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19401)

## Summary

This paper investigates the macroeconomic determinants of remittances in Nepal, one of the world's most remittance-dependent economies, using annual data from 1993 to 2024. The study develops composite indices for multi-country external demand and domestic monetary conditions using PCA and integrates them into an ARDL-ECM framework to model long-run dynamics. The results highlight a strong positive sensitivity of remittances to external demand and a significant negative impact from tighter domestic monetary policy, offering a framework for policy calibration and long-term economic forecasting.

## Key Contributions

- Constructs composite indices for multi-country external demand and domestic monetary conditions using PCA to address small-sample macroeconomic modeling limitations.
- Establishes a stable long-run cointegrating relationship between remittance-to-GDP ratios, external demand, and domestic monetary conditions via an ARDL-ECM framework.
- Provides medium-term projections for Nepal's remittance dynamics, forecasting a 28.3% GDP share by 2030 and quantifying the system's 26% annual error correction rate.
