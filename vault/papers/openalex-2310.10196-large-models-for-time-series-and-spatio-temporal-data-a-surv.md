# Large Models for Time Series and Spatio-Temporal Data: A Survey and Outlook

**Authors**: Ming Jin, Qingsong Wen, Yuxuan Liang, Chaoli Zhang, Siqiao Xue, Xue Wang, James Zhang, Yi Wang, Haifeng Chen, Xiaoli Li, Shirui Pan, Vincent S. Tseng, Yu Zheng, Lei Chen, Hui Xiong, Qingsong Wen
**Date**: 2026-06-23
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2310.10196](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.10196)

## Summary

This survey provides a systematic overview of the emerging field of foundation models adapted for time series and spatio-temporal data analysis. It categorizes current research into LM4TS and LM4STD frameworks, distinguishing between general-purpose and domain-specific models. The authors synthesize recent progress across architectures and tasks while identifying critical research gaps and future directions toward achieving artificial general intelligence in temporal data mining.

## Key Contributions

- Provides a comprehensive taxonomic review of large models applied to time series (LM4TS) and spatio-temporal data (LM4STD).
- Categorizes temporal foundation models across dimensions of data types, architecture, scope, and application domains.
- Curates an extensive resource repository of datasets, implementations, and tools for large model-centric temporal data analysis.

## Archivist Review

The survey is a comprehensive review paper that organizes existing literature but does not propose new methodologies, architectures, or datasets. Therefore, no new concepts or datasets were approved. The two open questions were approved as they capture foundational bottlenecks in the application of large models to temporal domains.

### Approved Open Questions
- Theoretical Limits of Temporal Adaptation: Establishing a theoretical basis is critical for moving beyond heuristic model designs, guiding the principled development of architectures that are robust to the unique properties of temporal and spatio-temporal data.
- Interpretability in Temporal Models: Without interpretability, large models face adoption barriers in sectors where accountability, regulatory compliance, and the ability to explain decisions are mandatory.
