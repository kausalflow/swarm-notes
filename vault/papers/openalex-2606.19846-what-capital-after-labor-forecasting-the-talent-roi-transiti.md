# What Capital After Labor? Forecasting the Talent ROI Transition in the Human-AI Era

**Authors**: Kwan Soo Shin, In Seok Kang
**Date**: 2026-06-18
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.19846](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19846)

## Summary

This paper introduces a forecasting framework to analyze the transition from time-based to output-based talent ROI accounting driven by AI augmentation. The authors propose the 'ROI Inversion at τ*' theorem to describe the decoupling of labor time from productive contribution. Empirical evidence from a DART panel of 365 Korean firms validates this overhead-pressure regime, where time-based accounting currently increases overhead despite labor-time compression. The framework acts as a planning tool for firms to adapt their productivity evaluation models for the human-AI era.

## Key Contributions

- Develops a forecasting framework for transitioning from time-based talent accounting to output-based talent ROI in the human-AI era.
- Identifies an 'overhead-pressure' regime using Korea's 52-hour workweek mandate as a natural experiment.
- Forecasts that output-based firms will outperform time-based peers by 1.5-2.0 percentage points in TFP growth by 2032.

### Approved Concepts
- ROI Inversion at τ*: Central theoretical component of the proposed framework for predicting the shift from time-based to output-based talent accounting.

### Approved Open Questions
- Non-additivity of talent overhead components: Standard cost accounting assumes overhead components are additive; if this assumption is invalid in the AI era, existing managerial accounting models for talent ROI are structurally flawed.
- Attribution uncertainty estimation in human-AI dyads: Understanding the real-world magnitude of attribution uncertainty is critical for evaluating the agency costs inherent in human-AI collaboration and testing theoretical predictions regarding output-based evaluation.
