# Likelihood estimation of an interpretable early-warning sign of critical transitions

**Authors**: Davide Papapicco, Lauren Smith, Graham Donovan
**Date**: 2026-07-07
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2607.05976](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05976)

## Summary

This paper introduces an interpretable early-warning signal to detect critical transitions in complex systems before catastrophic collapses occur. The proposed indicator is computed via maximum likelihood estimation directly from time series observations, providing an immediate assessment of the system's proximity to a bifurcation. The method's effectiveness is validated through performance evaluations on simulated models of ecosystems and ocean circulation stability.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a novel, interpretable early-warning signal for bifurcation-induced critical transitions in complex systems.
- Demonstrates the indicator's utility for detecting regime shifts in ecosystem models and ocean circulation collapse scenarios.
- Enables immediate state-of-system insights by leveraging maximum likelihood estimation on univariate or multivariate time series observations.
