# Fitness Inference in Presence of Migrations between Coupled Evolving Populations

**Authors**: Yu-Han Huang, Bastien Dumont, Hong-Li Zeng, John Barton, Erik Aurell
**Date**: 2026-05-21
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.22665](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22665)

## Summary

This paper extends the concept of Quasi-Linkage Equilibrium (QLE) to evolutionary populations coupled by migration, establishing its persistence under low migration regimes. By modeling the interplay of selection, mutation, recombination, and migration, the authors provide a theoretical framework for multi-locus systems. They validate this framework using whole-genome time-series data and present novel analytical relations to infer additive fitness and epistatic interactions.

## Key Contributions

- Extended Quasi-Linkage Equilibrium (QLE) theory to incorporate population coupling through migration.
- Demonstrated that the QLE phase remains stable in multi-locus evolutionary systems under low migration rates.
- Derived analytical inference relations for quantifying additive fitness and epistatic interactions from population time-series data.
