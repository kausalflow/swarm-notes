# Stablecoins as Dry Powder: A Copula-Based Risk Analysis of Cryptocurrency Markets

## Summary

This study investigates the under-explored structural role of stablecoins in transmitting systemic risk across the cryptocurrency ecosystem using copula-based modeling techniques. The authors quantify the transmission of volatility and trading activity from stablecoin markets to the broader digital asset markets, establishing significant in-sample causality across multiple time horizons. Key findings indicate that stablecoin volume and upside volatility act as predictors, effectively serving as 'dry powder' that influences overall market dynamics. Moreover, incorporating these stablecoin factors substantially improves forecasting accuracy by reducing Mean Squared Error and lowers risk in volatility-targeting portfolio strategies.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrated in-sample volatility and activity causality from stablecoin to cryptocurrency markets across daily, weekly, and monthly horizons using copula-based methods.
- Showed that incorporating stablecoin factors significantly reduces Mean Squared Error (MSE) in cryptocurrency forecasting models.
- Identified stablecoin volume and upside volatility as key factors driving broader cryptocurrency market volatility, effectively characterizing stablecoins as 'dry powder'.
- Established economic value by demonstrating reduced risk in a cryptocurrency volatility targeting model when stablecoin factors are included.

## Key Concepts

- [Stablecoin Risk Transmission](../concepts/stablecoin-risk-transmission.md): Quantifying how volatility and activity transmit from stablecoin markets to broader cryptocurrency markets using copula models.

## Limitations

The study focuses on in-sample causality, and further out-of-sample validation and analysis of different stablecoin classes (e.g., fiat-backed vs. algorithmic) would strengthen the findings.
