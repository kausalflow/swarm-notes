# Stablecoins as Dry Powder: A Copula-Based Risk Analysis of Cryptocurrency Markets

**Authors**: Elliot Jones, Toshiko Matsui, William Knottenbelt
**Date**: 2026-03-24
**ArXiv**: [2603.23480](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23480)

## Summary

This paper investigates the systemic risk contribution of stablecoins within the cryptocurrency ecosystem by quantifying their influence on broader market volatility. Utilizing copula-based modeling, the authors establish statistical causality, showing that stablecoin volume and upside volatility act as significant predictors, or "dry powder," for cryptocurrency market dynamics across multiple time horizons. Incorporating these stablecoin factors demonstrably improves forecasting accuracy by reducing the Mean Squared Error of cryptocurrency predictions. Furthermore, the analysis confirms the economic utility of these insights by showing that the factors enhance the performance of cryptocurrency volatility targeting risk management strategies.

## Key Contributions

- Quantified the transmission of volatility and activity from stablecoin markets to broader cryptocurrency markets using copula-based models.
- Demonstrated significant in-sample causality between stablecoin activity metrics (volume, volatility) and cryptocurrency market dynamics across daily, weekly, and monthly forecasting horizons.
- Showed that incorporating stablecoin factors significantly reduces Mean Squared Error (MSE) in cryptocurrency forecasting models.
- Established the economic value of stablecoin factors by demonstrating reduced risk in a volatility targeting strategy for cryptocurrency portfolios.

## Limitations

The study focuses on historical causality and may not fully capture sudden, idiosyncratic shocks specific to individual stablecoins or new regulatory changes. The specific copula families chosen might influence the magnitude of observed dependence.
