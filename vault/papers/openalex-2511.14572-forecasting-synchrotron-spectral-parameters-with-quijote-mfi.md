# Forecasting synchrotron spectral parameters with QUIJOTE-MFI2 in combination with Planck and WMAP

**Authors**: Ana Sofia Almeida, J. A. Rubiño-Martín, Roke Cepeda-Arroita, R. T. Génova-Santos, Debabrata Adak
**Date**: 2026-04-23
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2511.14572](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.14572)

## Summary

This paper presents a parametric component separation forecast for the QUIJOTE-MFI2 instrument, evaluating its ability to improve constraints on Galactic polarized synchrotron emission. By integrating simulated data from QUIJOTE-MFI2 with existing WMAP and Planck observations, the authors demonstrate substantial reductions in uncertainties for spectral index and curvature parameters. The findings highlight the instrument's role in providing necessary low-frequency data to improve foreground modeling for upcoming cosmic microwave background surveys.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies the forecasting improvement of the QUIJOTE-MFI2 instrument in constraining polarized synchrotron emission parameters (spectral index and curvature) when combined with WMAP and Planck.
- Demonstrates that QUIJOTE-MFI2 provides up to 43x reduction in synchrotron polarization amplitude uncertainty and a 6x reduction in synchrotron residuals at 100 GHz.
- Establishes detection thresholds for synchrotron curvature parameters, providing critical observational constraints for future CMB missions like LiteBIRD and the Simons Observatory.
