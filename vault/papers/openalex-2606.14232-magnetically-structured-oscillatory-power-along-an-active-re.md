## Summary

This paper utilizes high-resolution, stratospheric near-UV spectroscopy from the Sunrise III/SUSI instrument to analyze oscillatory power in solar active regions. By sampling 30 distinct absorption lines across a 2-hour time series, the authors map power distribution within three frequency bands (2–4, 4–6, and 6–12 mHz) and correlate these with specific magnetic environments. The findings reveal that oscillatory power is highly structured and environment-dependent, with significant line-to-line variability that challenges current single-line diagnostic approaches.

## Key Contributions

- Characterizes solar oscillatory power across diverse magnetic environments (weak-field, plage, sunspot, pore) using high-cadence near-UV spectroscopy.
- Demonstrates that power distribution in frequency bands (2–12 mHz) is highly dependent on both the local magnetic field and the specific spectral line probed.
- Establishes that multi-line spectroscopy is required to resolve local wave spectrum components that are often obscured by traditional single-line measurements.
