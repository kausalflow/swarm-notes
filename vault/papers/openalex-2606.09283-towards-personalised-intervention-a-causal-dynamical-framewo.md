# Towards personalised intervention: A causal-dynamical framework to determine psychological treatment trajectories

**Authors**: Lourens Waldorp, Titus Mürtz, Anita Jansen, Jonas Haslbeck
**Date**: 2026-06-08
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.09283](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09283)

## Summary

This paper proposes a causal-dynamical framework to improve personalized mental health treatment by moving away from traditional population-based diagnostic classification. The approach estimates individual causal graphs from intensive longitudinal data to understand unique patient dynamics. By simulating potential treatment trajectories based on these causal relationships, the framework allows clinicians to identify the most effective intervention focus for a specific patient.

## Key Contributions

- Introduced a framework that reduces decision-making bias by transitioning from population-based guidelines to personalized treatment focus.
- Enabled individual-level treatment strategy selection through the construction of causal graphs from longitudinal patient data.
- Demonstrated a methodology for simulating future patient time-series under different interventions to compare long-term effectiveness.
