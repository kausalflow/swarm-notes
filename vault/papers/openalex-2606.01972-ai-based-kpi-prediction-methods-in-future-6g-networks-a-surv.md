# AI-Based KPI Prediction Methods in Future 6G Networks: A Survey

**Authors**: Niloofar Mehrnia, Gourav Prateek Sharma, Samie Mostafavi, Andreas Johnsson, Sinem Çöleri, Carlo Fischione, James Gross
**Date**: 2026-06-01
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.01972](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01972)

## Summary

This paper presents a comprehensive survey of machine learning-based KPI prediction methods aimed at enabling proactive network management for 6G systems. The authors introduce a multi-dimensional taxonomy to categorize existing approaches across diverse networking contexts, ranging from traditional statistical models to advanced deep and reinforcement learning. By analyzing the current state of the art and discussing deployment challenges like scalability and sustainability, the work provides a structured roadmap for future research in AI-native network automation.

## Key Contributions

- Provides the first comprehensive systematic review of data-driven KPI prediction methods for future 6G networks.
- Introduces a multi-dimensional taxonomy for KPI prediction classifying by KPI type, data source, network protocol stack, prediction horizon, model family, and prediction objective.
- Identifies critical research gaps and deployment challenges, including data availability, privacy, and sustainability for AI-native 6G network management.
