# Towards Generalized Hydrological Forecasting using Transformer Models for 120-Hour Streamflow Prediction

**Authors**: Bekir Zahit Demiray, İbrahim Demir
**Date**: 2026-06-24
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2406.07484](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.07484)

## Summary

This paper presents a generalized Transformer-based framework for 120-hour streamflow prediction across 125 distinct locations in Iowa, leveraging historical precipitation, evapotranspiration, and discharge data. Unlike conventional approaches that require site-specific model training, this study shows that a single Transformer model can effectively capture and adapt to varied hydrological dynamics across different geographies. Experimental results confirm that the Transformer consistently outperforms LSTM, GRU, and Persistence models, achieving improved metrics across all evaluated performance indicators.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that a generalized Transformer model outperforms traditional location-specific hydrological models by training on 125 diverse locations.
- Achieves superior predictive accuracy for 120-hour streamflow forecasting compared to LSTM, GRU, Seq2Seq, and Persistence baselines.
- Validates the model's robustness across diverse hydrological conditions using NSE, KGE, Pearson's r, and NRMSE metrics.
