# FIFA World Cup 2026 as a Contamination-Free Benchmark for LLM Forecasting Agents: Four Models, a Bookmaker, and 104 Matches

**Authors**: Jiacheng Ding, Cong Guo, J Q Xu
**Date**: 2026-07-20
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2607.17765](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.17765)

## Summary

The authors introduce WC2026-Agents, a contamination-free benchmark for evaluating large language models as autonomous forecasting agents on the 104 matches of the 2026 FIFA World Cup. By deploying four frontier LLMs in a search-act-reflect loop with virtual betting against pre-match betting market odds, the study evaluates probabilistic calibration, financial decision-making, and self-knowledge. Results reveal that although the models exhibit nearly identical top picks and fail to outperform the market's Brier score, they diverge sharply in economic return-on-investment, market utilization, and post-match reflection error rates.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces WC2026-Agents, a contamination-free benchmark and dataset evaluating LLMs as autonomous forecasting agents across all 104 matches of the 2026 FIFA World Cup.
- Compares four frontier LLMs against an economically grounded pre-match betting market baseline using a search-act-reflect loop with virtual betting and post-match reflection.
- Demonstrates that while all four models output identical top picks in 92% of matches and fail to beat the market's Brier score, they diverge significantly in decision-making quality, financial ROI (ranging from -18% to +10%), and self-reported error rates.
