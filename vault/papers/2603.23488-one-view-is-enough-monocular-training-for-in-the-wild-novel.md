# One View Is Enough! Monocular Training for In-the-Wild Novel View Generation

**Authors**: Adrien Ramanana Rahary, Nicolas Dufour, Patrick Perez, David Picard
**Date**: 2026-03-24
**ArXiv**: [2603.23488](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23488)

## Summary

This paper introduces OVIE, a novel approach for monocular novel-view synthesis that circumvents the need for multi-view supervision by training exclusively on large-scale, unpaired internet images. The core innovation involves using a pre-trained monocular depth estimator to create a 3D geometric scaffold for generating pseudo-target views during training. To manage the inherent noise and disocclusions from uncurated data, the authors propose a masked training loss applied only to valid, geometrically consistent regions. The resulting model achieves state-of-the-art zero-shot novel view generation performance while being computationally efficient at inference because it is geometry-free post-training.

## Key Contributions

- Introduction of OVIE, a model capable of monocular novel-view synthesis trained exclusively on unpaired, in-the-wild internet images.
- Development of a training methodology that leverages a monocular depth estimator as a temporary geometric scaffold to generate pseudo-target views for supervision.
- Proposal of a masked training formulation that restricts losses to geometrically consistent, valid regions to effectively handle disocclusions during training.
- Demonstration that OVIE achieves superior zero-shot performance compared to prior methods while being significantly faster (600x) at inference due to its geometry-free nature.

## Limitations

The method relies on an off-the-shelf monocular depth estimator during training, meaning the model's geometric accuracy is constrained by the performance of the estimator. It is primarily evaluated in a zero-shot setting, and its performance when fine-tuned or compared against multi-view supervised methods is not detailed.
