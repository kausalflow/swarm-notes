# One View Is Enough! Monocular Training for In-the-Wild Novel View Generation

## Summary

This paper introduces OVIE, a novel method for synthesizing new views from a single in-the-wild image, circumventing the traditional reliance on multi-view supervision. The core idea is to utilize a pre-trained monocular depth estimator during training to scaffold the process: the source image is lifted to 3D, transformed by a sampled camera, and projected to create a pseudo-target view for loss calculation. To enable training on vast, uncurated datasets, the authors propose a masked training formulation that selectively applies losses only to geometrically consistent regions, thereby managing the ambiguity introduced by disocclusions. Crucially, OVIE operates without any explicit 3D representation or depth estimation at inference time, achieving superior zero-shot performance and significantly faster generation speeds compared to existing baselines.

## Key Contributions

- Introduction of OVIE, a framework for monocular novel-view synthesis trained entirely on unpaired in-the-wild images without requiring multi-view supervision.
- Development of a training mechanism that uses a monocular depth estimator only to generate pseudo-target views via 3D lifting and projection.
- Design of a masked training formulation that restricts geometric, perceptual, and textural losses to valid regions to handle occlusions during self-supervised training.
- Demonstration of state-of-the-art zero-shot performance against prior methods while being significantly faster (600x) at inference time due to its geometry-free runtime design.

## Key Concepts

- [OVIE (One View Is Enough!)](../concepts/ovie-monocular-novel-view-synthesis.md): A model for monocular novel-view synthesis trained purely on unpaired, in-the-wild images by leveraging a depth estimator only during training.

## Limitations

The method relies on an external depth estimator during training, meaning the quality of the generated views is fundamentally tied to the accuracy of the pre-trained depth estimator. The geometry-free inference is achieved at the cost of retaining the learned geometric understanding implicitly.
