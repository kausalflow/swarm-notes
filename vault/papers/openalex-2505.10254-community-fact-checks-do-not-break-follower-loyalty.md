# Community Fact-Checks Do Not Break Follower Loyalty

**Authors**: Michelle Bobek, Nicolas Pröllochs
**Date**: 2026-04-09
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2505.10254](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.10254)

## Summary

This study investigates whether community-based fact-checking on social media platforms influences user follower retention. By analyzing time-series follower count data from 4,391 community-checked posts, the authors employ quasi-experimental methods to determine if such corrections lead to audience disengagement. The results indicate that fact-checks do not significantly reduce a spreader's follower count, suggesting high levels of follower loyalty to purveyors of misinformation. These findings highlight the limitations of current fact-checking mechanisms and suggest a requirement for more robust, multi-faceted interventions.

## Key Contributions

- Evaluated the impact of community-based fact-checks on user follower loyalty using quasi-experimental methods.
- Demonstrated that community fact-checks on social media platforms do not trigger significant follower loss for users posting misleading content.
- Provided empirical evidence that follower engagement remains largely immune to external correction mechanisms, highlighting the need for alternative misinformation interventions.
