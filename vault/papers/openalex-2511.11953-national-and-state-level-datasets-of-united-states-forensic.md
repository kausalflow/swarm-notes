# National and state-level datasets of United States forensic DNA databases 2001–2025

**Authors**: Yemko Pryor, Virum Ranka, Joao Pedro Donadio, Samantha C. Muller, Jenna Wilson, Tina Lasisi
**Date**: 2026-06-09
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2511.11953](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.11953)

## Summary

This paper introduces a comprehensive, harmonized dataset series documenting the structure, composition, and policy evolution of forensic DNA databases in the United States from 2001 to 2025. It integrates national-level statistics from the National DNA Index System (NDIS) with state-level metadata on collection laws and demographic reporting. By standardizing these disparate records, the work enables longitudinal and cross-jurisdictional research into forensic infrastructure growth and legislative variation. The released data provides a critical empirical foundation for investigating the historical development of forensic DNA systems.

## Key Contributions

- Assembles a comprehensive, longitudinal dataset series on US forensic DNA databases from 2001 to 2025.
- Reconstructs monthly time series of national-level NDIS statistics including offender, arrestee, and forensic profiles.
- Provides a harmonized state-level dataset covering collection statutes, familial search practices, and demographic breakdowns.
