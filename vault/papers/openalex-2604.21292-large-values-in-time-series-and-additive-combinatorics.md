# Large values in time series and additive combinatorics

**Authors**: Alex Iosevich, Dr. Vishal Gupta
**Date**: 2026-04-23
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.21292](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21292)

## Summary

This paper provides a rigorous mathematical framework to explain why extreme values in real-world time series often exhibit visible, structured patterns. By leveraging the Fourier ratio and a generalized version of Chang's lemma, the authors prove that sets of large values can be additively generated by small sets of integers. The theory is validated on US inflation and Delhi climate data, showing that surprisingly small generating sets suffice to span dozens of extreme points. These results offer a formal basis for the observation that extreme values in time series are information-rich and highly structured.

## Key Contributions

- Established a mathematical foundation for the structural organization of extreme time series values using additive combinatorics and discrete Fourier analysis.
- Demonstrated that small sets of coefficients can generate the set of largest time series values when the Fourier ratio is small.
- Empirically validated that a generating set of size 4–7 is sufficient to span large spectra in US inflation and Delhi climate datasets.
