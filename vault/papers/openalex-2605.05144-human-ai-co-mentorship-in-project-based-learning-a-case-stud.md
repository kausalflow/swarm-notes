# Human-AI Co-Mentorship in Project-Based Learning: A Case Study in Financial Forecasting

**Authors**: Freyaa Chawla, Ahan Chawla, Rishi Singh, Joe Germino, Grigorii Khvatskii
**Date**: 2026-05-06
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.05144](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05144)

## Summary

This paper explores a pedagogical approach to project-based learning where high-school and undergraduate students utilize AI-driven tools to execute financial forecasting tasks. By emphasizing workflow design and iterative interaction with AI assistants, students were able to focus on high-order problem formulation and conceptual understanding despite limited prior technical knowledge. The authors demonstrate that this model allows for accelerated skill development and practical model construction within a mentored, collaborative environment.

## Key Contributions

- Presents a project-based learning framework where AI tools enable high-school and undergraduate students to perform technical market analysis and financial forecasting.
- Demonstrates a shift from traditional classroom-based instruction to workflow-centered design for accelerated skill acquisition.
- Provides a case study on using AI-assisted iterative coding and daily stand-ups to bridge the gap between limited technical background and practical model building.
