# Krylov-space anatomy and spread complexity of a disordered quantum spin chain

**Authors**: Bikram Pain, David E. Logan, Sthitadhi Roy
**Date**: 2026-03-26
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2603.25724](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25724)

## Summary

This paper investigates the anatomy and complexity of quantum states within the Krylov space generated by the Hamiltonian of a disordered, interacting spin chain, comparing the ergodic and Many-Body Localized (MBL) phases. The authors demonstrate that the long-time Krylov spread complexity serves as a sharp diagnostic, scaling linearly with the Fock-space dimension in the ergodic phase but sublinearly in the MBL phase. Furthermore, the MBL phase exhibits a stretched-exponential decay profile for the infinite-time state along the Krylov chain. This difference is explained via a large-deviation analysis showing that MBL states are dominated by a small fraction of high-complexity eigenstates, whereas ergodic states utilize nearly all eigenstates for their composition.

## Key Contributions

- The Krylov spread complexity sharply distinguishes between the ergodic and Many-Body Localized (MBL) phases of a disordered quantum spin chain.
- In the ergodic phase, the infinite-time Krylov spread complexity scales linearly with the Fock-space dimension, signifying spread over a finite fraction of the Krylov chain.
- In the MBL phase, the complexity grows sublinearly, indicating the long-time state occupies only a vanishing fraction of the chain.
- The infinite-time state in the MBL phase exhibits a stretched-exponential decay profile along the Krylov chain, linked to a broad distribution of decay lengthscales.
- Large-deviation analysis shows the MBL complexity is dominated by a vanishing fraction of anomalously large-complexity eigenstates, unlike the ergodic phase which receives contributions from almost all eigenstates.

## Limitations

The analysis is performed on a one-dimensional, interacting, disordered spin chain, and the direct applicability to higher-dimensional or different disordered systems remains to be explored.
