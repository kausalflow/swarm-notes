# Indian Peak Power demand Forecasting : Transformer Based Implementation of Temporal Architecture

**Authors**: Vishvaditya Luhach, Shashwat Jha
**Date**: 2026-03-31
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2603.29179](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.29179)

## Summary

This paper investigates the challenge of long-term electricity peak demand forecasting, which is critical for future generation and transmission planning. The authors implement a Temporal Fusion Transformer-based deep learning model to address the volatility and unpredictability inherent in long-term energy projections. Experiments using a 6-year dataset of Indian power demand demonstrate that the proposed architecture provides superior performance and variance modeling compared to traditional forecasting approaches.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a Temporal Fusion Transformer (TFT) approach specifically adapted for the long-term forecasting of peak power demand in India.
- Demonstrates improved accuracy in capturing demand variance compared to standard forecasting baselines over a one-year prediction horizon.
- Evaluates the model on a 6-year historical peak power demand dataset, addressing the challenges of long-term temporal dependencies in energy infrastructure planning.
