# Influence of local and global solar magnetic fields on medium-term forecasting of the number and parameters of coronal mass ejections

**Authors**: I. A. Bilenko
**Date**: 2026-07-21
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2607.18971](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18971)

## Summary

This paper investigates the cyclic variations and differing behaviors of strong and weak coronal mass ejections (CMEs) across solar cycles 23-25. The author proposes a predictive method leveraging local and global solar magnetic fields to forecast CME parameters and cycle minima for cycles 26 and 27. By drawing analogies to cycle 20 and analyzing active regions, the method successfully yields CME forecasts for cycle 25 that show good agreement with observations.

## Key Contributions

- Proposed a method for separately predicting strong and weak coronal mass ejections (CMEs) based on local and global solar magnetic fields.
- Determined cycle parameters for cycles 26 and 27, including cycle minima timing and GMF sectorial dominance periods.
- Generated a Wolf number forecast for solar cycle 26 assuming similarities to cycle 20 to predict strong and weak CME occurrences.
