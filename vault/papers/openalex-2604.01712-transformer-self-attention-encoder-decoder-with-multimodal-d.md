# Transformer self-attention encoder-decoder with multimodal deep learning for response time series forecasting and digital twin support in wind structural health monitoring

**Authors**: Feiyu Zhou, Marios Impraimakis
**Date**: 2026-04-02
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.01712](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01712)

## Summary

This paper presents a transformer-based encoder-decoder model for forecasting wind-induced structural responses, serving as a core component for digital twin-based structural health monitoring. By leveraging the model's temporal capture capabilities, the framework identifies structural deviations through the comparison of vibration predictions against measured data without requiring assumptions about wind stationarity. The approach is validated using real-world monitoring data from the Hardanger Bridge, demonstrating its efficacy in adaptive, long-term infrastructure health assessment under varying environmental conditions.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a transformer-based encoder-decoder framework for wind-induced structural response forecasting and digital twin monitoring.
- Demonstrates that the model avoids assumptions regarding wind stationarity or normal vibration behavior, outperforming traditional approaches in dynamic structural health monitoring.
- Validates the framework's capability to detect structural deviations by comparing predictions with real-world sensor measurements from the Hardanger Bridge.
