# Negative redispatch power for green hydrogen production: Game changer or lame duck? A German perspective

**Authors**: Jonathan Brandt, Astrid Bensmann, Richard Hanke‐Rauschenbach
**Date**: 2026-04-15
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2508.06500](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.06500)

## Summary

This paper evaluates the economic viability of utilizing German regional redispatch markets to power water electrolysers for green hydrogen production. By modeling various power purchase scenarios against recreated historical redispatch time series, the authors analyze the impact of regulatory conditions and market dynamics on project costs. Findings suggest that while redispatch participation can offer significant cost advantages, its effectiveness is highly sensitive to price levels, storage configurations, and off-taker demand flexibility.

## Key Contributions

- Develops an economic model for green hydrogen production projects utilizing negative redispatch power to comply with EU regulations.
- Demonstrates that lower redispatch price levels can significantly reduce green hydrogen production costs while incentivizing system-beneficial electrolyser siting.
- Identifies project size, storage costs, and off-taker flexibility as primary determinants for the economic feasibility of electrolyser participation in redispatch markets.
