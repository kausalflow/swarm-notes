# Landau and fractionalized theories of periodically driven intertwined orders

**Authors**: Oriana K. Diessel, Subir Sachdev, Pietro M. Bonetti
**Date**: 2026-03-25
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2603.24592](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24592)

## Summary

This work investigates the phase diagrams of field theories featuring intertwined orders when subjected to periodic driving by a symmetry-preserving external field. The analysis considers two theoretical frameworks: a standard Landau theory for competing orders and a more complex fractionalized theory where order parameters emerge from composites of a multi-component Higgs field. Working in the large $N$ approximation coupled to a Markovian bath, the authors characterize the long-time steady states, which manifest as constant values, oscillations at the drive frequency or half-frequency, quasi-periodic behavior, or full chaos. The resulting phase diagrams offer insight into the stability and long-term dynamics of ordered phases under periodic perturbations.

## Key Contributions

- Derived the phase diagrams for field theories of intertwined orders under periodic driving that preserves all symmetries.
- Analyzed both conventional Landau theory and a fractionalized theory where order parameters are composites of an underlying multi-component Higgs field.
- Investigated the long-time behavior in the large N limit coupled to a Markovian bath, observing non-zero averages, periodic, quasi-periodic, or chaotic oscillations.

## Limitations

The analysis is conducted in the large $N$ limit and coupled to a Markovian bath, which simplifies the underlying dynamics and may omit non-Markovian or finite-$N$ effects.
