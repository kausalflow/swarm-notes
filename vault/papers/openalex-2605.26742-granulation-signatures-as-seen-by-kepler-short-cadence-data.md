# Granulation signatures as seen by Kepler short-cadence data. II. A hierarchical route to inferring stellar radii from granulation

**Authors**: Jens R. Larsen, Mia S. Lundkvist, Guy R. Davies, Martin B. Nielsen
**Date**: 2026-05-26
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.26742](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26742)

## Summary

This paper introduces a Bayesian hierarchical framework to infer stellar radii from photometric granulation signals observed in long-duration space-based data. By modeling the relationship between radius and granulation parameters—specifically total amplitude and characteristic frequency—alongside atmospheric variables, the study enables reliable stellar property estimation. The approach integrates multiple granulation models via Bayesian evidence weighting, achieving approximately 10% precision across diverse stellar populations. The technique provides a powerful diagnostic for stellar characterization applicable to current and future missions like Kepler, TESS, and PLATO.

## Key Contributions

- Constructs a Bayesian hierarchical model that links granulation observables (total amplitude, characteristic frequency) with stellar fundamental parameters (radius, effective temperature, metallicity).
- Develops a method to weight posterior radii derived from multiple background models using Bayesian evidence, ensuring robust estimation across different physical assumptions.
- Demonstrates an approximate 10% precision in stellar radius estimation, validated on an independent sample of 367 stars with 73% of recovered radii falling within the 1σ interval of reference values.
