# Deep Tech to Space: Space Data Centers and AI Revolution at the Edge

**Authors**: Jonas Weiß, Patricia Sagmeister, Gabriel Maiolini Capez, Dinesh Verma, Roberto Garello, Alberto Perotti, Dawid Lazaj, Alicja Musial, Jakub Nalepa, Thomas Morf, Martin Schmatz, Marek Krawczyk, Mateusz Przeliorz, Kevin Roche, Sagar Tayal, Mahalakshmi Lakshminarayanan, Nicolas Longépé, Pierre-Philippe Mathieu, Agata Wijata
**Date**: 2026-05-19
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.19892](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19892)

## Summary

This paper addresses the bottleneck of transmitting massive space-generated data to ground stations by proposing Space Data Centers (SDCs)—in-orbit, AI-driven, multi-tenant computing platforms. The authors detail the system architecture, including inter-satellite link topology, computational resource management, and software service orchestration. By utilizing forecasting models linked to technology roadmaps, the work assesses the technical and economic viability of scaling these orbital platforms for Earth observation and lunar exploration applications.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the Space Data Center (SDC) architecture for LEO constellations, integrating multi-tenant AI processing, inter-satellite networking, and software orchestration.
- Provides a comprehensive technical and economic feasibility analysis of orbital data centers using technology-roadmap-informed forecasting models.
- Validates the SDC concept through Earth observation and lunar exploration case studies, demonstrating potential for reduced latency and congestion compared to traditional ground-link workflows.
