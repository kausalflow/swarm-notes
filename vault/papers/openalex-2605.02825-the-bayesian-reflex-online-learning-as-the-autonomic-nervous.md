# The Bayesian Reflex: Online Learning as the Autonomic Nervous System of Modern and Future AI

**Authors**: Durba Bhattacharya, Sucharita Roy, Sourabh Bhattacharya
**Date**: 2026-05-04
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.02825](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02825)

## Summary

This paper introduces the 'Bayesian Reflex,' a unifying framework for online learning that treats AI adaptation similarly to the autonomic nervous system. The framework utilizes probabilistic belief maintenance, sequential Bayesian updates, and uncertainty-driven decision-making to maintain equilibrium in dynamic environments. Central to this approach are the 'look-up table' principle for inference and an ellipsoidal decomposition framework for nearly exact posterior sampling. The authors demonstrate the adaptability of these principles across applications ranging from complex climate modeling and time series analysis to deep architectures and theoretical number theory.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the 'Bayesian Reflex' framework, unifying online learning mechanisms through probabilistic belief maintenance, sequential updates, and uncertainty-driven action.
- Formalizes two core computational principles: the 'look-up table' principle for function space inference and 'ellipsoidal decomposition' for efficient posterior sampling.
- Demonstrates framework versatility by applying the methodology to diverse tasks including climate dynamics, prime number distribution analysis, and point process characterization.
