# Multiscale Comparison of Nonparametric Trend Curves

**Authors**: Marina Khismatullina, Michael Vogt
**Date**: 2026-06-01
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2209.10841](https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.10841)

## Summary

This paper proposes a multiscale econometric framework for the rigorous comparison of nonparametric time trends across multiple time series. The methodology allows researchers not only to test for equality of trends but also to identify the specific time intervals where discrepancies occur, providing localized confidence statements. Additionally, the authors introduce a clustering algorithm derived from this testing procedure to partition time series into groups sharing common trend behavior, supported by asymptotic theory and empirical applications.

## Key Contributions

- Develops a multiscale testing framework for formally comparing nonparametric time trends across multiple time series.
- Enables rigorous identification of specific time intervals where trends significantly differ between time series.
- Introduces a clustering algorithm based on the multiscale test to group time series with statistically indistinguishable trends.
- Provides asymptotic theoretical guarantees and empirical validation through GDP growth and house pricing data applications.
