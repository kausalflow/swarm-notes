# Anomalous, pre-yield grain-boundary sliding in copper revealed with in-situ high-resolution strain mapping

**Authors**: Benjamin Poole, David Lunt, Luke Hewitt, Chris Hardie
**Date**: 2026-04-29
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.26515](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26515)

## Summary

This study investigates deformation mechanisms in oxygen-free high-conductivity copper by performing in-situ tensile testing under a scanning electron microscope. By employing high-resolution digital image correlation, the authors capture time-series strain maps that reveal extensive, previously unrecognized grain boundary sliding occurring before macroscopic yield. The results show that grain boundary sliding induces higher extreme strain and in-plane rotation values compared to crystallographic slip during the initial stages of deformation. These findings provide a refined understanding of pre-yield deformation processes in metals.

## Key Contributions

- Identifies that grain boundary sliding occurs in copper at room temperature prior to macroscopic yield.
- Demonstrates using in-situ high-resolution digital image correlation (HRDIC) that grain boundary sliding generates higher localized strains than crystallographic slip before plastic deformation.
- Characterizes the sliding as pure in-plane, pure out-of-plane, or mixed-mode using combined HRDIC, confocal microscopy, and EBSD analysis.
