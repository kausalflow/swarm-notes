## Summary

This study presents near-ultraviolet spectroscopic observations of a sunspot using the Sunrise-III/SUSI instrument to analyze magnetohydrodynamic wave behavior in the solar atmosphere. By fitting over 44 spectral lines covering the photosphere to the low chromosphere, the authors extract intensity and line-of-sight velocity time series. Using wavelet power spectra and unsupervised clustering, the study identifies a complex, multifrequency structure in wave power up to 12 mHz that does not align with simple formation-height models.

## Key Contributions

- Presented systematic multiline wave signatures in a sunspot using near-ultraviolet spectroscopy from the Sunrise-III/SUSI instrument.
- Demonstrated the presence of significant wave power up to 12 mHz in the solar photosphere and low chromosphere.
- Revealed a progression of dominant frequencies from 2 to 10 mHz across different spectral lines using unsupervised clustering, challenging simple formation-height ranking models.
