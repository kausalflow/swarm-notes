# WaveSFNet: A Wavelet-Based Codec and Spatial--Frequency Dual-Domain Gating Network for Spatiotemporal Prediction

**Authors**: Xinyong Cai, Runming Xie, Hu Chen, Yuankai Wu
**Date**: 2026-03-24
**ArXiv**: [2603.23284](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23284)

## Summary

WaveSFNet is proposed as an efficient recurrent-free framework for spatiotemporal prediction, addressing the challenge of modeling long-range dynamics while preserving high-frequency details. The core innovation lies in integrating a wavelet-based codec to maintain high-frequency subband cues during feature resolution changes. The network's translator component injects adjacent-frame differences and employs a novel dual-domain gated fusion mechanism, combining large-kernel spatial modeling with frequency-domain global modulation. Experiments show WaveSFNet achieves competitive accuracy on Moving MNIST, TaxiBJ, and WeatherBench, offering a balance between prediction quality and computational efficiency.

## Key Contributions

- Introduction of WaveSFNet, an efficient framework for spatiotemporal prediction combining a wavelet-based codec with a dual-domain gated translator.
- Development of a wavelet-based codec that explicitly preserves high-frequency subband details during feature downsampling and reconstruction, mitigating detail loss common in strided convolution approaches.
- Design of a dual-domain gated spatiotemporal translator that explicitly enhances dynamic information via adjacent-frame differences and fuses large-kernel spatial modeling with frequency-domain global modulation.
- Demonstration of competitive prediction accuracy on benchmarks like Moving MNIST, TaxiBJ, and WeatherBench while maintaining low computational complexity.
