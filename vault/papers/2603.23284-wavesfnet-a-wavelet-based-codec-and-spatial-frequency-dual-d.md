# WaveSFNet: A Wavelet-Based Codec and Spatial--Frequency Dual-Domain Gating Network for Spatiotemporal Prediction

## Summary

WaveSFNet is proposed as an efficient, recurrent-free framework for spatiotemporal prediction aimed at resolving the trade-off between modeling long-range dynamics and preserving high-frequency details. The core innovation lies in coupling a wavelet-based codec, which preserves essential high-frequency subband cues during sampling, with a spatial--frequency dual-domain gated spatiotemporal translator. This translator explicitly enhances dynamic information using adjacent-frame differences before fusing large-kernel spatial modeling with frequency-domain global modulation. Extensive experiments across Moving MNIST, TaxiBJ, and WeatherBench show that WaveSFNet delivers competitive accuracy while sustaining low computational overhead.

## Key Contributions

- Proposing WaveSFNet, an efficient recurrent-free framework for spatiotemporal prediction that integrates a wavelet-based codec for detail preservation.
- Introducing a spatial--frequency dual-domain gated spatiotemporal translator that explicitly enhances dynamic information via adjacent-frame differences.
- Implementing a dual-domain fusion mechanism combining large-kernel spatial modeling and frequency-domain modulation for superior prediction quality.
- Achieving competitive prediction accuracy on Moving MNIST, TaxiBJ, and WeatherBench benchmarks while maintaining low computational complexity.

## Key Concepts

- [Wavelet-Based Codec](../concepts/wavelet-based-codec.md): A codec designed to preserve high-frequency subband cues during the downsampling and reconstruction processes for spatiotemporal data.
- [Spatial--Frequency Dual-Domain Gating](../concepts/spatial-frequency-dual-domain-gating.md): A mechanism within the spatiotemporal translator that fuses large-kernel spatial local modeling with frequency-domain global modulation.
