## Summary

The study reports the discovery of a multi-planet system orbiting the G-type star TOI-5624, comprised of four transiting sub-Neptunes and one additional non-transiting companion. By combining TESS and CHEOPS transit photometry with HARPS-N and SOPHIE radial velocity measurements, the authors performed a high-precision dynamical analysis using N-body simulations. This allowed for the detection of transit timing variations (TTVs) and provided mass and radius characterizations that rival the precision only previously achieved for the TRAPPIST-1 system.

## Key Contributions

- Discovered and characterized a multi-planet system around TOI-5624 containing four transiting sub-Neptunes and one non-transiting outer companion.
- Attained sub-1.7% precision in planetary radii measurements using integrated TESS and CHEOPS photometric data.
- Used N-body dynamic analysis and Transit Timing Variations (TTVs) to confirm the existence and masses of the planets in the system.
