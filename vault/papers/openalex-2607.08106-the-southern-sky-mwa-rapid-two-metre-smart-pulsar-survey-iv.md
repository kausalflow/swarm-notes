# The Southern-sky MWA Rapid Two-metre (SMART) pulsar survey--IV. Survey update and an atlas of 205 non-recycled southern pulsars

**Authors**: N. D. R. Bhat, C. P. Lee, SJ Mcsweeney, B. W. Meyers, C. M. Tan, M. Xue, Q. Fu, N. A. Swainston, S. M. Ord, G. J. Sleap, S. E. Tremblay, W. van Straten, A. Williams, C. Di Pientrantonio, C. J. Harris, P.J. Elahi
**Date**: 2026-07-09
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2607.08106](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08106)

## Summary

The Southern-sky MWA Rapid Two-metre (SMART) survey provides high-sensitivity pulsar and fast transient searches at low frequencies (140–170 MHz) by utilizing raw voltage recording. This paper details the survey's progress and presents an atlas of 205 non-recycled pulsars, including critical diagnostic measurements like dispersion and rotation measures. These results serve as a benchmark for low-frequency pulsar detection, aiding future large-scale science programs with the SKA-Low observatory.

## Key Contributions

- Presents the SMART pulsar survey, detailing the systematic search and re-detection of pulsars at 140–170 MHz.
- Provides an atlas of 205 non-recycled southern pulsars including pulse profiles, dispersion measures, rotation measures, and mean flux densities.
- Establishes a low-frequency pulsar reference catalogue to facilitate science verification and survey planning for the future SKA-Low observatory.
