# Europe and the Geopolitics of AGI: The Need for a Preparedness Plan

**Authors**: Maximilian Negele, Daan Juijn, Afek Shamir, David Janků, Bengüsu Özcan, Lisa Soder, Lucia Velasco, Max Reddel, Michiel Bakker, Lorenzo Pacchiardi, Maksym Andriushchenko
**Date**: 2026-05-13
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.13634](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13634)

## Summary

This paper investigates the geopolitical impact of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) and assesses Europe's preparedness for its emergence. By synthesizing empirical AI trends and expert forecasting, the authors project a plausible AGI timeline between 2030 and 2040 and highlight how such systems could disrupt global power dynamics. The study concludes that Europe faces significant strategic and structural deficits, recommending a coordinated policy agenda to enhance institutional situational awareness and AI value chain resilience.

## Key Contributions

- Provides a strategic analysis of AGI emergence timelines, estimating a plausible window between 2030 and 2040.
- Identifies systemic gaps in European preparedness, including infrastructure limitations, talent retention, and fragmented governance.
- Proposes a policy framework for Europe focused on institutional capacity for AGI situational awareness, strengthening value chain competitiveness, and fostering international stability.
