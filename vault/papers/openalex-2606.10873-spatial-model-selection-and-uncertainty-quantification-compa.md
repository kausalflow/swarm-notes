# Spatial Model Selection and Uncertainty Quantification: Comparing Continuous and Discrete Wound Healing Models

**Authors**: John T. Nardini, Jana L. Gevertz
**Date**: 2026-06-09
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.10873](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10873)

## Summary

This paper introduces a model selection pipeline designed to systematically evaluate continuous (PDE) and discrete (ABM) modeling modalities for spatial processes. Using Approximate Bayesian Computation (ABC), the framework performs parameter estimation and uncertainty quantification, revealing critical tradeoffs between computational efficiency and model selection preferences. The study demonstrates that while PDE models are drastically faster, they are frequently selected by information criteria even when the data is generated by an ABM, highlighting the importance of explicit model selection in scientific practice. Finally, the authors apply this pipeline to wound healing data to identify an optimal, albeit uncertain, PDE-based model.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a systematic pipeline for model selection and uncertainty quantification to navigate the tradeoff between continuous (PDE) and discrete (ABM) spatial modeling modalities.
- Demonstrates that while PDE models are significantly more computationally efficient (over 1000x) and frequently preferred by information criteria, they can exhibit higher parametric uncertainty compared to ABMs.
- Identifies a PDE model with cell pulling and time delay as the most appropriate fit for public wound healing data, providing a benchmark application for the proposed framework.
