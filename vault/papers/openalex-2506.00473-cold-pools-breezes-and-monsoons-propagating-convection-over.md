# Cold Pools, Breezes, and Monsoons: Propagating Convection Over New Guinea

**Authors**: Mingyue Tang, Jimy Dudhia, Changhai Liu, Giuseppe Torri
**Date**: 2026-07-04
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2506.00473](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.00473)

## Summary

This study investigates the physical mechanisms behind the pronounced offshore propagation of diurnal convection over New Guinea using GPM satellite data and convection-permitting simulations. It identifies two specific propagation modes driven by multi-scale thermally driven flows, including sea breezes and land breezes intensified by cold pools. The findings demonstrate how these processes, in concert with monsoonal winds, sustain long-range convective systems, providing critical insights for improving climate and weather forecasting models in the Maritime Continent.

## Key Contributions

- Identified two distinct diurnal convective propagation modes over New Guinea: a ridge-to-coast mode and an over-ocean mode separated by a 100 km gap.
- Demonstrated that sea-breeze fronts interrupt the first propagation mode via cooling, while hybrid land breezes combined with cold pools facilitate the second mode's offshore regeneration.
- Quantified the impact of monsoonal background winds in sustaining precipitation over 200–600 km, providing a physical basis for improving precipitation simulations over the Maritime Continent.
