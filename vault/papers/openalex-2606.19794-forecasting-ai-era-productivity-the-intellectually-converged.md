# Forecasting AI-Era Productivity: The Intellectually Converged Human Framework and a Missing Cognitive Mediator in Production Function Theory

**Authors**: Kwan Soo Shin, In Seok Kang
**Date**: 2026-06-18
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.19794](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19794)

## Summary

The paper addresses the AI productivity paradox by proposing the Intellectually Converged Human (ICH) framework, which models effective productive capacity as a function of both human capital and a newly identified mediator called 'Convergence Capacity' (C). By incorporating C—a construct involving metacognition, temporal integration, and integrative thinking—into the production function, the model explains why AI investment alone fails to yield commensurate growth. The authors provide cross-national evidence across 20 OECD economies and offer a falsifiable 10-year forecast for measuring AI-driven TFP improvements.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the ICH framework to replace traditional separable AI production functions with a human-centered, cognitively mediated model.
- Defines 'Convergence Capacity' as a critical four-dimensional cognitive mediator (embodied understanding, metacognition, temporal integration, and integrative thinking) missing from current economic theory.
- Demonstrates that the interaction between AI utilization and Convergence Capacity explains 86% of TFP variance across 20 OECD nations compared to only 31% for AI alone.
