# Human Capital, Not Model Benchmarks, Predicts Hybrid Intelligence in Forecasting

**Authors**: Vivienne Ming
**Date**: 2026-07-02
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2607.02467](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02467)

## Summary

This study investigates human-AI collaboration in forecasting using real-money prediction market data to assess how different human cognitive and collaborative traits influence hybrid outcomes. It reveals that hybrid forecasting performance is not a monolithic average but trimodal, where most users either blindly defer to or ignore AI models. Only a minority of users, characterized by high intellectual humility and curiosity, engage in genuine complementary reasoning to achieve superior forecasting accuracy.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that human-AI forecasting performance is trimodal, rather than a single average effect, characterized by model deferral, rubber-stamping, or complementary reasoning.
- Identifies that collaborative traits like perspective-taking and intellectual humility are stronger predictors of hybrid forecasting success than raw cognitive ability or model-specific benchmarks.
- Validates findings using real-money prediction market resolutions (Polymarket) as an objective, externally resolved performance benchmark.
