# GARTFIMA Models: A Class of Observation-Driven Models with Tempered Fractional Dynamics

**Authors**: Guilherme Pumi, Sharandeep Singh Pandher, Taiane Schaedler Prass
**Date**: 2026-07-21
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2607.19311](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19311)

## Summary

This paper introduces GARTFIMA models, a novel class of observation-driven time series models featuring a systematic component with a tempered fractional differencing term to generalize long-range dependent processes. The authors develop a partial maximum likelihood estimation procedure alongside rigorous inferential tools such as hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, and forecasting evaluation. Finite-sample performance is validated through Monte Carlo simulations, and practical utility is demonstrated via an empirical forecasting application.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces GARTFIMA models, a class of observation-driven time series models incorporating tempered fractional dynamics that generalize long-range dependent models.
- Develops a partial maximum likelihood estimation approach along with corresponding inference tools including hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, and goodness-of-fit assessment.
- Demonstrates the finite-sample performance of the proposed estimation framework via Monte Carlo simulations and illustrates practical utility through an empirical forecasting application.
