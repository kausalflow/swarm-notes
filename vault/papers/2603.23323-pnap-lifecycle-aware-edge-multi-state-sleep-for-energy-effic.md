# PNap: Lifecycle-aware Edge Multi-state sleep for Energy Efficient MEC

**Authors**: Federico Giarrè, Holger Karl
**Date**: 2026-03-24
**ArXiv**: [2603.23323](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23323)

## Summary

This paper addresses the high energy consumption in Multi-access Edge Computing (MEC) systems caused by keeping servers active during low-load phases. The authors propose PowerNap (PNap), a novel orchestration framework that integrates server power management (multi-state sleep) with service lifecycle management. PNap leverages traffic forecasting to intelligently decide when to transition servers into deeper sleep states, thereby minimizing active server count while respecting the non-instantaneous wake-up delays required for service continuity. Evaluations show that PNap significantly reduces energy usage by up to 14.9% compared to state-of-the-art methods while successfully matching their service availability metrics.

## Key Contributions

- Introduction of PowerNap (PNap), a lifecycle-aware orchestration framework for Multi-access Edge Computing (MEC) servers.
- Jointly managing server sleep states and service lifecycle states to balance energy saving and service continuity.
- Leveraging traffic forecasting to dynamically minimize the number of active edge servers while maintaining service availability.
- Demonstrating up to 14.9% energy consumption reduction compared to a state-of-the-art solution while achieving equivalent service availability.
