# PNap: Lifecycle-aware Edge Multi-state sleep for Energy Efficient MEC

## Summary

This paper addresses the high energy consumption of Multi-access Edge Computing (MEC) infrastructure during low-load phases by proposing PowerNap (PNap), a lifecycle-aware orchestration framework. PNap jointly manages the progressively deeper sleep states of edge servers and the non-instantaneous lifecycle operations of hosted services to balance energy savings against wake-up latency penalties. By leveraging traffic forecasting, the framework optimizes the number of active servers to ensure service continuity, a crucial coupling often overlooked in pure energy-saving strategies. Experimental results demonstrate that PNap achieves up to 14.9% energy reduction compared to existing state-of-the-art methods while preserving service availability metrics.

## Key Contributions

- Introduction of PowerNap (PNap), a novel orchestration framework that integrates multi-state server sleep management with service lifecycle management for MEC.
- Development of a joint optimization strategy that uses traffic forecasting to minimize the number of active edge servers and concurrently avoid service disruptions.
- Demonstration that PNap reduces energy consumption by up to 14.9% compared to a state-of-the-art solution while maintaining equivalent service availability.

## Key Concepts

- [PowerNap (PNap)](../concepts/power-nap-pnap.md): A lifecycle-aware orchestration framework that jointly manages edge server sleep states and service lifecycle states to minimize energy consumption while ensuring service continuity.

## Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly detail limitations, but the reliance on accurate traffic forecasting implies vulnerability to forecasting errors.
