# Comparative Evaluation of Modern Deep Learning Methodologies for Portfolio Optimization

**Authors**: Samuel Ozechi, Banjo Francis, Wisdom Yakanu, Joe Wayne Byers
**Date**: 2026-04-27
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.24486](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24486)

## Summary

This study conducts a comparative performance analysis of deep learning architectures—including GNNs, Transformers, and DRL—for portfolio optimization tasks. The research finds that while pure deep learning models often struggle with structural market awareness, hybrid approaches effectively capture both temporal dependencies and relational asset structures to enhance stability. The empirical results suggest that the most robust portfolios emerge from integrating these advanced feature extractors into classical financial optimization frameworks.

## Key Contributions

- Evaluates the predictive performance of GNNs, DRL, Transformers, and Autoencoders in a unified portfolio optimization framework.
- Demonstrates that hybrid models (e.g., Transformer+GNN) significantly outperform standalone deep learning architectures in volatility reduction and risk control.
- Confirms that traditional Mean-Variance Optimization (MVO), when supplied with deep-learning-based feature inputs, maintains superior performance in cumulative returns and Sharpe ratios compared to pure deep learning agents.
