# OccAny: Generalized Unconstrained Urban 3D Occupancy

**Authors**: Anh-Quan Cao, Tuan-Hung Vu
**Date**: 2026-03-24
**ArXiv**: [2603.23502](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23502)

## Summary

OccAny addresses the lack of scalability and generalization in current 3D occupancy prediction methods, which often rely on precise sensor priors and in-domain annotations. This work introduces a novel, unconstrained framework that operates on sequential, monocular, or surround-view images from uncalibrated scenes to predict metric occupancy coupled with segmentation features. Key innovations include Segmentation Forcing, which improves occupancy quality and allows for mask-level output, and a Novel View Rendering pipeline for geometry completion via test-time augmentation. Experiments show OccAny surpasses visual geometry baselines on urban occupancy prediction tasks while maintaining strong competitiveness with specialized in-domain methods.

## Key Contributions

- Proposed OccAny, the first generalized 3D occupancy framework capable of handling out-of-domain, uncalibrated urban input views (sequential, monocular, or surround-view).
- Introduced Segmentation Forcing to enhance occupancy quality and enable mask-level prediction directly from the model.
- Developed a Novel View Rendering pipeline that infers novel-view geometry to facilitate test-time view augmentation for robust geometry completion.
- Demonstrated state-of-the-art performance over visual geometry baselines on 3D occupancy prediction and competitive performance against in-domain self-supervised methods.

## Limitations

The paper does not explicitly list limitations, but the reliance on out-of-domain generalization suggests potential degradation in highly novel or exceptionally cluttered scenes not represented in general visual geometry models.
