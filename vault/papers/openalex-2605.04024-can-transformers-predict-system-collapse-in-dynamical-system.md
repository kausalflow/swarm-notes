# Can Transformers predict system collapse in dynamical systems?

**Authors**: Zheng-Meng Zhai, Celso Grebogi, Ying-Cheng Lai
**Date**: 2026-05-05
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.04024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04024)

## Summary

This paper investigates the effectiveness of Transformer architectures in predicting catastrophic collapse in nonlinear dynamical systems through parameter-space extrapolation. While Transformers are often proposed as foundation models for dynamics reconstruction, the authors demonstrate that they fail to generalize to unseen parameter regimes where a system undergoes bifurcation. Conversely, reservoir computing is shown to reliably predict these transitions, suggesting that current Transformer attention mechanisms are insufficient for capturing parameter-dependent temporal evolution.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that Transformer architectures consistently fail to predict system collapse (bifurcation points) in nonlinear dynamical systems when extrapolated to unseen parameter regimes.
- Establishes that while reservoir computing effectively captures the underlying dynamical climate and its parameter-dependent evolution, Transformers struggle with this form of temporal generalization.
- Identifies fundamental limitations of permutation-invariant attention mechanisms in capturing parameter-dependent structural changes in nonlinear dynamics.
