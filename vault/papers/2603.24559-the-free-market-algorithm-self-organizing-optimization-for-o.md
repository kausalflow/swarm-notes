# The Free-Market Algorithm: Self-Organizing Optimization for Open-Ended Complex Systems

**Authors**: Martin Jaraiz
**Date**: 2026-03-25
**ArXiv**: [2603.24559](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24559)

## Summary

The Free-Market Algorithm (FMA) is a novel metaheuristic optimization approach modeled after free-market economics, using distributed supply-and-demand dynamics rather than fixed fitness functions or centralized control. It features a universal market mechanism layer coupled with pluggable, domain-specific behavioral rules. The FMA demonstrates exceptional capability in open-ended search, successfully discovering the fundamental building blocks of life (amino acids, nucleobases) from basic atoms and achieving competitive macroeconomic GDP forecasts with zero parameter fitting. This decentralized, emergent optimization framework suggests a fundamental organizational principle applicable to complex physical and economic systems.

## Key Contributions

- Introduction of the Free-Market Algorithm (FMA), a decentralized metaheuristic where fitness is emergent and the search space is open-ended, structured around supply-and-demand dynamics.
- Demonstration of FMA's capability to autonomously discover complex chemical intermediates (amino acids, nucleobases) starting only from fundamental atoms in prebiotic chemistry simulations.
- Achieving state-of-the-art macroeconomic forecasting performance (MAE of 0.42% for GDP) using only structural input-output tables without explicit parameter estimation.
- Providing an explicit, tunable mechanism for generating the selection signatures described by Assembly Theory.

## Limitations

The paper focuses heavily on demonstrating capability in two disparate domains rather than providing a generalized theoretical analysis of the algorithm's convergence properties across all potential search spaces.
