# Convective and non-convective nature of Balearic meteotsunamis: a 50-year historical review

**Authors**: A. Jansà, Diego S. Carrió, Carlos Alberto de Melo, R. Romero, Baptiste Mourre, V. Homar
**Date**: 2026-06-22
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2512.10535](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.10535)

## Summary

This study provides a 50-year historical review of meteotsunamis (rissaga) in the Balearic Islands, cataloging 191 events to analyze the impact of convective versus non-convective atmospheric disturbances. The authors demonstrate that while non-convective events triggered by internal gravity waves are more frequent, convective events drive the most extreme sea-level oscillations. By evaluating multiple modeling approaches, the research shows that no single system consistently predicts both types, highlighting the need for hybrid, mechanism-aware early-warning frameworks.

## Key Contributions

- Presents a comprehensive 50-year dataset of 191 meteotsunami events in Ciutadella, Balearic Islands, from 1975 to 2025.
- Establishes a diagnostic distinction between convective and non-convective atmospheric triggers, showing that convective events are responsible for all recorded extremes exceeding 220 cm.
- Demonstrates that forecasting performance is mechanism-dependent: high-resolution coupled modelling (BRIFS) excels for convective events, while reduced-physics methods (TRAM) are superior for non-convective triggers.
