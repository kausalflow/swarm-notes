# Simultaneous Tricolor Video Observations of Three Tiny Near-Earth Asteroids with Sub-Minute Rotation Periods

**Authors**: Jin Beniyama, Ryou Ohsawa, Shigeyuki Sako, S. Takita, Daisuke Kuroda, Tomohiko Sekiguchi, Keisuke Isogai
**Date**: 2026-06-15
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.17047](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17047)

## Summary

This paper presents simultaneous g-, r-, and i-band high-cadence video observations of three tiny near-Earth asteroids using the TriCCS instrument on the 3.8 m Seimei Telescope. The study addresses the challenge of systematic color offsets inherent in sequential photometry for fast-rotating objects with sub-minute periods. By capturing lightcurves at 1-5 second exposures, the authors identify significant surface color heterogeneity on one of the targets and classify the compositions of all three objects within the X- and S-complexes.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates the efficacy of high-cadence, simultaneous multiband photometry using the TriColor CMOS Camera and Spectrograph (TriCCS) to mitigate systematic offsets in lightcurve analysis for fast-rotating objects.
- Determines precise sub-minute rotation periods for three tiny near-Earth asteroids (2021 TY14, 2021 UW1, and 2022 GQ1), confirming their status as fast rotators.
- Identifies surface color heterogeneity on 2021 TY14, revealing a projected spot fraction of approximately 50% through high-frequency time-series color analysis.
