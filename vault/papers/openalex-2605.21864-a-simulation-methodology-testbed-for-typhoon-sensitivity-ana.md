# A Simulation Methodology Testbed for Typhoon Sensitivity Analysis: Framework Development and Perturbation-Response Experiments with the Pangu Weather Model

**Authors**: Yuehua Peng, Yuchen Zhang, Qin Huang, Chengzhi Ye, Jingsong Yang
**Date**: 2026-05-21
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.21864](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21864)

## Summary

This study introduces a specialized simulation testbed to analyze how typhoon tracks and intensities respond to localized environmental perturbations. By coupling the Pangu weather forecasting model with a PID control system, the platform enables precise, modular testing of perturbation-response dynamics. This methodology bridges the gap between low-order dynamical models and operational-grade AI forecasting, offering a foundation for future research in weather intervention and predictability.

## Key Contributions

- Developed a modular simulation testbed for typhoon sensitivity analysis by integrating the Pangu-Weather AI model with PID control feedback.
- Implemented a cross-platform MATLAB/ONNX framework to enable controlled velocity and thermal perturbation-response experiments.
- Quantified input-output coupling and energy-scale response characteristics, demonstrating the testbed's viability for analyzing typhoon predictability and intervention potential.
