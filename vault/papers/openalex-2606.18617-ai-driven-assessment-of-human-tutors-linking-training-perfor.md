# AI-Driven Assessment of Human Tutors: Linking Training Performance to Real-Life Practice

**Authors**: D Thomas, Marie Cynthia Abijuru Kamikazi, Clara Brandt, Conrad Borchers, Kenneth R. Koedinger
**Date**: 2026-06-17
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.18617](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18617)

## Summary

This paper presents an AI-driven assessment framework that bridges the gap between simulated human tutor training and actual real-life practice. By analyzing transcriptions of authentic tutoring sessions using Gemini-2.5-pro, the system measures the transferability of pedagogical skills. Quantitative analysis of 86 human math tutors reveals that training performance is a statistically significant predictor of real-world tutoring quality, with open-ended responses offering superior predictive value compared to multiple-choice tasks. The study also offers a set of open resources to facilitate reproducibility in AI-based educational research.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces an AI-driven framework utilizing Gemini-2.5-pro to assess human tutor performance by linking training simulations to authentic real-life tutoring sessions.
- Demonstrates that training performance in scenario-based lessons significantly predicts real-life pedagogical execution quality with an effect size of 0.25 SD.
- Provides a transparent, reproducible pipeline for tutor assessment through open datasets, validated prompt sets, and scoring rubrics.
