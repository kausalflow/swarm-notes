# Detecting Diffusion-Generated Time Series Under Generator Shift

**Authors**: Zhi Wen Soi, Aditya Shankar, Gert Lek, Abele Mălan, Daniel Neider, Jian-Jia Chen, Lydia Chen
**Date**: 2026-05-27
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.28355](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28355)

## Summary

This paper investigates the detectability of diffusion-generated time series, contrasting white-box reconstruction-based methods with black-box classification techniques. The authors demonstrate that while reconstruction-based approaches are standard in image forensics, they falter in the time series domain due to the lack of universal generator priors, particularly when generator shift occurs. Instead, simple black-box classifiers are shown to be significantly more effective, providing a new benchmark for synthetic time series identification.

## Key Contributions

- Provides the first systematic exploration comparing white-box and black-box detection methods specifically for diffusion-generated time series.
- Demonstrates that reconstruction-based white-box detection, effective in the image domain, fails for time series under generator shift due to the lack of a universal reconstruction prior.
- Establishes that off-the-shelf classifiers achieve superior detection performance (79.2 F1 score) compared to reconstruction-based approaches, proving that time series detection does not directly translate from the image domain.
