# Correlation analysis of the dispersion of SARS-CoV-2 in Mexico

**Authors**: Pablo Carlos López, Marcos Carias Flores, Soham Biswas
**Date**: 2026-04-02
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.01735](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01735)

## Summary

This paper introduces a method for analyzing the correlations between non-stationary pandemic-related time series across different geographical regions in Mexico. By focusing on post-pandemic data, the approach evaluates the collective societal response, including mobility patterns, vaccination campaigns, and regulatory restrictions. The framework shifts the paradigm from traditional medical modeling toward understanding the complex long-term dynamics of pandemic spread and control strategies.

## Key Contributions

- Introduced a non-stationary time series correlation analysis framework for comparing pandemic data across geographical regions.
- Shifted the analytical focus from real-time epidemiological modeling to post-pandemic assessment of societal response impacts.
- Provided a methodology to quantify how measures like lockdowns and mobility restrictions manifest in regional collective behavior dynamics.
