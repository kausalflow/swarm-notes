# Forecasting neutrino mass constraints from the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope

**Authors**: Francesco Spezzati, Yun Wang, Andrew Hearin
**Date**: 2026-04-16
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.14504](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14504)

## Summary

This paper provides realistic forecasts for the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope's ability to constrain the absolute neutrino mass scale using full-shape analyses of galaxy power spectra. By utilizing simulated H-alpha emission-line galaxy mock catalogs covering a significant redshift range and sky area, the authors evaluate two inference frameworks: a model-dependent Effective Field Theory (EFT) of Large-Scale Structure approach and a model-independent phenomenological approach. The study finds that Roman can provide competitive constraints on neutrino mass and fundamental cosmological parameters like H0 and Omega_m, highlighting the potential of the upcoming Stage IV survey.

## Key Contributions

- Provides realistic neutrino mass scale forecasts for the Roman Space Telescope using full-shape galaxy power spectrum analysis on simulated lightcone mock catalogs.
- Compares model-dependent EFT of LSS and model-independent phenomenological approaches for cosmological parameter inference.
- Demonstrates that the Roman High Latitude Wide Area Spectroscopic Survey can constrain neutrino mass at sub-0.3 eV levels (68% C.L.) when combined with Planck priors.

## Limitations

Forecasts depend on the accuracy of the simulated lightcone mock catalogs and the specific assumptions made regarding priors and survey characteristics.
