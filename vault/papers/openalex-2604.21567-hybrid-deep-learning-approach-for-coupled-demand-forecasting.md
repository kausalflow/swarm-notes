# Hybrid Deep Learning Approach for Coupled Demand Forecasting and Supply Chain Optimization

**Authors**: Nusrat Yasmin Nadia, Md Habibul Arif, Habibor Rahman Rabby, Md Iftekhar Monzur Tanvir, Md. Jakir Hossen, M. F. Mridha
**Date**: 2026-04-23
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.21567](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21567)

## Summary

This paper presents the Hybrid AI Framework for Demand-Supply Forecasting and Optimization (HAF-DS), which couples an LSTM-based predictive model with a mixed-integer linear programming (MILP) prescriptive layer. By integrating temporal demand modeling directly with inventory allocation decisions, the framework simultaneously minimizes forecasting errors and operational costs. Evaluated on textile and PPE supply chain datasets, the approach demonstrates superior performance over standalone statistical and deep learning models in both predictive accuracy and supply chain efficiency.

## Key Contributions

- Proposed the Hybrid AI Framework for Demand-Supply Forecasting and Optimization (HAF-DS), integrating LSTM-based predictive forecasting with an MILP prescriptive optimization layer.
- Demonstrated that joint optimization of forecasting error and operational cost outperforms isolated approaches in textile and PPE supply chain scenarios.
- Achieved a 14.7% reduction in MAE and a 27.5% reduction in stockouts on textile supply chain datasets compared to baseline methods.
