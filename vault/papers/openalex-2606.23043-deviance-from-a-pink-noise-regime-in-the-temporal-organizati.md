# Deviance from a pink noise regime in the temporal organization of semantic relations in psychosis

**Authors**: Paola Moreno Ancalmo, Emre Bora, Rieke Roxanne Mülfarth, Svenja Seuffert, Tilo Kircher, Frederike Stein, Philipp Homan, Wolfram Hinzen
**Date**: 2026-06-22
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.23043](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23043)

## Summary

This paper investigates the temporal structure of language in psychosis by applying detrended fluctuation analysis (DFA) to semantic similarity time series derived from BERT embeddings. The study identifies that semantic discourse in patients with psychosis deviates from a 'pink noise' scale-invariant regime, showing abnormally strong long-range correlations and excessive persistence. These findings suggest that linguistic anomalies in psychosis can be quantified as a breakdown in the multi-timescale organization of meaning, paralleling findings in brain function.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies temporal scaling of semantic discourse using Detrended Fluctuation Analysis (DFA) on BERT-derived cosine-similarity trajectories.
- Demonstrates that patients with psychosis exhibit significantly elevated DFA scaling exponents, reflecting abnormally strong long-range persistence in semantic fluctuations compared to controls.
- Links semantic discourse anomalies in psychosis to deviations from critical pink noise regimes observed in physiological and brain function studies.
