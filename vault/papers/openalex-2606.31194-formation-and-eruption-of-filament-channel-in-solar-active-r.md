# Formation and Eruption of Filament Channel in Solar Active Region 12975: Insights from Observations and Simulations of Magnetic Field Evolution

**Authors**: Dinesh Mishra, P. Vemareddy, Brajesh Kumar
**Date**: 2026-06-30
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.31194](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31194)

## Summary

This study utilizes a time-dependent magnetofrictional (TMF) model to simulate the magnetic evolution and eruption of solar active region 12975. By driving the model with observed photospheric vector-magnetograms, the authors successfully replicate the formation of a sigmoidal, twisted flux rope. The results demonstrate high morphological accuracy while providing critical insights into the quantitative limitations of using helicity ratios as predictors for eruptive instability in varied magnetic environments.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that TMF modeling, driven by time-series photospheric vector-magnetograms, accurately reproduces the sigmoidal, twisted flux rope (FR) development.
- Shows that modeled magnetic energy and helicity evolution are consistent with observed injection rates in solar active region 12975.
- Identifies quantitative discrepancies between current-carrying helicity ratios and torus-instability thresholds, highlighting challenges in predicting eruptive behavior for diverse AR configurations.
