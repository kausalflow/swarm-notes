# Quo vadis, stochastic thermodynamics?

**Authors**: Jan Korbel, Artemy Kolchinsky, Sarah A. M. Loos, Gonzalo Manzano, Rosalba Garcia-Millan, Olga Movilla Miangolarra, Édgar Roldán
**Date**: 2026-04-29
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.26601](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26601)

## Summary

This perspective article reviews the current state and future trajectories of stochastic thermodynamics, a framework for quantifying thermodynamic properties along individual stochastic trajectories. It surveys recent methodological advancements, such as the integration of memory effects, hidden degrees of freedom, and geometric approaches like optimal transport. Furthermore, the authors discuss the challenges of scaling these principles to complex macroscopic systems and highlight emerging applications in biology, computation, and social sciences. The work advocates for an expanded, unorthodox framework for understanding the thermodynamics of diverse complex systems.

## Key Contributions

- Provides a comprehensive overview of the extension of stochastic thermodynamics beyond traditional physical systems.
- Synthesizes advancements in handling systems with memory, hidden degrees of freedom, and optimal transport formulations.
- Identifies critical challenges in bridging statistical irreversibility and thermodynamic dissipation in complex macroscopic systems.
- Explores the cross-disciplinary application of thermodynamic frameworks to non-physical fields like computation and social dynamics.
