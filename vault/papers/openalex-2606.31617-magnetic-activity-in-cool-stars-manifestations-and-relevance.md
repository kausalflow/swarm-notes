# Magnetic activity in cool stars: manifestations and relevance to exoplanets

**Authors**: E. Isik, A. Valio, A. Strugarek, S. Järvinen, K. Vida, A. Buccino, K. Namekata, T. Hackman, J. Alvarado-Gomez, D. Nandy, A. O. Farrish, K. Poppenhäger, P. Figueira
**Date**: 2026-06-30
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.31617](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31617)

## Summary

This paper reviews the manifestations of magnetic activity in cool stars, highlighting its critical role in defining planetary space environments and hindering exoplanet detection through quasi-periodic signals in time series data. It utilizes the 'Sun in Time' framework, which maps the magnetic evolution of solar-mass stars over Gyr timescales using a series of solar analogues. The authors further synthesize diagnostic tools and forward-modelling techniques necessary for interpreting stellar activity, offering a comprehensive resource for the exoplanet community.

## Key Contributions

- Reviews observational manifestations and MHD-based physical models of magnetic activity in cool stars within the context of exoplanet science.
- Synthesizes rotation-activity relationships across photospheric, chromospheric, and coronal layers for solar-like stars.
- Evaluates forward-modelling tools that bridge surface magnetic field distributions with observational signals relevant to exoplanet detection and characterization.

The paper provides a review of stellar magnetism and its impact on exoplanet science. No datasets were approved as none were presented as novel benchmarks or unique, reusable resources.

### Approved Concepts
- Sun in Time framework: Central to the paper's proposal of using a sequence of solar analogues to model the evolution of stellar magnetism over Gyr timescales.

### Approved Open Questions
- ZDI Field Topology Interpretability: ZDI is the standard observational tool for mapping large-scale stellar magnetism, but its output is used as input for physics-based models where errors propagate into environmental characterizations for exoplanets.
- Surface-to-Environment Mapping Uncertainty: This is the critical bottleneck in linking stellar activity to exoplanetary habitability and atmospheric evolution, as EUV/X-ray inputs are primary drivers of planetary atmospheric escape.
