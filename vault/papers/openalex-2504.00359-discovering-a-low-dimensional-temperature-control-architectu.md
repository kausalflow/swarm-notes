# Discovering a low-dimensional temperature control architecture across animals

**Authors**: Cody FitzGerald, Andrew J. Engedal, Niall M. Mangan
**Date**: 2026-05-19
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2504.00359](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.00359)

## Summary

This paper addresses the long-standing mystery of interbout arousals in hibernating mammals by using model selection and dynamical systems theory to identify underlying control structures from sparse, partially observed body temperature data. The authors propose a low-dimensional, environmentally sensitive core regulator that effectively characterizes body temperature modulation across a broad spectrum of species. Beyond thermophysiology, the study presents a general methodology for uncovering the mechanisms governing partially observed systems in diverse fields such as physics, chemistry, and biology.

## Key Contributions

- Developed a model selection approach to differentiate between competing physiological hypotheses for hibernation-induced body temperature oscillations.
- Discovered a low-dimensional core temperature regulator that qualitatively captures dynamics across diverse species including Arctic ground squirrels, birds, shrews, and bears.
- Proposed a generalizable modeling procedure for elucidating underlying mechanisms in partially observed time series data across scientific domains.
