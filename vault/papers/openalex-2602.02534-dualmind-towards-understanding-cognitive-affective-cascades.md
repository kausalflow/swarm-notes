# DualMind: Towards Understanding Cognitive-Affective Cascades in Public Opinion Dissemination via Multi-Agent Simulation

**Authors**: Enhao Huang, Tongtong Pan, Shuhuai Zhang, Qishu Jin, Liheng Zheng, Kaichun Hu, Yiming Li, Zhan Qin, Kui Ren
**Date**: 2026-05-28
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2602.02534](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.02534)

## Summary

DualMind is an LLM-driven multi-agent simulation framework designed to model the complex dynamics of public opinion during crises. By explicitly capturing the interplay between transient affective responses and long-term cognitive beliefs, the system enables more accurate reconstruction of opinion trajectories. Evaluated on 15 real-world PR crises post-August 2024, the framework demonstrates superior performance in forecasting public sentiment compared to existing baselines.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces DualMind, an LLM-driven multi-agent platform that models the interplay between transient affective responses and persistent cognitive beliefs during public relations crises.
- Demonstrates that DualMind reconstructs opinion trajectories more accurately than state-of-the-art baselines across 15 real-world crises.
- Provides a high-fidelity simulation tool for proactive crisis management and opinion forecasting.
