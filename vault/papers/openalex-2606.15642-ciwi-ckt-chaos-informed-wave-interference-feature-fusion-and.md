# CIWI-CKT: Chaos-Informed Wave Interference Feature Fusion and Cross-City Knowledge Transfer for Traffic Flow Forecasting

**Authors**: Abdul Joseph Fofanah, Lian Wen, David Chen, Shaoyang Zhang
**Date**: 2026-06-14
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.15642](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15642)

## Summary

The paper presents CIWI-CKT, a framework designed for few-shot traffic flow forecasting across cities where historical data is limited. By modeling traffic as chaotic wave components rather than deterministic signals, the method captures complex, non-linear interactions across urban networks. It utilizes meta-interference processing and chaos-aware meta-learning to facilitate knowledge transfer while maintaining the integrity of the underlying chaotic dynamics. Empirical results confirm that CIWI-CKT surpasses current state-of-the-art spatio-temporal and few-shot forecasting models.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces CIWI, a feature fusion framework that models traffic flow as adaptive wave components derived from chaos invariants to capture complex spatio-temporal dependencies.
- Develops meta-interference processing to analyze wave-like interactions between support and query traffic regimes, providing explicit confidence scores through predictability estimation.
- Proposes a chaos-aware meta-learning mechanism that enables efficient cross-city knowledge transfer while theoretically guaranteeing stability and generalization in data-scarce scenarios.
