# Dynamic Light Spanners in Doubling Metrics

**Authors**: Sujoy Bhore, Jonathan Conroy, Arnold Filtser
**Date**: 2026-03-24
**ArXiv**: [2603.23490](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23490)

## Summary

This paper presents a novel dynamic algorithm for maintaining a $(1+\varepsilon)$-spanner for a set of points undergoing insertions and deletions within a metric space of constant doubling dimension. The structure maintains the spanner's total weight within a constant factor of the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) weight, establishing a "light-weight" property. Crucially, the authors achieve an update time complexity bounded by $\operatorname{poly}(\log \Phi)$, where $\Phi$ is the aspect ratio of the point set. This breakthrough resolves the long-standing challenge of efficiently maintaining light-weight spanners dynamically, even in basic Euclidean settings.

## Key Contributions

- Developed the first efficient dynamic algorithm for maintaining a $(1+\varepsilon)$-spanner in a metric space with constant doubling dimension.
- Achieved polylogarithmic time complexity, $\operatorname{poly}(\log \Phi)$, for both point insertions and deletions, where $\Phi$ is the aspect ratio.
- The total weight of the maintained spanner is guaranteed to be within a constant factor of the weight of the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) of the point set.
- This solves a long-standing open problem, as no efficient dynamic algorithm was previously known even for low-dimensional Euclidean space.

## Limitations

The update time complexity depends on the aspect ratio $\Phi$, which can be large in general metric spaces. The algorithm's practical performance may degrade as $\Phi$ increases.
