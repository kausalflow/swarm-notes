# Dynamic Light Spanners in Doubling Metrics

## Summary

This paper addresses the dynamic spanner problem in metric spaces with a constant doubling dimension, where points undergo insertions and deletions. The authors present an algorithm that maintains a $(1+\varepsilon)$-spanner whose total weight is bounded by a constant multiple of the MST weight for the current point set. Crucially, each update operation (insertion or deletion) is achieved in $\operatorname{poly}(\log \Phi)$ time, where $\Phi$ is the aspect ratio of the point set. This result resolves a major open problem, as no efficient dynamic algorithm for lightweight spanners existed previously, even for Euclidean spaces.

## Key Contributions

- Developed the first efficient dynamic algorithm for maintaining a $(1+\varepsilon)$-spanner for a dynamic point set in a doubling metric space, with update times in $\operatorname{poly}(\log \Phi)$.
- Ensured the total weight of the maintained spanner remains within a constant factor of the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) weight.
- Solved the open problem of maintaining a lightweight spanner efficiently under insertions and deletions, even for low-dimensional Euclidean spaces.

## Limitations

The update time depends polynomially on $\Phi$, the aspect ratio of the point set. Further work might explore tighter bounds or independence from the aspect ratio.
