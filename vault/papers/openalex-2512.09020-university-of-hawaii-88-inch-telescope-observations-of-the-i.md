# University of Hawaii 88-inch Telescope Observations of the Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS: Spectrophotometric Blue-Sensitive Spectral Time Series Spanning Two Months from Discovery

**Authors**: W. B. Hoogendam, D. Kuesters, B. J. Shappee, G. Aldering, J. J. Wray, Bohong Yang, K. J. Meech, M. A. Tucker, M. E. Huber, Katie Auchettl, C. R. Angus, D. Desai, J. T. Hinkle, J. **Date**: 2026-07-15
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2512.09020](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09020)

## Summary

This paper presents a two-month spectral time series of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS obtained using the SuperNova Integral Field Spectrograph. The observations track the object's pre-perihelion approach, providing the earliest blue-sensitive spectrum (λ ≤ 3800 Å) and confirming cometary activity through Ni and CN emission. The authors analyze spectral slopes and synthetic photometry, finding relatively stable color evolution despite the object's distance variations.

## Key Contributions

- Presents the earliest blue-sensitive spectrophotometric spectral time series of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS starting 12.5 hours post-discovery.
- Characterizes cometary activity through Ni and CN emission detection across a two-month observation period.
- Reports stable color evolution and wavelength-varying spectral slopes for 3I/ATLAS during its pre-perihelion approach (4.4 to 2.5 au).
