# Role of SKA in Advancing Remote Measurements of Magnetic Fields of Solar Coronal Mass Ejections

**Authors**: Devojyoti Kansabanik, Surajit Mondal, Shaheda Begum Shahik, P Zhang, Puja Majee, Angelos Vourlidas, John Morgan, Divya Oberoi, A. Charan Kumari, Alec Thomson, Bin Chen, Carl Shneider, Hariharan Krishnan, Kamen Kozarev, Sneha Pandit, Vanessa Moss
**Date**: 2026-06-30
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.31440](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31440)

## Summary

This paper examines the potential of the Square Kilometre Array (SKA) to advance remote-sensing measurements of magnetic fields in Coronal Mass Ejections (CMEs). While traditional visible-light coronagraphs are limited to geometric and kinematic data, radio-frequency observations offer a viable method for directly diagnosing magnetic field topology. The authors outline how SKA's technical specifications will enhance data quality for space weather models while noting that specialized calibration and imaging techniques remain critical for future operations.

## Key Contributions

- Analyzes the role of Square Kilometre Array (SKA) observations in remote-sensing of coronal mass ejection (CME) vector magnetic fields.
- Identifies specific advantages of SKA, including high sensitivity and broad frequency coverage, for constraining space weather prediction models.
- Highlights technical challenges and necessary developmental strategies in scheduling, calibration, and imaging required for non-standard solar radio observations.
