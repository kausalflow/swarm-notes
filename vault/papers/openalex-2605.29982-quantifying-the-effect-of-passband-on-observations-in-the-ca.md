## Summary

This paper addresses the challenge of cross-calibrating century-long solar observations in the Ca II K line, which are often incompatible due to varying spectral passbands and spatial resolutions. By using high-resolution data from the Sunrise III mission to emulate different observational parameters, the authors derive empirical power-law relationships between contrasts observed at different resolutions. These relationships significantly improve the consistency of historical solar activity records, providing a robust methodology for merging multi-observatory data.

## Key Contributions

- Established empirical power-law relationships between solar contrast measurements across spectral passbands (0.1–9 Å) and spatial resolutions (1–6 arcsec).
- Utilized high-resolution data from the Sunrise III mission to emulate diverse instrument characteristics for cross-calibration.
- Demonstrated the efficacy of the developed relationships by improving cross-calibration between existing solar Ca II K archives.
