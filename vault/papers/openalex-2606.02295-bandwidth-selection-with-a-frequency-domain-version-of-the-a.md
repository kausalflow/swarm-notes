# Bandwidth selection with a frequency-domain version of the AIC

**Authors**: Erhard Reschenhofer
**Date**: 2026-06-01
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.02295](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02295)

## Summary

This paper addresses the lack of a standardized, automatic method for nonparametric spectral density estimation in time series analysis, which often relies on subjective visual bandwidth selection. The author proposes a frequency-domain version of the Akaike Information Criterion (AIC) to objectively determine smoothing parameters. Through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, the study shows that this simple frequency-domain AIC provides performance comparable to established parametric autoregressive order selection methods.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a frequency-domain version of the Akaike Information Criterion (AIC) for automatic bandwidth selection in nonparametric spectral density estimation.
- Demonstrates that the proposed frequency-domain AIC achieves estimation performance comparable to standard parametric AR-based approaches across varying spectral complexity.
- Provides an objective alternative to subjective visual smoothing parameter selection for time series spectral analysis.
