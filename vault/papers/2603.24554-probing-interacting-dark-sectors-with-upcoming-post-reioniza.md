# Probing Interacting Dark Sectors with upcoming Post-Reionization and Galaxy Surveys

**Authors**: Rahul Shah, Antara Dey, Purba Mukherjee, Supratik Pal
**Date**: 2026-03-25
**ArXiv**: [2603.24554](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24554)

## Summary

This paper forecasts the improvement in parameter constraints for interacting dark sector models, specifically involving dynamical dark energy and dark matter, by incorporating simulated data from upcoming astrophysical surveys. The methodology involves generating mock observations from SKA-mid (21-cm intensity mapping, galaxy clustering) and Euclid (cosmic shear), and then performing Markov Chain Monte Carlo analyses to constrain cosmological parameters beyond the standard six. The primary finding is that both SKA and Euclid observations offer significant improvements over current combined constraints (Planck, DESI, Pantheon+), with the full SKA2 configuration projected to yield the tightest constraints on the interaction strength. The work underscores the crucial role of next-generation large-scale structure probes in testing fundamental dark sector physics.

## Key Contributions

- Forecasted the constraining power of future SKA-mid and Euclid galaxy surveys on interacting dark sector models (dynamical dark energy and dark matter).
- Generated mock datasets for 21-cm intensity mapping, galaxy clustering, and cosmic shear from these upcoming surveys, incorporating standard and interacting dark sector parameters.
- Demonstrated that combining current data with mock SKA-mid and Euclid data significantly tightens constraints on the interaction strength ($\omega$) and dark energy equation of state ($w$).
- Determined that the SKA2 configuration provides the tightest projected constraints, especially on the interaction strength, surpassing the precision of SKA1 and Euclid.

## Limitations

The analysis relies on the assumption of specific interacting dark sector models (dynamical dark energy and dark matter interaction) and the fidelity of the generated mock datasets based on current fiducial values. It does not explore the full parameter space of potential dark sector interactions.
