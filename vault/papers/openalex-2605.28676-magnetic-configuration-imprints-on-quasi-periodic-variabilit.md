# Magnetic Configuration Imprints on Quasi-Periodic Variability in GRMHD Simulations of Thin Accretion Disks

**Authors**: Jing-Ze Xia, Hong-Xuan Jiang, Indu K. Dihingia, Yosuke Mizuno
**Date**: 2026-05-27
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.28676](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28676)

## Summary

This paper investigates the role of magnetic field configurations in the generation of quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) in thin accretion disks using global GRMHD simulations. By initializing disks with various multi-loop magnetic topologies, the study shows that these configurations induce local truncations and resonant cavities that sustain QPO-like variability via viscous-epicyclic overstability. The findings suggest that magnetic structure is a critical factor in explaining observed X-ray binary variability, while also highlighting that increased disk thickness and turbulent diffusion can suppress these oscillation signatures.

## Key Contributions

- Identifies that multi-loop magnetic field configurations in GRMHD simulations naturally generate puffed-up inner regions in thin accretion disks.
- Establishes that QPO-like variability frequencies in accretion disks correspond to the local radial epicyclic frequency and its harmonics, driven by viscous-epicyclic overstability.
- Demonstrates that magnetic topology regulates the truncation radius and the position of resonant cavities, with turbulent diffusion suppressing these signals as disk thickness increases.
