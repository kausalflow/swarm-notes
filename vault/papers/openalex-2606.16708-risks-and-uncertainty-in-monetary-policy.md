# Risks and Uncertainty in Monetary Policy

**Authors**: Tobias Adrian, Domenico Giannone, Matteo Luciani, Mike West
**Date**: 2026-06-15
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.16708](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16708)

## Summary

This paper addresses the long-standing disconnect between narrative-based scenario analysis and probabilistic macroeconomic forecasting. The authors propose a unified framework that treats both baseline forecasts and scenarios as conditional predictive densities relative to a reference distribution. By introducing the Scenario Synthesis technique, the framework provides a reproducible way to assign probabilities to scenarios, thereby enhancing policy deliberation and risk assessment under significant uncertainty.

## Key Contributions

- Unifies scenario analysis and distributional forecasting within a single coherent framework based on conditional predictive densities.
- Introduces Scenario Synthesis to assign consistent weights to qualitative scenarios based on reference predictive distributions.
- Provides a reproducible methodology for macroeconomic risk assessment and policy deliberation under conditions of deep uncertainty.
