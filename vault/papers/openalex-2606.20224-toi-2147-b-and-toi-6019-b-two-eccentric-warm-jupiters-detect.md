## Summary

This paper presents the detection and characterization of two eccentric warm Jupiters, TOI-2147 b and TOI-6019 b, initially identified by the TESS mission. Through a combination of multiband ground-based photometry and high-precision radial velocity measurements from the Manfred Hirt Planet Finder Spectrograph (MaHPS), the planetary nature and orbital parameters of these objects were confirmed. Analysis reveals that both planets possess eccentric orbits and bulk densities lower than that of Jupiter, with interior modeling suggesting that tidal heating contributes to their observed radius inflation. The findings support a high-eccentricity migration pathway for the evolution of these planetary systems.

## Key Contributions

- Characterization of two new eccentric warm Jupiters, TOI-2147 b and TOI-6019 b, using TESS and high-precision MaHPS radial velocity data.
- Determination that both planets exhibit inflated radii and non-zero orbital eccentricities, supporting a high-eccentricity migration origin.
- Interior structure modeling via GASTLI suggesting tidal heating as a primary contributor to planetary radius inflation.
