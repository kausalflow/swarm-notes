# Axion-Like Particle Dark Matter Intensity Mapping: A New Probe via Cross-Correlation with Galaxy Surveys

**Authors**: Wen-Qing Guo
**Date**: 2026-05-07
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.05906](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05906)

## Summary

This paper introduces a novel approach for detecting axion-like particle (ALP) dark matter by cross-correlating radio intensity mapping data with large-scale galaxy survey distributions. The framework specifically models the stimulated radiative decay of ALPs in the presence of Cosmic Microwave Background and extragalactic radio background fields. The study provides sensitivity forecasts for the upcoming Square Kilometre Array (SKA) Phase 2, confirming that this technique offers a powerful complementary probe for dark matter in the local universe.

## Key Contributions

- Develops a theoretical framework for ALP dark matter detection using stimulated decay effects from CMB and ERB fields.
- Demonstrates that cross-correlating radio intensity mapping with galaxy surveys provides a viable detection probe for micro-eV scale dark matter.
- Forecasts the detection sensitivity for the SKA Phase 2 radio telescope array.
