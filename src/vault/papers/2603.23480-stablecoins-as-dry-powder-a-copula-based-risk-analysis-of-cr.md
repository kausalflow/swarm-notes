# Stablecoins as Dry Powder: A Copula-Based Risk Analysis of Cryptocurrency Markets

## Summary

This study investigates the systemic role of stablecoins in the cryptocurrency ecosystem by quantifying the transmission of risk and activity from stablecoin markets to broader digital asset markets using copula-based methods. The authors establish in-sample causality across multiple forecasting horizons and show that stablecoin volume and upside volatility act as critical leading indicators, effectively functioning as 'dry powder' for market movements. Incorporating these stablecoin factors significantly improves forecasting accuracy, as evidenced by reduced Mean Squared Error in cryptocurrency predictions. Furthermore, the practical economic value is demonstrated by showing that using these factors in a volatility targeting strategy leads to overall risk reduction.

## Key Contributions

- Quantified the transmission of volatility and activity from stablecoin markets to broader cryptocurrency markets using copula-based analysis across daily, weekly, and monthly horizons.
- Demonstrated that incorporating stablecoin factors significantly reduces Mean Squared Error in cryptocurrency forecasting models.
- Established that stablecoin volume and upside volatility serve as significant predictors (dry powder) for broader cryptocurrency market volatility.
- Showed that employing stablecoin factors leads to reduced risk when applied to a cryptocurrency volatility targeting portfolio strategy.

## Key Concepts

- [Stablecoin Volatility Targeting Model](../concepts/stablecoin-volatility-targeting-model.md): A risk management model for cryptocurrency markets that uses stablecoin factors to target a specific level of volatility.

## Limitations

The study focuses on causality and risk transmission, but a deeper exploration of the *mechanisms* through which stablecoin volume translates into market activity (e.g., arbitrage vs. new capital inflow) could be further investigated.
