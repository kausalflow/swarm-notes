# Statistical Efficiency of Single- and Multi-step Models for Forecasting and Control

## Summary

This paper quantitatively analyzes the trade-off between using single-step and multi-step predictors for forecasting and subsequent closed-loop control, particularly in the context of compounding error in learning-based control. The authors investigate three predictor classes applied to linear dynamical systems: single-step, direct multi-step, and single-step models trained with multi-step losses. They theoretically establish that if the system dynamics are perfectly specified, single-step models yield the lowest asymptotic prediction error. However, in the presence of model misspecification due to partial observability, direct multi-step predictors offer significant advantages in reducing bias and improving overall accuracy, a benefit that extends to practical control scenarios.

## Key Contributions

- Derived the first quantitative analysis comparing the asymptotic prediction error of single-step, multi-step, and single-step models trained with multi-step losses for linear dynamical systems.
- Demonstrated that when the model class is well-specified, single-step models achieve the lowest asymptotic prediction error.
- Showed that when the model class is misspecified due to partial observability, direct multi-step predictors significantly reduce bias and improve accuracy.
- Provided theoretical and empirical evidence that these trade-offs between model complexity and prediction accuracy persist when models are deployed in closed-loop control.

## Limitations

The analysis is strictly focused on linear dynamical systems.
