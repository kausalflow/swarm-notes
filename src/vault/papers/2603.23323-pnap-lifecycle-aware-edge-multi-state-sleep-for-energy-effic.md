# PNap: Lifecycle-aware Edge Multi-state sleep for Energy Efficient MEC

## Summary

This paper addresses the high energy consumption in Multi-access Edge Computing (MEC) systems caused by keeping multiple servers active during low-load periods. The authors introduce PowerNap (PNap), a novel orchestration framework designed to jointly manage server multi-state sleep mechanisms and service lifecycle operations to ensure service continuity. PNap utilizes traffic forecasting to intelligently decide when to transition servers into deeper sleep states, thereby minimizing the number of active servers while avoiding service disruptions from slow wake-up times. Experimental results demonstrate that PNap achieves up to 14.9% energy savings compared to a state-of-the-art approach while achieving equivalent service availability metrics.

## Key Contributions

- Introduction of PowerNap (PNap), a lifecycle-aware orchestration framework that jointly manages MEC server sleep states and service lifecycle states.
- Demonstration of PNap's ability to minimize the number of active edge servers and service disruptions by leveraging traffic forecasting.
- Validation showing PNap reduces energy consumption by up to 14.9% compared to a state-of-the-art solution while maintaining service availability.
