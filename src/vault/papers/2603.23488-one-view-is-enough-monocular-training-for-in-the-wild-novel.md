# One View Is Enough! Monocular Training for In-the-Wild Novel View Generation

## Summary

The paper introduces OVIE, a novel approach to monocular novel view synthesis that eliminates the need for multi-view supervision by training exclusively on large-scale, unpaired internet images. The core idea involves using a monocular depth estimator to scaffold the training process by lifting the source view into 3D, applying a transformation, and projecting a pseudo-target view. To manage noise from the unstructured data, a masked training formulation restricts various loss functions to valid projection regions, allowing scaling to 30 million images. Crucially, the resulting OVIE model is geometry-free at inference, achieving superior zero-shot performance while being significantly faster than previous methods.

## Key Contributions

- Development of OVIE, a framework for monocular novel view synthesis trained entirely on unpaired, in-the-wild internet images.
- Introduction of a training methodology leveraging a monocular depth estimator to create pseudo-target views via 3D lifting and camera transformation, which is discarded at inference.
- Proposal of a masked training formulation to focus geometric, perceptual, and textural losses on valid regions, enabling scaling to 30 million uncurated images.
- Achieving state-of-the-art zero-shot performance compared to prior methods while being 600x faster at inference time due to the geometry-free nature of the final model.
