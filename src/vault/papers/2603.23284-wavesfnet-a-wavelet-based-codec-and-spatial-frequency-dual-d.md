# WaveSFNet: A Wavelet-Based Codec and Spatial--Frequency Dual-Domain Gating Network for Spatiotemporal Prediction

## Summary

WaveSFNet is an efficient recurrent-free framework proposed for spatiotemporal prediction, addressing the challenge of preserving high-frequency details during downsampling. It integrates a novel wavelet-based codec to retain high-frequency subband cues, which is coupled with a spatial--frequency dual-domain gated translator. This translator enhances dynamics by incorporating adjacent-frame differences and fuses large-kernel spatial modeling with frequency-domain global modulation. Experiments on datasets like Moving MNIST, TaxiBJ, and WeatherBench demonstrate WaveSFNet achieves competitive accuracy while maintaining low computational overhead.

## Key Contributions

- Introduction of a wavelet-based codec to preserve crucial high-frequency details lost during downsampling in recurrent-free spatiotemporal prediction models.
- Design of a spatial--frequency dual-domain gated spatiotemporal translator that fuses large-kernel spatial modeling with frequency-domain global modulation.
- Explicit enhancement of dynamic information by injecting adjacent-frame differences into the spatiotemporal translator.
- Achieving competitive prediction accuracy on Moving MNIST, TaxiBJ, and WeatherBench benchmarks while maintaining low computational complexity.

## Key Concepts

- [Wavelet-Based Codec](../concepts/wavelet-based-codec.md): A codec component that utilizes wavelet transforms to preserve high-frequency subband cues during the downsampling and reconstruction phases of spatiotemporal feature extraction.
- [Spatial-Frequency Dual-Domain Gating Network](../concepts/dual-domain-gated-network.md): A spatiotemporal translator that performs gated fusion between large-kernel spatial local modeling and frequency-domain global modulation.
