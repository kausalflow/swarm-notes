# Foveated Diffusion: Efficient Spatially Adaptive Image and Video Generation

## Summary

This work addresses the quadratic complexity bottleneck in high-resolution image and video diffusion models by introducing Foveated Diffusion, which exploits the non-uniform acuity of human vision based on known gaze location. The method assigns a higher density of tokens to the foveal region where detail is critical and a lower density to the periphery. A novel mechanism is proposed for constructing these mixed-resolution tokens directly from high-resolution inputs, allowing existing diffusion models to be efficiently post-trained. This results in generation speeds and token counts vastly reduced compared to full-resolution generation while maintaining perceptual quality, validated by both technical metrics and human studies.

## Key Contributions

- Introduction of a non-uniform token allocation scheme based on human visual eccentricity to drastically reduce the token count in image and video generation.
- Development of a mechanism to construct mixed-resolution tokens from high-resolution data, enabling post-training of existing diffusion models for foveated generation.
- Demonstration that foveated generation yields results perceptually indistinguishable from full-resolution generation while significantly improving efficiency.
- Validation of the approach through extensive analysis and a user study confirming the practical scalability of foveation for efficient content generation.

## Key Concepts

- [Foveated Diffusion](../concepts/foveated-diffusion.md): An efficient generative modeling approach that leverages known or estimated gaze location to assign higher token density to the foveal region and lower density to the periphery, mimicking human vision.

## Limitations

The approach relies on the availability or accurate estimation of the user's gaze location.
